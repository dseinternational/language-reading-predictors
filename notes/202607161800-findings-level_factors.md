## What these models ask

The companion "levels" view of the [gain_factors](202607161800-findings-gain_factors.md) family: instead of modelling each period's _gain_, it models the **score itself at each of the four timepoints**, with a group×time contrast vector (`b_grp_time`) and a block-design ability×time vector (`gamma_ability_time`), fit as a Beta-Binomial on the bounded post-score count via a logit linear predictor. There are eleven models, one per outcome — word reading (W), the two standardised vocabulary measures receptive (R) and expressive (E), letter sounds (L), phonetic spelling (P), phoneme blending (B), CELF basic concepts (F), TROG receptive grammar (T), the two taught-vocabulary measures receptive (TR) and expressive (TE), and nonword reading (N). Only the **t2 group contrast** (`b_grp_time[1]`) is a **clean randomised effect** — at t2 the immediate arm has been treated and the waiting-list arm has not, so the two groups differ only by the toss of the randomisation coin. The t1 contrast (`b_grp_time[0]`) is a baseline-balance check, and the t3/t4 contrasts (`b_grp_time[2]`, `[3]`) are measured **after the waiting-list children have crossed over**, so those group differences are associations, not randomised effects. Every ability×time, age, early-reading and interaction term is an **adjusted association**, never a causal effect (see [What is causal](#what-is-causal)).

A note on Bayesian vocabulary for the frequentist-trained reader: a **89% credible range** is the interval that contains the true parameter with 89% probability _given the data and priors_ — a direct probability statement about the parameter itself, unlike a confidence interval, which is a statement about the procedure. **P(effect > 0)** (equivalently the "direction probability") is the posterior probability that the true effect has the stated sign. **ROPE** is a "region of practical equivalence" — a band around zero deemed too small to matter (here ±1 item for most outcomes, ±2 items for the standardised-vocabulary measures R/E and letter sounds L, and ±0.1 = ±10 percentage points for the off-floor binary outcomes P and N); the ROPE verdict asks whether the effect is big enough to matter, separately from whether it is non-zero. **Positive `tau` / positive contrast = the intervention helps.**

## Convergence gate

**All 11 passed.** Every model recorded 0 divergences and a maximum R-hat between 1.0005 (lf-006, B) and 1.0025 (lf-003, E), comfortably under the 1.01 gate; ESS and BFMI clear their thresholds throughout. No model is flagged.

## Results — all models (randomised t2 group contrast)

Two of the eleven outcomes (F and T) did not generate an items-scale conversion in this fit (their `rope_summary.csv` is absent), so their causal contrast is reported on the **logit** scale instead — the contrast still exists and is interpretable, it simply was not translated into items. Direction is the probability the true effect lies in its favoured direction (with the favoured direction named), and the evidence label grades that probability on the project ladder (inconclusive < 0.75 ≤ suggestive < 0.91 ≤ moderate < 0.97 ≤ strong < 0.99 ≤ very strong). Reading notes for a lay audience. The "direction (probability)" column reports the probability in the _favoured_ direction — for most outcomes that is positive (intervention helps), but for the two standardised vocabulary measures the posterior leans negative, so their probabilities are P(effect < 0). A "negative" lean here is _directional evidence about the levels view_, not evidence the intervention harms vocabulary — the sharper [ITT](202607161800-findings-itt.md) and [gain_factors](202607161800-findings-gain_factors.md) analyses read these same vocabulary effects as inconclusive and probably negligible, and a levels model is the least reliable place to read a small vocabulary effect. The two off-floor rows (P and N) are on a **different scale** from the items rows: they report a Bernoulli off-the-floor _risk difference_ in percentage points — the change in the probability of scoring above the floor — so their "+0.5 pp" / "−0.9 pp" are not comparable to the "+2.5 items" magnitudes elsewhere, and both sit almost entirely inside the ROPE (practically indistinguishable from zero).

## Adjusted associations across the family

These are the covariate couplings the models estimate alongside the causal t2 contrast. **Every one is a latent-ability-confounded adjusted association** — read as "children who score higher on X tend to score higher on the outcome, holding the model's other terms fixed", never as "X drives the outcome".

The **dominant and most consistent signal in the whole family is the block-design ability×time coupling** (`gamma_ability_time`): higher general ability goes with higher scores at each wave, and for the standardised-vocabulary and language-comprehension outcomes it is one of the largest effects in the model.

## The one-paragraph story

A **levels** model is a blunter instrument than a gain model — it does not use each child as their own baseline, so its confidence intervals are wider and its t2 signal is weaker than the [ITT](202607161800-findings-itt.md) or [gain_factors](202607161800-findings-gain_factors.md) views. Even so, **letter sounds** shows moderate evidence of a positive randomised t2 effect (+2.5 items, 96% positive) — the strongest randomised signal in the family and consistent with the sharper analyses — and **word reading** leans positive (+1.4 items, 81%), positive-leaning but not conclusive at this coarser view. The negative-leaning receptive/expressive standardised-vocabulary levels at t2 are noisy and should be read alongside the ITT verdict that those effects are **inconclusive and probably negligible**. Across every outcome the ROPE verdict is inconclusive: no t2 contrast is confidently "big enough to matter" at the levels view, and the two off-floor outcomes (P, N) sit almost entirely inside their practical-equivalence band.

## What is causal

**Only the t2 contrast.** Everything at t3/t4 is post-crossover association; the t1 contrast is a baseline-balance quantity; and every ability×time, early-reading, age, speech, hearing and group×ability term is an adjusted association, latent-ability-confounded and never to be read as "X drives Y". Unlike the gain models, this family carries **no** other-skill adjusters on purpose: conditioning a levels model on another skill's contemporaneous level would condition on a post-treatment mediator and corrupt the group×time reading (the reasoning recorded in #247).
