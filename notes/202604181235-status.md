## Registered variants

- **Log-transformed** (`lrp01_log`, `lrp02_log`,
  `lrp02_log_select`): for right-skewed targets with floor at 0.
- **Quantile α=0.5** (`lrp02_quantile`, `lrp04_quantile`,
  `lrp02_log_quantile`): MedAE-optimising objective.
- **Construct-driven Select02** (`lrp02_drop_age_measures`,
  `lrp04` as current primary): research-question framed cuts that
  trade accuracy for interpretability.
- **Prediction variants** (`lrp01_prediction`, `lrp02_prediction`,
  `lrp02_log_prediction`): RMSE-tuned for forecasting rather than
  explanation.
- **Exploratory** (`lrp01_exp_eowpvt`, `lrp01_exp_gender`): single
  variable additions to the LRP01 minimal set.

## Cross-model patterns observed during the Select01 programme

1. **MAE-tune wins CV MAE/R²; carry-forward wins CV MedAE**
   (LRP05, LRP07, LRP08). The MAE objective trades median
   accuracy for mean. Makes α=0.5 quantile a natural future
   variant for each.
2. **Level vs gain stories diverge**: top predictors of level
   (`aptinfo`, `eowpvt`, `ewrswr`) often sit at zero importance
   on the corresponding gain task — most extreme in LRP07.
3. **Target pathology shapes tuning regime**:
   - Ceilinged target (LRP06 `yarclet`) → many-slow-trees
     (277 trees, lr 0.016).
   - Hard-to-predict gain (LRP07 `rowpvt_gain`) →
     few-fast-shallow-wide (31–42 trees, lr 0.14–0.18).
   - Near-Gaussian target (LRP08 `rowpvt`) → tune-insensitive;
     baseline and tuned performance essentially tied.
   - Mild-skew gains (LRP05 `yarclet_gain`) → fewer-faster-wider
     regimes.
4. **Select01 magnitude varies with noise-floor depth**. LRP06
   and LRP07 both had very deep noise floors (9–11 features at
   exactly 0.000) supporting aggressive 32→10 / 34→12 cuts.
   LRP08's flatter importance distribution supported only 32→17.
