## Motivation

LRP05 predicts letter-sound knowledge **gains** (`yarclet_gain`) —
paralleling LRP01 (word-reading gains) and LRP03 (expressive-vocabulary
gains). Baseline started from the full `Predictors.DEFAULT_GAIN` set
plus the auto-included `yarclet` base variable (34 predictors total),
n=160, with no outlier exclusion.

Following the established playbook, MAE hyperparameter tuning on
the full predictor set precedes any feature-selection work.

### Notable observations

- **`yarclet` base level is the #1 predictor** at importance 0.328
  — but at baseline it was 0.871. The tuning redistributed signal
  massively away from the dominant base-level feature.
- **`time` at rank 2 (0.059)** — time-since-baseline is a strong
  predictor of gain. Makes sense: larger gains accumulate over
  longer intervals.
- **`attend` (intervention attendance) at rank 4 (0.036)** —
  mirrors LRP01, where attend was a top-3 predictor. Consistent
  with intervention effect.
- **`deappfi` (articulation error)** at rank 5 (0.032) — articulation
  quality matters for letter-sound learning. Similar construct
  picture to LRP03 where articulation features led.
- **Reading measures are at the bottom**: `ewrswr` (0.003),
  `yarcsi` (0.003), `nonword` (0.001). Letter-sound gains are
  **not** much driven by other reading measures — the base
  `yarclet` + time + age + attend are the dominant story.
- **3 features at exactly 0.000** (`area`, `gender`, `behav`).
  Another ~10 below 0.005.
