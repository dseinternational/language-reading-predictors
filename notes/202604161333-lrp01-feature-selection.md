## Decision: tune before selecting

Default LightGBM params (`n_estimators=1200`) massively over-trained at n=152,
producing CV R² = −0.03 (worse than predicting the mean). Importance rankings
from a model that doesn't generalise are unreliable for feature selection.

The Optuna-tuned params (50 trials, 10-split GroupKFold, seed 47, best trial #14)
give CV RMSE 3.31 ± 0.54 with `n_estimators=83` — a model that actually learns.
These tuned params are now the base `LRP01` params (no longer a separate variant).

## Final model structure: exploratory vs prediction

### Decision: keep outliers in for the primary model

This is an exploratory research study. The goal is to identify which factors
most influence reading gains, not to build a deployment prediction system. The
children with gains >= 15 — the ones who made the most progress — are arguably
the most informative observations. Excluding them biases the importance rankings
toward predictors of moderate gains and prevents the model from learning what
drives large improvements.

The outlier threshold was originally set to improve prediction accuracy (CV RMSE
dropped from 3.94 to 3.21 with it). But for exploratory purposes, prediction
accuracy on the typical case is secondary to getting honest importance rankings
across the full range of outcomes.

MAE-tuned hyperparameters with LightGBM objective=mae are preferred because:
- MAE treats all errors linearly, so 5 extreme-gain observations do not
  dominate the loss function the way they do with squared error
- The MAE-tuned model uses only 29 estimators with high min_child_samples (40),
  making it a conservative ensemble that does not chase outlier residuals
- Importance rankings from MAE-tuned models reflect predictor relevance across
  the full outcome distribution, not just for typical cases

### Model structure

Two models share the same 15-predictor set:

- `lrp01` (primary) -- MAE-tuned, no outlier exclusion, n=157. For
  identifying important predictors of reading gains.
- `lrp01_prediction` (variant) -- RMSE-tuned, outliers >= 15 excluded,
  n=152. For best prediction accuracy on typical cases.
