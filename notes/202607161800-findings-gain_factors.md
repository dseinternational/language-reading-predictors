## What these models ask

These models look at each child's **post-period score given their pre-period score** (an ANCOVA — analysis of covariance) and stack every study period together, with a child random intercept (a per-child offset that partially pools stable differences between children — a shrunken stand-in for between-child heterogeneity, **not** a control for latent general ability). The one **causal** number is the **on-intervention effect during the randomised first period** (period 1, when the immediate arm is treated and the waiting-list arm is still an untreated control). It is reported as an **items-scale risk difference** (how many more items, out of the test's total, a treated child gets right at post than they otherwise would have), and it is a genuinely independent second look at the [ITT](202607161800-findings-itt.md) effect using a different model structure. Every other coefficient (own baseline, age, ability, upstream skills, the treatment-by-covariate interactions) is an **adjusted association** describing _who_ progresses — a latent-ability-confounded correlation, not a lever you could pull.

There are 11 primary models (`gf-001–011`, one per outcome) and 8 "treated-only" companions (`gf-101–108`) that look only at children while on the programme and therefore estimate **no** treatment effect — they are association-only checks that let us see whether the same predictor structure holds when the treatment contrast is removed.

Two outcomes — **phonetic spelling (P, gf-005)** and **nonword reading (N, gf-011)** — sit on the floor of their tests for most children (almost everyone scores zero), so they use a different, floor-aware ("off-floor") model: instead of an items count it models the probability that a child has come **off the floor** (scored above zero) at post, and its treatment effect is reported as a **risk difference in percentage points (pp)** — the change in that probability — rather than on the items scale used by the other nine outcomes. The treated-only companion for P (`gf-105`) is off-floor too and carries no own-baseline term.

## How to read the Bayesian quantities

For readers more used to frequentist output, three plain-English translations:

- A **95% credible range** is the range within which the parameter lies with 95% probability **given the data and priors** — a direct probability statement about the parameter itself, unlike a confidence interval (which is a statement about the procedure).
- **P(favoured direction)** is the posterior probability that the true effect has the sign shown (positive = benefit, or negative where the point estimate is negative) — read straight off the posterior, no p-value involved.
- **ROPE** = "region of practical equivalence": a band around zero deemed too small to matter in practice. `prob_in_rope` is the posterior probability the effect falls inside that band — high in-ROPE mass means "most likely a practically negligible effect", which is often more informative than the raw direction probability.

Evidence labels grade the **direction** probability on the project ladder (#179): inconclusive < 0.75 ≤ suggestive < 0.91 ≤ moderate < 0.97 ≤ strong < 0.99 ≤ very strong. Positive τ = intervention benefit.

## The one-paragraph story

This model, built quite differently from the ITT model, **reproduces the ITT headline**: **very strong** evidence of benefit on **letter sounds** (+3.3 items) and **word reading** (+2.6 items), and the same fade-out for broad standardised vocabulary (R suggestive-negative but half inside the ROPE; E inconclusive). Getting the same answer from a period-stacked ANCOVA as from the simple randomised comparison is the kind of triangulation that makes the core reading finding credible. The remaining nine outcomes are, at most, suggestive of benefit and mostly inconclusive.

## Adjusted associations across the family — what they say about _who_ progresses

None of these is causal; all are latent-ability-confounded correlations, so read them as "children who look like X tend to progress like Y", never "X drives Y".

- **Own baseline is the near-deterministic engine.** For all nine continuous outcomes the child's own pre-period score is the most strongly resolved association, **gamma_own 0.42–0.78 with P(>0)>0.999** ("higher start → higher finish"). This is expected regression-to-the-outcome structure, not a treatment effect. The qualifier applies only to the nine continuous outcomes: the two off-floor models (P gf-005, N gf-011, and treated-only P gf-105) carry **no** own-baseline term at all.
- **Age is a consistent modest negative.** gamma_A runs roughly −0.08 to −0.14 across the family, several times at very-strong evidence in the negative direction (W, T, and the treated-only W, T) — within a timepoint, older children add slightly fewer items.
- **Non-verbal ability tracks the more cognitively-loaded outcomes.** Ability is a strong-to-very-strong positive association for grammar/concepts (F gf-007 +0.469 very strong; T gf-008 couplings) but near-zero for word reading (gf-001). The treated-only companions show the same pattern (F, T, E, R all positive).
- **Upstream-skill couplings are as the DAG expects.** Letter sounds couple strongly into phonetic spelling and nonword reading (**gamma_L +0.53–0.56 in gf-005/gf-011, +0.63 in treated-only gf-105**); receptive and expressive vocabulary cross-couple; taught-vocabulary terms (gamma_TR, gamma_TE) couple into their untaught and grammar counterparts. Phoneme blending couples into phonetic spelling (gamma_B +0.43 in gf-005).
- **The salient interaction signal is treatment-by-own-baseline, not a threshold.** No nonlinearity/knee is modelled anywhere in this family. Instead there is a recurring **negative treatment-by-own-baseline interaction** (gamma_int_trt_own moderate-to-strong negative in gf-002, gf-003, gf-006, gf-008, gf-009, gf-010) — children starting higher show a smaller on-intervention increment — with the notable exception of **phonetic spelling (gf-005), where it flips strongly positive (+0.80, very strong)**. Treatment-by-ability interactions vary in sign (F gf-007 strongly negative −0.304; T gf-008 strongly positive +0.219). A **consistently positive age-by-ability interaction** appears at very-strong evidence in gf-007, gf-008 and the treated-only gf-107, gf-108. All of these are adjusted associations, not causal moderation.

## What is causal

Only the **period-1 on-intervention marginal** (`beta_trt` / treatment_marginal) is causal — it rests on the randomised first period, when the immediate arm is treated and the waiting-list arm is still an untreated control. Own-baseline, age, ability, upstream-skill and all interaction coefficients are adjusted associations. Consistently, across the nine continuous outcomes, the child's **own starting score** is the most strongly resolved association (≈99.9% for "higher start → higher finish"), which is expected and not a treatment effect; the two off-floor outcomes (P, N) have no own-baseline term. The `gf-101–108` treated-only companions carry no treatment contrast and estimate **no** effect at all — they exist purely as association checks, and they confirm the same own-baseline / ability / upstream-skill structure holds without the randomised comparison.
