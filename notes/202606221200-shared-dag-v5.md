## Edges (the canonical set, so the diagram is unambiguous)

- **`age ‚Üí g`** ‚Äî age acts on the skills *through* general ability (developmental
  level), **not** directly. (The apparent direct `age ‚Üí letter-sounds` link was
  an over-adjustment artefact: raw age‚Üîskills are all positive, and the "ability"
  control was itself age-dependent.)
- **`SES ‚Üí g`** ‚Äî dashed, weak, *tested* (background only in this sample).
- **`g ‚Üí {letter-sounds, language/vocab, blending, non-verbal MA, baseline
  reading}`** ‚Äî one general-ability factor drives the correlated baselines (this
  is why the skills correlate and why non-verbal ability drops out once language
  + letter-sounds are in).
- **`intervention ‚Üí sessions (dose) ‚Üí outcome`** plus a direct **`intervention ‚Üí
  outcome`** (ITT). The intervention cannot touch pre-measured baselines.
- **each baseline skill `‚Üí outcome`**; **`baseline reading ‚Üí outcome`**
  (autoregression / regression-to-mean).
- **`age ‚Üí outcome`** ‚Äî younger children gain more, net of baseline.
- **`non-verbal MA ‚Üí outcome`** and **`behaviour ‚Üí outcome`** ‚Äî dashed, *tested*
  (not assumed).

**Deliberately absent:** no `age ‚Üí skill` edges; no `SES ‚Üí outcome`; no
`SES ‚Üí dose`.

## Per-model subsets

Each model adjusts for a subset of this structure:

- **LRP65** (between-child predictors): the `g ‚Üí baselines`, `age ‚Üí g`,
  `age ‚Üí outcome`, and `skill/baseline-reading ‚Üí outcome` portion ‚Äî the
  intervention/dose arm is omitted because it is a predictor model, not ITT.
- **LRP66** (latent ability): the **reflective-`g` measurement specialisation**
  ‚Äî `g` is fitted as a reflective factor of the baseline skills.
- **ITT (LRP52‚Äì55)**: the `intervention ‚Üí sessions ‚Üí outcome` + direct-ITT arm,
  with baseline-of-outcome for precision; no predictor-cluster adjustment
  (randomisation handles confounding).
- **Mechanism/mediation (LRP56‚Äì62)**: per-effect back-door adjustment sets that
  are subsets of this DAG (see each `docs/models/<id>/index.qmd`).
