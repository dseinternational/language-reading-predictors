Their anchor-wave PSIS-LOO diagnostics contain no Pareto k above 0.7.

The LCF reporting posterior uses 6 chains × 6000 retained draws at `target_accept = 0.999` and passes its gate: maximum R-hat 1.00102, minimum effective sample size 5157, minimum chain BFMI 0.800 and zero divergences. The original pipeline silently skipped the pointwise log likelihood needed for LOO because PyMC 6.1 gave transform-free `LKJCorr` value variables an extra `_cholesky` suffix that did not match the saved posterior names. The repaired implementation evaluates the fitted model's exact marginal multivariate-normal log density from posterior `mean_z` and `Sigma_z`, in bounded chunks and with one contribution per child; an independent SciPy calculation agrees to `1e-10` across multiple missingness patterns. PSIS is then applied to that exact log likelihood, so PSIS-LOO remains an importance-sampling approximation. Recovered per-child PSIS-LOO is elpd −1663.99 (SE 55.70), effective parameter count 40.83; 1/54 children has 0.7 < Pareto k ≤ 1 (maximum 0.883) and none exceeds 1. The influential child means any future model comparison needs care.

The same naming mismatch had left the parameter-prior group empty and omitted `log_prior`. The repaired trace now contains 1000 parameter-prior draws and constrained-scale log-prior terms for all nine free-RV groups, restoring prior/posterior overlays and power-scaling sensitivity. That diagnostic flags potential prior–data conflict for all 12 per-wave factor-correlation pairs and all three trait shares (prior sensitivity 0.054–0.104; likelihood sensitivity 0.202–0.336). It strengthens the requirement for an alternative-prior refit rather than satisfying it.

## Triangulation, not an attenuation gate

The original #313 wording assumed every latent correlation or slope must be at least as large as its observed counterpart. That is too strong. Classical measurement error often attenuates a simple observed bivariate association under restrictive assumptions, but the LCF latent-domain correlation, the mean indicator-pair correlation and #312's mutually adjusted nonlinear items-scale marginal are different estimands. Factor aggregation, residual assumptions, conditioning, regularisation, bounded scales and sampling uncertainty can move them in either direction. The report now treats the comparison as descriptive triangulation; legacy cross-check artifact and field names remain only for compatibility. The latent correlation has a posterior interval, whereas the mean observed indicator-pair comparator is a point estimate without its own interval, so this output does not quantify uncertainty in their gap.

In this reporting fit all 12 nominal latent domain correlations exceed the corresponding mean indicator-pair correlation, but that direction is descriptive rather than a pass condition and is not a prior-robust conclusion. The directed comparison with #312 is now reproducible rather than manually summarised: `lcf_concurrent_comparison.csv` contains 32 target/predictor/wave rows covering `L` against `R`/`E`/`TR`/`TE` and `TR`/`TE` against `L`/`B` at all four waves. Each row places the LCF target-item translation for a `+1 same-wave SD` predictor change, evaluated at the mean operating point, beside #312's adjusted average marginal effect for the same change.

Both quantities are directional, but they remain different estimands. The LCF translation aggregates indicators into domains, holds the third latent domain fixed and evaluates one operating point. The concurrent model holds the other five observed tests plus age and group fixed and averages its nonlinear marginal effect over analysed rows. Differences therefore show sensitivity to measurement, conditioning and marginalisation choices; they are not an agreement test and do not identify a causal skill pathway.
