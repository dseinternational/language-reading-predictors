## Motivation

The first round of feature selection (notes/202604161333) used RMSE-tuned
hyperparameters with outlier exclusion (ewrswr_gain >= 15 removed). This
optimised for prediction accuracy on typical cases but excluded the children
who made the most progress — arguably the most informative observations for
an exploratory study.

For exploratory purposes — identifying which factors most influence reading
gains — we prefer to:

1. Keep all observations (no outlier exclusion) so the full range of outcomes
   informs importance rankings
2. Use MAE as both the LightGBM training objective and tuning metric, because
   MAE treats all errors linearly and is not dominated by a few extreme cases

## Comparison: MAE vs RMSE feature selection

The MAE-tuned selection produced a substantially smaller final set:

| | RMSE-tuned selection | MAE-tuned selection |
|---|---|---|
| Final predictors | 15 | 6 (or 5) |
| Features retained by both | -- | age, b1exto, celf, yarclet, attend, blending |

Features retained by RMSE but dropped by MAE selection:
- time, gender, aptinfo, eowpvt, trog, deappin, deappvo, deappfi, agebooks

Features retained by MAE but dropped by RMSE selection:
- hearing (retained through select05, dropped at select06)

Key differences:
- MAE selection drops all three DEAP speech measures (deappin, deappvo,
  deappfi) which were important in the RMSE model. The MAE model's shallow
  trees (max_depth=3) may not capture the non-linear contribution these make.
- MAE selection drops time, which was rank 3 in the RMSE model. This may
  reflect that time's effect is largely captured by age.
- MAE selection drops gender, which was rank 7 in the RMSE model.
- Both agree on the core: age, yarclet, attend, celf, b1exto are important.

## Final model structure

Two models share the same 6-predictor set (age, b1exto, celf, blending,
yarclet, attend), no outlier exclusion, n=157:

- `lrp01` (primary) -- MAE-tuned, for identifying predictor importance
- `lrp01_prediction` (variant) -- RMSE-tuned, for prediction comparison

The MAE-tuned model (lrp01) is preferred for both exploratory and prediction
purposes at this sample size. The RMSE-tuned variant is retained for comparison
but over-trains significantly.

Domain coverage of the final 6 predictors:
- Demographics: age
- Language: b1exto (expressive vocabulary), celf (basic concept knowledge)
- Reading: yarclet (letter sounds), blending (phoneme blending)
- Intervention: attend (sessions attended)
