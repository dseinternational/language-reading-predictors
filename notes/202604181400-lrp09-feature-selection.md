## Motivation

LRP09 predicts CELF receptive-grammar **gains** (`celf_gain`) —
paralleling LRP01 (word-reading gains), LRP03 (expressive-vocabulary
gains), LRP05 (letter-sound knowledge gains), and LRP07 (receptive-
vocabulary gains). Baseline started from the full
`Predictors.DEFAULT_GAIN` set (34 predictors; `celf` is already a
member of that set so the GainModel auto-include is a no-op),
n=160, with no outlier exclusion.

Following the established playbook, MAE hyperparameter tuning on
the full predictor set precedes any feature-selection work.

**Many-slow-deep-trees with strong L2 regime**: 194 trees, very

Similar in spirit to LRP05's tune but with much stronger L2.
Different from LRP07's few-fast-shallow regime — CELF gains
apparently reward an ensemble averaging over many weak deep
learners.

Every CV metric improves. **CV R² crosses from negative to
positive** (−0.011 → 0.094) — the untuned model was worse than
predicting the mean. CV MAE drops 0.09, CV MedAE drops 0.16.
In-sample R² drops from heavy memorisation (0.986) to honest
tight fit (0.628). CV R² std tightens from 0.349 to 0.199.

CV R² of 0.094 is similar to LRP07 (0.090) and LRP03 (0.087) —
firmly in the "harder gain targets" cluster. LRP05's `yarclet_gain`
(0.218) remains the easiest gain to predict. The 0-18 coarse
scale likely caps R² achievable here.

### Notable observations

- **`celf` base level dominates** at 0.610 (down from 0.981 at
  baseline). Tuning redistributed signal away from the base but
  it still carries ~11× the next feature.
- **Grammar measures `trog` (0.039) and `aptgram` (0.027) are
  present but not top** — rank 3 and 9 respectively. The nearest-
  construct predictors contribute but don't dominate. Instead
  the top non-base features are a diffuse cluster: articulation
  (`deappfi`), phonology (`spphon`), language (`b1exto`,
  `ewrswr`), plus `attend` and `age`.
- **`deappfi` at rank 2 (0.046)** — articulation quality leads
  the non-base predictors. Consistent with LRP05 and LRP07 where
  articulation is prominent.
- **No features at exactly 0.000**. The noise floor is shallower
  than LRP07 (11 at 0) or LRP06 (9 at 0) — all 34 features
  contribute something. Likely an artefact of the deep-tree
  many-estimator regime giving every feature some small share.
- **Importance more evenly distributed** than other models:
  ranks 2–23 range only 0.012–0.046, no sharp cliffs.

## Candidates for Select01

**Tier A — ≤ 0.005 importance (8 features):**

`area`, `vision`, `numchil`, `earinf`, `behav`, `hearing`, `group`,
`gender` (borderline at 0.007).

Much thinner Tier A than LRP07 (had ~15). With deep-tree many-
estimator tuning, the noise floor is shallow — the aggressive
one-shot cut that worked for LRP05/LRP06/LRP07 would be too
blunt here.

**Tier B — 0.006–0.015 (10 features, near-noise):**

`group` (0.006), `gender` (0.007), `yarcsi` (0.007), `agespeak`
(0.008), `agebooks` (0.009), `dadedupost16` (0.012), `erbword`
(0.013), `blending` (0.013), `time` (0.014), `mumedupost16`
(0.016).

A **conservative** Select01 drops Tier A (~8). A **moderate** cut
(probably the right call for LRP09) adds the ~5 lowest Tier B
features where redundancy is plausible — target ~18–20
predictors.
