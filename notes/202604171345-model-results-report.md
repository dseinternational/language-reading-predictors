|---|---|---|---:|---|

| LRP03 | Expressive-vocabulary gain | `eowpvt_gain` | 161 | Change between timepoints |

This report takes the *best-fitting* configuration of each family (as

All data and models are preliminary; findings are for research use.

## What we have done

### Pipeline and methodology

- **Estimator**: LightGBM across all models. Adopted in April 2026
  after paired comparison against Random Forest (`notes/202604121451-
  lightgbm-model-selection.md`): accuracy within 0.04 CV RMSE of RF at
  ~30× lower wall-time, tighter importance rankings, and explicit
  zero-importance zeros that streamline feature selection.
- **Cross-validation**: `GroupKFold` grouped by `subject_id`. 51–53
  splits under the reporting configuration (essentially near-leave-
  one-subject-out), so each test fold is a single child's longitudinal
  timepoints.
- **Objective**: LightGBM `objective="mae"` for the primary exploratory
  models, tuned to minimise CV MAE via Optuna (TPE, 150 trials, 10-
  split inner GroupKFold, seed 47). The MAE objective prevents
  importance rankings from being dominated by a handful of high-
  scoring children. RMSE-tuned and quantile-tuned variants exist for
  sub-questions (absolute prediction accuracy and median prediction
  respectively).
- **Target transforms**: `log1p` for LRP02 (hard floor at 0, heavy
  right skew); signed-log for LRP01 (signed gains); none for LRP03 /
  LRP04 (mild skew). Predictions are inverse-transformed before
  scoring, so all reported metrics remain in original target units.
- **Feature selection**: iterative importance-based pruning using
  permutation importance + distance-correlation redundancy, starting
  from `Predictors.DEFAULT_GAIN` (34 predictors) or
  `Predictors.DEFAULT_LEVEL` (32 predictors). Each selection step is
  documented with a `SelectionStep` on the model class and a dated
  markdown note in `notes/`.
- **Metrics**: classic CV mean-of-fold metrics (MAE, RMSE, R², MedAE)
  plus **pooled out-of-fold metrics** (MAE, RMSE, R², MedAE computed
  on the stacked OOF predictions against the global target mean).
  Per-fold R² is unstable under near-leave-one-subject-out grouping
  because within-fold target variance is tiny; the pooled OOF R² is
  the reliable generalisation signal and is the headline metric in
  this report.
- **Diagnostics**: permutation importance, SHAP values + scatter
  plots, partial-dependence plots, distance-correlation dendrograms
  and heatmaps, mutual-information heatmaps, Spearman correlations,
  and importance × cluster tables for each fitted model.
- **Exploration-layer diagnostics** (added after importance pruning
  proved to have four distinct failure modes — see
  *Direction and stability diagnostics* below):
  - **Construct-level importance** — permutation importance
    aggregated by construct family (language composite, reading
    decoding, articulation, demographics, …) via
    `Variables.CONSTRUCTS`. Reveals which *domains* matter even when
    the within-construct instrument that wins the race differs
    across variants.
  - **SHAP direction diagnostics** — per-feature Spearman rank
    correlation between feature value and SHAP value. Gives a
    precise numerical direction of effect and a monotonicity score,
    less error-prone than hand-reading beeswarms. Produces
    `shap_direction_diagnostics.csv` per fit.
  - **Stability selection** — 30 subject-level bootstrap resamples
    at 80% subject fraction; records per-feature top-5 appearance
    rate and rank IQR across bootstraps. Separates robustly
    important predictors from those whose rank is fold-luck, which
    matters most for the low-R² gain models. Produces
    `stability_selection.csv`.

### Sensitivity investigations

- **Time-invariant baseline measures**: 14 predictors are captured
  once at baseline (parent report or t1-only assessment) and
  replicated across timepoints in the long format — `hearing`,
  `earinf`, `hearing_c`, `vision`, `numchil`, `mumocc`, `dadocc`,
  `agebooks`, `mumedupost16`, `dadedupost16`, `bedtimeread`,
  `othertimeread`, `blocks`, `agespeak` (listed in
  `Variables.TIME_INVARIANT_BASELINES`). `GroupKFold` prevents
  test-set leakage but not training-side row-count inflation. A
  sensitivity check on LRP02 (`notes/202604171220-lrp02-time-
  invariant-weight-sensitivity.md`) found that inverse-frequency
  subject weighting leaves the importance rankings essentially
  unchanged, but that dropping `agebooks` + `agespeak` and retuning
  produces a strictly better model on every mean-based pooled
  metric. Layer-1 mitigation (labelling these features so reviewers
  can flag them during selection) is in place.
- **MAE vs RMSE tuning**: RMSE-tuned variants consistently over-fit
  at these sample sizes (in-sample R² approaches 1.0 while CV R²
  drops). MAE tuning is the primary regime; RMSE-tuned variants are
  kept for cross-checking prediction accuracy.
- **Quantile α=0.5 objective**: LightGBM's pinball loss at the
  median directly minimises MedAE and is a useful comparison point
  for typical-case prediction. On LRP04 it improves every mean CV
  metric modestly; on LRP02 with the log transform it produces the
  tightest MedAE seen in that family (2.90).
- **Cap-fix for `n_estimators`**: a bug in `RunConfig` was replacing
  (rather than capping) tuned `n_estimators` during `--config test`
  / `dev` runs. Fixed in PR #16; all variants were subsequently
  refit under honoured tuned values (`notes/202604171017-lrp02-
  refit-under-cap-fix.md`).

### What this model indicates

The strongest predictors of word-reading gain are **developmental
stage** (age), **intervention engagement** (attendance), and
**pre-reading skills** (letter sounds, phoneme blending). Language
measures (expressive vocabulary, basic concepts) contribute but at
roughly half the magnitude of the top-three predictors. Crucially,
*baseline word-reading score* (`ewrswr`) was dropped during selection
(importance 0.000 in Select02) — how far a child has already come in
word reading does not, in this sample, determine how much further
they gain between timepoints. That contrasts sharply with LRP03
where baseline `eowpvt` dominates gain prediction.

### Caveats

- **Low generalisation strength**: pooled R² = 0.10 means the model
  explains ~10% of between-child variance in reading gain. Individual
  prediction is unreliable; the importance *rankings* carry the
  research signal.
- **n=157 with 53-split CV**: per-fold variance is high; conclusions
  come from pooled OOF and importance stability rather than absolute
  CV values.

substantially below the cognitive / linguistic cluster but are
retained because they clear the noise floor and provide
interpretive breadth.

## Cross-cutting findings

### Level prediction is 5-6× easier than gain prediction

| Family | Framing | Pooled R² | In-sample R² | Gap |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| LRP02 | Level | 0.64 | 0.95 | 0.31 |
| LRP04 | Level | 0.69 | 0.87 | 0.18 |
| LRP01 | Gain | 0.10 | 0.25 | 0.15 |

Level models generalise strongly; gain models have weak signal at
this sample size. This is structural — gains have much less between-
child variance to exploit once a child's baseline level is accounted
for. The generalisation gap (in-sample − CV) is not hugely different
between framings (0.15–0.31), but the *absolute* CV R² is lower on
the gain side because there is simply less signal.

**Observations**:

1. **Language composites (`aptinfo`, `celf`, `trog`)** appear in
   every model — they are consistently predictive across both
   outcomes and both framings.
2. **`yarclet` (letter sounds)** is critical for reading (top of
   LRP02, top-3 of LRP01) and a secondary predictor even for
   expressive vocabulary level (LRP04). Letter-sound knowledge
   appears to be a broad literacy and language-development signal.
3. **Articulation (`deappvo`, `deappin`)** matters far more for
   vocabulary gain than for reading. Articulation quality is linked
   to rate of change in expressive vocabulary but not to current
   reading level.
4. **`attend` (intervention attendance)** appears only in LRP01
   — word-reading gain. This is the one outcome where the
   intervention directly targets the skill being measured, and the
   one model where attendance survives feature selection.
5. **Age** is top-5 in all four models — developmental stage
   matters across the board, and its role is largest in the gain
   models (LRP01 rank 1, LRP03 rank 5).
6. **Baseline level of the target** has a strikingly different
   role in the two gain models: `eowpvt` dominates LRP03
   (importance 0.25, rank 1) but `ewrswr` was dropped by LRP01's
   feature selection. Word-reading gain is driven by foundational
   sub-skills (letter sounds, phonemes) rather than current reading
   level; expressive-vocabulary gain is driven by current vocabulary
   level.

### Direction and stability diagnostics

The three exploration-layer diagnostics (construct-level importance,
SHAP direction diagnostics, subject-level stability selection)
produce a substantially more disciplined picture than permutation
importance plus hand-read beeswarms alone. They were added to this
report after the beeswarm pass and they **overturn several of the
earlier direction calls**:

- **LRP01 `age`** is cleanly monotonic **negative** (Spearman −0.90).
  The earlier read of "mixed / non-monotonic" was wrong —
  hand-reading the beeswarm missed that the apparent extreme-
  negative outliers at high age were in fact the monotonic trend's
  tail.
- **LRP02 `agebooks` and `agespeak` are non-monotonic** (Spearman
  0.04, 0.11) — their importance is genuine but there is no clean
  direction. The earlier "earlier milestones → higher reading" read
  was not supported.
- **LRP02 `mumedupost16` is negatively directional** (Spearman −0.64),
  not positive as originally read. Coding-convention check needed
  before interpreting; the SHAP-Spearman disagreement with the
  earlier hand read is unambiguous.
- **LRP03 `age` is non-monotonic** (Spearman −0.30), not positive
  as originally read.
- **LRP04 `deappin` is non-monotonic** (Spearman −0.12), not the
  "small monotonic positive" originally read. `deappin` also fails
  the stability test (10% top-5 appearance).

**Direction-and-stability patterns across models:**

1. **Level models are directionally clean for their substantive
   predictors** (LRP02 top 7, LRP04 top 6 all monotonic_+; the
   non-monotonic `agebooks` / `agespeak` / `deappin` are
   secondary). The core "more capability → higher level" story
   stands.
2. **LRP03's `eowpvt` is the cleanest signal in the entire
   study**: Spearman −0.84, 100% stability. The regression-to-
   the-mean finding is the most robust single result across the
   four models.
3. **LRP01 is uniformly clean and stable**: all 6 predictors
   monotonic, top 5 at ≥ 80% stability. A weak-R² model with a
   strong direction-and-stability profile — the right combination
   for treating it as exploratory.
4. **Non-monotonic features cluster in the middle-tier predictors**
   across both level models. They add noise to the interpretive
   story without changing the top-tier conclusions.
5. **Stability selection reveals a hard cliff in LRP02.** The top
   6–7 predictors appear in the top-5 of ≥ 50% of bootstraps;
   predictors ranked 8 and below appear in the top-5 of < 15%.
   Their ordering in the single-fit permutation importance is
   partly fold-luck.

Each outcome has a **dominant construct** that carries the
majority of the model's explanatory power:

- **Word-reading gain (LRP01)**: reading decoding + intervention + age, roughly balanced.
- **Word-reading level (LRP02)**: reading decoding (5.5× anything else) — cognitive / phonological skills dominate.
- **Expressive-vocabulary gain (LRP03)**: expressive vocabulary (baseline regression-to-mean) + language composite + articulation.
- **Expressive-vocabulary level (LRP04)**: language composite (4.8× anything else) — the `aptinfo` domain alone.

The construct view also shows the **informative absences**:
LRP03 has zero reading-decoding signal; LRP04 has zero articulation
contribution beyond `deappin` (which is itself non-directional).
These gaps are as interpretively important as the dominances — they
mean those domains are genuinely orthogonal at this sample size.

**Take-home**: for exploration, report *construct-level* importance
first, then zoom into instruments within a dominant construct only
for substantive clarification. The within-construct substitutions
(which instrument "won" — `eowpvt` vs `b1exto` vs `aptinfo`) are
mostly noise; which *domain* won is the stable signal.

### Target transforms pay off unequally

| Model | Transform | CV R² (raw → transform) |
|---|---|---|
| LRP02 | `log1p` | 0.60 → 0.64 (+0.04) |
| LRP01 | signed-log | 0.10 → 0.10 (tied) |
| LRP03 | none explored | — |
| LRP04 | none explored | — |

The LRP02 `log1p` benefit comes from its hard floor at 0 and heavy
right skew. LRP01's signed-log transform helps negative-gain
quartile MAE by ~14% in-sample but does not translate to CV R². LRP03
and LRP04 have milder skew and have not been tested with transforms
yet — a cheap sanity check for a future PR.

### Mean-based vs median-based metrics

MAE-tuning (the primary regime) produces different optima than
quantile-tuning (direct median minimisation). On LRP04, `lrp04_
quantile` improves every mean CV metric modestly but doesn't clear
α=0.05 at n=10 folds. On LRP02 with the log transform,
`lrp02_log_quantile` achieves the tightest MedAE seen in that
family (2.90). When typical-case prediction accuracy matters more
than average, the quantile variants are preferred; the MAE-tuned
primaries remain the **importance-ranking reference** because they
provide the most balanced predictor weightings.

## Limitations

1. **Sample size**. n=157–215 across the four outcomes. Near-leave-
   one-subject-out CV is the only robust option at this scale, but
   it yields high per-fold variance. Absolute CV values are reported
   with care; pooled OOF metrics and importance stability carry the
   substantive signal.
2. **Preliminary models, exploratory study**. Feature-selection
   decisions reflect current evidence and may shift as more data
   arrive. None of these models should be used for individual
   prediction or clinical decisions.
3. **Time-invariant baselines**. The sensitivity check confirmed
   that `agebooks` and `agespeak` (replicated across timepoints per
   child) can inflate their training-side importance, though the
   distortion on current LRP02 importance rankings is small. The
   systematic issue is labelled (`Variables.TIME_INVARIANT_
   BASELINES`) but not yet acted on per-model beyond LRP02.
4. **Statistical significance of comparisons**. Paired-fold tests
   at n=10 lack the power to reject α=0.05 for modest effects.
   Directional consistency (fold-win counts) and pooled OOF metrics
   are the primary comparison signals.
5. **No causal claims**. Every finding above is associational.
   Predictor importance indicates "which features help this
   GBDT explain between-child variance on this outcome", not
   "which features *cause* progress".

## Next-step candidates

- **LRP03 / LRP04 target transforms**. Cheap sanity check; milder
  skew makes the expected payoff modest but not zero.
- **LRP03 / LRP04 quantile variants**. Mirror the LRP02 / LRP04
  quantile investigations on the gain task and the raw-target
  level task.
- **LRP01 prediction variant under log transform**. Currently only
  the MAE-tuned log variant exists.
- **Sub-population sensitivity** for any model recommending
  promotion from primary to variant (e.g. `lrp02_drop_age_
  measures`): per-age-band and per-baseline-score slices to confirm
  pooled-metric improvements aren't masking regressions on
  identifiable sub-groups.
- **Time-invariant baseline treatment for LRP04**. Four of LRP04's
  seven predictors are time-varying (aptinfo, rowpvt, ewrswr, celf,
  age) and two are t1 / replicated (deappin, agebooks — *agebooks*
  is not actually in LRP04's final set; only `age` and `deappin`
  here are on the boundary). Worth re-checking under the
  `TIME_INVARIANT_BASELINES` label whether any LRP04 features
  warrant the drop-and-retune sensitivity check we did for LRP02.
- **Cross-model feature-stability analysis**. The cross-cutting
  table above is informal; a principled joint analysis would
  stabilise rankings across models where the same feature appears
  and quantify the uncertainty.
