Date: 2026-04-18

## Decision

The joint model's age Gaussian process is **disabled by default**
(`use_age_gp=False`). A new flag on `build_joint_model` exposes this
setting; with the flag off, the shared `mu_A` HSGP and all eight
outcome-specific `delta_A_k` HSGPs are omitted from the PyMC graph.
The flag can be turned back on for a sensitivity fit via
`ModelSpec.extra={"use_age_gp": True, ...}`.

Combined with the earlier LKJ-residual drop
(`notes/202604181630-lrp55-lkj-drop-and-comparison-fix.md`), LRP55 is
now a purely linear Beta-Binomial over eight outcomes with shared
predictor structure.

- **Divergences eliminated.** Dropping the nine age-GP amplitudes
  removes the residual Neal's-funnel that the LKJ drop had not
  resolved. The source of the LRP55 divergences was the η × basis-
  weights geometry of the age GP, not the LKJ block.
- **ELPD improves** (−1164.1 vs −1167.3; ΔELPD = 3.2, SE ≈ 25). LOO
  is indifferent to slightly prefers the simpler model — consistent
  with the LRP52 age-GP finding.
- **p_loo halves** from 101.6 (LKJ on) to 66.7 (both off) —
  confirming the removed blocks were spending effective degrees of
  freedom on noise.
- **ESS_bulk for τ[W] triples** from 18 794 → 50 984 — the sampler
  no longer hits pathological geometry on ~8 % of transitions, so
  every post-tune transition contributes signal.
- **τ shifts are small but systematic.** All τ's move slightly
  toward zero in the simpler model (the GP had been absorbing some
  age signal that the linear intercept + baseline couplings now
  carry). Shifts are well within MC error of the previous runs.
