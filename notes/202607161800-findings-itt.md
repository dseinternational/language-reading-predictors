## What these models ask

The trial randomly assigned children to start the reading/language intervention **now** (immediate arm) or **after a wait** (waiting-list arm). "Intention-to-treat" (ITT) compares the two groups **as randomised**, regardless of how much intervention each child actually did. Because assignment was random, a difference between the groups is a genuine **cause-and-effect** estimate of offering the programme — this is the study's flagship causal analysis. Note that this is a single as-randomised contrast over one trial window, **not** a timepoint-by-timepoint growth trajectory across the four waves: modelling growth over time is the job of the `level_factors`/`growth` families, and no result here should be paraphrased as a growth-curve statement.

Each model fits one outcome with a Beta-Binomial model (appropriate for a bounded test score out of a fixed number of items) and reports the treatment effect **τ** (tau) translated onto the **items scale** ("+3.5 of the letter-sounds items"). For two outcomes that sit on the floor for most children — phonetic spelling (P) and nonword reading (N) — the headline is instead a **risk difference**: the change in the _chance of scoring above zero_ ("coming off the floor"), in percentage points (pp). This "floor rule" (`bernoulli_offfloor`) replaces the items-scale effect for those two outcomes only.

**A quick Bayesian glossary** (these terms recur below). A **95% credible range/interval** is the range within which the true parameter lies with 95% probability _given the data and the priors_ — unlike a frequentist confidence interval, it is a direct probability statement about the parameter. **P(effect>0)** is the posterior probability that the true effect is positive (helps). **ROPE** is a "region of practical equivalence" — a band around zero deemed too small to matter (e.g. ±1 item); the ROPE figure answers whether the effect is big enough to care about, which is a separate question from whether it is positive.

**How to read the evidence label** (used throughout): it grades the probability that the effect is in the stated direction — _suggestive_ ≥ 75%, _moderate_ ≥ 91%, _strong_ ≥ 97%, _very strong_ ≥ 99%; below 75% is _inconclusive_. The label describes **confidence in direction, not size**. Because the boundaries are sharp, percentages below are shown to one decimal so the label always matches the raw number (e.g. 98.6% is _strong_, not _very strong_; 96.8% is _moderate_, not _strong_).

## Convergence gate

All 27 in-scope ITT models **passed** the gate (R̂ ≤ 1.01, ESS ≥ 400, zero divergences, BFMI ≥ 0.30) — so every result below is safe to interpret _as far as sampling is concerned_. One extra caveat a science reader will want: the prior-sensitivity (power-scaling) diagnostic flags a **potential prior-data conflict on τ in 12 of the 27** models — mostly the strong-effect anchors (ITT-003, 007, 008, 009, 011, 013, 019, 023, 025, 028, 113, 114, i.e. letter sounds L, word reading W under SES adjustment, phoneme blending B, and the floored P/N). This is not a convergence failure and does not overturn any headline, but it means the τ posterior for those models moves noticeably if the prior is re-weighted, so treat their precise credible ranges as prior-influenced rather than purely data-driven.

## The one-paragraph story

The programme most clearly helps the skills it directly teaches and the skills closest to decoding: **very strong** evidence for **letter-sound knowledge** (+3.5 items), and **strong** evidence for **word reading** (+2.4), **phoneme blending** (+1.0), and **directly taught expressive vocabulary** (+1.5). Directly taught _receptive_ vocabulary (TR, +1.4) is a shade behind at _moderate_ (96.8%). Effects on **broad, standardised vocabulary (R and E)** are **inconclusive and probably negligible** — most of the posterior sits inside the "too small to matter" zone — so the benefit does not generalise to general vocabulary over the trial window. Basic concepts (F, +0.9) and receptive grammar (T, +0.7) lean positive but only _suggestively_. The floored spelling/nonword measures (P, N) lean positive but are too uncertain to call.

## The adjusted associations (covariate terms — read with care, none is causal)

Only τ is causal. Every covariate in these models is a **precision term** entered to sharpen τ, and each is a latent-ability-confounded **adjusted association**, never "X drives the outcome" (reading them as effects would be the Table-2 fallacy). The note deliberately keeps them out of the headline tables for that reason; recorded here for completeness:

- **Own-baseline coupling (`gamma_own`)** is a strong positive association in every model — roughly **+0.6 to +0.88 on the logit scale** — meaning a child's own earlier score on the same test strongly tracks their later score, as expected. It is strongest for word reading (ITT-010/024/027 at ≈ +0.81, the highest in the family) and around +0.6–0.73 for the vocabulary and letter-sound outcomes. This is a within-child persistence association, not a treatment effect.
- **Linear age (`gamma_A`)** is centred near zero with a 95% credible range spanning zero in **every model except ITT-113** (letter sounds + SES), where it is +0.235 (95% +0.007 to +0.46) and just excludes zero — the sole covariate-age association in the family that does. Elsewhere age is inconclusive as a precision term (which is fine; it is there for precision, not interpretation).
- **Family SES** (maternal and paternal post-16 education, age the child first had books; ITT-013/113) — all three adjusters have credible ranges crossing zero (e.g. ITT-013 W: mum-edu +0.038, dad-edu −0.139, age-books −0.065). No SES association is distinguishable from zero here.
- **Block-design non-verbal ability** (`gamma_blocks`; ITT-017–024) is small (≤ ~+0.12 on the logit scale) and crosses zero in all eight fits — consistent with ability being a modest, uncertain background correlate once own baseline is in the model.
- **Site/area** (`gamma_area`; ITT-027/028) crosses zero in both.

The takeaway: these are confounded background associations that improve the precision of τ; the family's causal discipline is intact because none of them is being read as an effect.

## What is causal here, and what is not

Only **τ** (the group contrast) is causal, and only because assignment was randomised. The models deliberately include just a child's **own baseline** score, **age**, and (in the robustness variants) SES / non-verbal ability / site as _precision_ terms — they are not "predictors of who improves" and must not be read that way. Which covariates appear was fixed in advance by the study's causal diagram (DAG); a skill that is absent was excluded by the diagram, not found unimportant. Positive τ = the programme helps.
