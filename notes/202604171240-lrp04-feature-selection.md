## Motivation

LRP04 predicts expressive-vocabulary **level** (`eowpvt`), paralleling
LRP02's task on word-reading level. Baseline started from the full
`Predictors.DEFAULT_LEVEL` set minus the target (32 predictors),
n=215, with no outlier exclusion.

Following the established playbook, tune MAE hyperparameters on the
full predictor set before any feature-selection work. The MAE
objective keeps importance rankings from being dominated by a few
high-scoring observations and pairs naturally with median-absolute-
error (MedAE) evaluation.

## Hyperparameter tuning on all 32 predictors (n=215)

Optuna tuning (150 trials, 10-split GroupKFold, seed 47, scoring=mae,
lgbm_objective=mae). **Tuner-inner CV MAE 6.1434 ± 1.0213.**

| Parameter | Baseline (untuned) | MAE-tuned |
|---|---|---|
| n_estimators | 500 | **298** |
| learning_rate | 0.05 | 0.0195 |
| num_leaves | 15 | 17 |
| max_depth | 6 | **3** |
| min_child_samples | 16 | 4 |
| subsample | 0.8 | 0.688 |
| colsample_bytree | 0.8 | 0.642 |
| reg_alpha | 0.1 | 0.002 |
| reg_lambda | 0.1 | 0.003 |

The tuner settled on a **many-slow-shallow-trees ensemble**: 298
trees, very slow learning (0.019), depth-3 trees, small leaf size.
Very different from LRP03's 16-tree fast-learning regime, and
closer in spirit to the LRP02 log-space tune. Larger n=215 gives
the model room to grow many conservative trees without
over-fitting.

### Baseline-under-defaults vs MAE-tuned (test config, 10-fold GroupKFold)

| Metric | Untuned baseline (500 trees) | MAE-tuned (298 trees) |
|---|---|---|
| CV MAE | 6.562 ± 1.719 | **6.156 ± 1.115** |
| CV RMSE | 7.898 | **7.679** |
| **CV R²** | 0.470 | **0.543 ± 0.278** |
| CV MedAE | 6.043 | **5.268** |
| **In-sample R²** | **0.998** | **0.913** |

Every CV metric improves. CV R² rises from 0.470 to 0.543. CV
MedAE drops 0.77. In-sample R² falls 0.998 → 0.913 — the tuned
model still fits in-sample tightly but not to the memorisation
level the untuned 500-tree fit showed.

The CV R² of 0.543 is notably higher than LRP03's (0.087 at the
11-predictor retune). This is consistent with level prediction
being easier than gain prediction at this sample size — level
has much more signal variance.

### Observations

- **`b1exto` and `aptinfo` dominate** (0.116 and 0.107) — expressive
  vocabulary measures. This makes sense: the target is itself an
  expressive-vocabulary instrument, so other expressive-language
  measures carry the strongest signal.
- **Top 5 are all language / vocabulary measures** (`b1exto`,
  `aptinfo`, `rowpvt`, `ewrswr`, `celf`). Reading is in there via
  `ewrswr` and `yarclet`.
- **Speech articulation features** (`deappin`, `deappfi`, `deappvo`)
  are far less prominent than on LRP03's gain task — makes sense,
  articulation matters more for *rate of change* than for *current
  level*.
- **Demographics are all low** — `dadedupost16` (0.009) is the
  strongest, but still well below the language features.
- **2 features at exactly 0.000**: `group`, `hearing`.
- **15 more features below 0.005**: big tail of near-noise
  features.
