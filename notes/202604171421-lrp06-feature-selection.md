## Motivation

LRP06 predicts letter-sound knowledge **level** (`yarclet`) —
paralleling LRP02 (word-reading level) and LRP04 (expressive-
vocabulary level). Baseline started from the full
`Predictors.DEFAULT_LEVEL` set minus the target (32 predictors),
n=214, with no outlier exclusion.

**Left-skewed with a ceiling at 32** — many children score at the
instrument maximum. The skew is in the *opposite* direction to LRP02
(right-skewed, floor at 0) and LRP04 (mild right skew, no floor).

Implications:

- `log1p` transform is **inappropriate** here (wrong skew direction).
- A reflection-log (`log1p(32 − y)`) or quantile objective would be
  the relevant alternatives to investigate later.

### Notable observations

- **`ewrswr` is overwhelmingly dominant** at importance 0.500 —
  half of the total model importance. Word reading is by far the
  strongest predictor of letter-sound knowledge level. Makes
  sense: the two skills develop together, and word reading
  aggregates phoneme-grapheme knowledge.
- **`b1exto` (expressive vocabulary) second** at 0.105 — similar
  to LRP04's dominance pattern (`b1exto` was also top there).
  Expressive language and letter-sound knowledge are related.
- **`deappin` (articulation)** at rank 3 — a language domain
  feature from the speech side.
- **Speech-error cluster features** (`erbword`, `erbnw`) appear
  mid-table.
- **Reading cluster features** (`spphon`, `nonword`, `blending`)
  sit below `ewrswr` — they share signal with ewrswr but don't
  add much beyond it.
- **9 features at exactly 0.000 or negative**: `aptgram`, `trog`,
  `yarcsi`, `aptinfo`, `behav`, `gender`, `hearing`, `celf`, `area`.
  Plus several more below 0.005.
