## What these models ask

"Does a child's **earlier** level of one skill predict their **later change** in another, within the same child?" A latent change-score model (LCSM) separates each child's stable level from their change and estimates **cross-lagged couplings** between skills over time — a way to probe temporal ordering (does letter-sound growth lead reading growth, or vice versa?). The couplings are **conditional predictive associations among latent trajectories, not causal skill-to-skill effects**. Each model also carries a _separate_ randomised window-1 consistency contrast as a check.

## The one-paragraph story

The temporal pattern points the way reading theory expects: **earlier letter-sound knowledge is associated with more subsequent word-reading change** (lcsm-082/091), consistent with letter sounds leading reading rather than the reverse. Within vocabulary, earlier taught-receptive knowledge tracks later taught-expressive change. And older children show _less_ subsequent reading change (the recurring age pattern). The randomised window-1 consistency contrasts in each model point the same direction as the couplings, which is reassuring.

## What is causal

**Nothing.** These are within-child predictive couplings among latent trajectories under the model's assumptions — suggestive about ordering, but not manipulation effects. They complement, and are weaker-assumption cousins of, the [mediation](202607161800-findings-mediation.md) decompositions.
