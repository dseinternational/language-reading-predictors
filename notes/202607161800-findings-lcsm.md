## What these models ask

"Does a child's **earlier** level of one skill predict their **later change** in another, within the same child?" A latent change-score model (LCSM) separates each child's stable latent level from their period-to-period change and estimates **cross-lagged couplings** between skills over time — a way to probe temporal ordering (does letter-sound knowledge lead reading growth, or the reverse?). Every score is modelled on a Beta-Binomial likelihood through a logit (log-odds) linear predictor, so all coefficients below are on the **logit scale**: a positive coupling means an earlier higher level of the predictor is associated with more subsequent latent change in the outcome. The family ships no percentage-point or items translation for the couplings, so read the magnitudes comparatively (a larger absolute logit = a stronger association) rather than as a directly interpretable effect size. Crucially, the couplings are **conditional predictive associations among latent trajectories, not causal skill-to-skill effects** — they are confounded by each child's latent general ability, which the model only partially absorbs through the stable-level term. Four of the five models (081, 082, 091, 181) additionally carry a _separate_ randomised **window-1** consistency contrast — the intervention effect on the first, all-baseline-at-entry latent change — which _is_ a causal quantity; lcsm-067 carries no such contrast.

The five models in scope: **lcsm-067** (word-reading change with age as a covariate), **lcsm-081** (taught-receptive → taught-expressive), **lcsm-082** (letter-sounds/word-reading with a bidirectional dominance test on word-reading ↔ B), **lcsm-091** (change-on-change: does prior _change_, not just prior level, predict later change?), and **lcsm-181** (a no-reverse-coupling comparator for the taught-vocabulary route).

## Convergence gate

All 5 **passed** the gate (R-hat ≤ 1.01, ESS ≥ 400, BFMI ≥ 0.3, 0 divergences). Two sample a little less efficiently — lcsm-081 (min ESS ≈ 1207) and lcsm-181 (min ESS ≈ 1374) — but both clear the 400-effective-sample threshold comfortably. Worst R-hat across the family is 1.0072 (lcsm-081), i.e. R̂ ≤ 1.008; zero divergent transitions in every fit.

## How to read the tables

An **89% credible range** (CrI) is the interval the parameter lies in with 89% probability _given the data and priors_ — a direct probability statement about the parameter, unlike a frequentist confidence interval. **P(dir)** is the posterior probability the true effect is in its favoured direction (positive for a positive median, negative for a negative one); it grades the _direction_, not the size. Labels follow the project evidence ladder (#179): inconclusive < 0.75 ≤ suggestive < 0.91 ≤ moderate < 0.97 ≤ strong < 0.99 ≤ very strong. A **ROPE** ("region of practical equivalence") is a band around zero deemed too small to matter; this family does **not** ship a ROPE table, so no "big enough to matter" verdict is available — treat the logit magnitudes comparatively. Only terms flagged **causal** are licensed by randomisation; every coupling and covariate is an **association**.

## The one-paragraph story

The temporal pattern points the way reading theory expects: **earlier letter-sound level is associated with more subsequent word-reading change** (g_L_W = +0.249 in 082 and g_L = +0.226 in 091, both very strong), and earlier expressive vocabulary tracks later word-reading change too (+0.287 in 067, +0.249 in 091). Within vocabulary, earlier taught-_receptive_ knowledge is very strongly associated with later taught-_expressive_ change (+0.432 in 081, +0.449 in 181). Two patterns recur robustly across every model: word-reading (and taught-vocabulary) **self-feedback is negative** — children already high change less, i.e. regression toward each child's stable latent level — and older children show **less** subsequent change (the negative age gradient). But two cautions sit under the headline. First, the change-on-change model (091) shows that prior _change_ carries no predictive signal (h_L and h_E both inconclusive) — only prior _level_ does. Second, the one place the data could formally adjudicate "which skill leads" — the W ↔ B dominance test in 082 — is **inconclusive** (+0.094, P = 0.688), and the L → W route has no estimated reverse (W → L) to compare against, so the "letter sounds lead reading rather than the reverse" reading is a plausible story consistent with the couplings, not a resolved directional finding. The randomised window-1 consistency contrasts (present in 081/082/091/181) point the same direction as the couplings, which is reassuring; lcsm-067 carries none.

## What is causal

**Only the randomised window-1 contrasts.** Each cross-lagged coupling, self-feedback term, age gradient and covariate slope is a within-child predictive **association** among latent trajectories under the model's assumptions, confounded by latent general ability — suggestive about ordering, but not a manipulation effect and never "X drives Y". The exception is the window-1 latent-change ITT contrast (`itt_w1`), which _is_ licensed by randomisation: it is positive on word reading in all four models that carry it (~+0.40, P ≈ 0.98, strong) and positive on the taught measures and letter sounds where estimated, favouring the intervention — the one causal read on latent change in this family, and consistent with the [ITT](202607161800-findings-itt.md) suite. lcsm-067 has no such contrast, so it is entirely associational. These couplings complement, and are weaker-assumption cousins of, the [mediation](202607161800-findings-mediation.md) decompositions.
