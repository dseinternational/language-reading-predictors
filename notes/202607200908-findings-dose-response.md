# Dose-response (session intensity) findings (2026-07-20)

> [!NOTE]
> Drafted by a LLM-based AI tool (Claude Code/Opus 4.8).

This note reports the full re-fit (production `reporting` configuration: 6000 draws × 6000 tune × 6 chains) of the **dose-response family** — 5 models that ask whether children who attended **more intervention sessions** also made more progress. It is a companion to the shared reading guide `notes/202607200900-findings-00-index-and-reading-guide.md` (family note 08); read that first for the study background, the outcome measures and their maxima, and the house reporting standard. Every figure here is traceable to a model's own output CSVs in `output/statistical_models/models/<id>-reporting/`. Work in progress; all data are preliminary.

## What this family does, and the question it answers

The study is a **waitlist-crossover randomised trial**: children with Down syndrome were randomly assigned to receive a reading/phonics intervention immediately or after a wait, tested at four timepoints. The dose-response models set the randomised _arm_ aside and ask a different, descriptive question: **among children on the intervention, did those who received more teaching sessions show larger gains?** Each model relates a bounded outcome count (scored on a proportion-correct scale via a logit link — the log-odds transform that keeps predictions inside 0-100%) to the standardised number of sessions a child received in each study period, and — in four of the five fits — lets that dose-outcome relationship differ across the three period transitions ("period-varying dose"). A subject **random intercept** (a per-child offset that partially pools stable individual differences) is included in every model.

The single most important framing point: **session dose was not randomised, and it is a partial collider in the study's causal diagram (DAG).** How many sessions a child attended reflects ability, attendance, family circumstances and availability — a collider is a variable sitting downstream of two things we care about, so reading a slope _through_ it can open a spurious back-door path. Abler or better-attending children plausibly _both_ do more sessions _and_ score higher, and a raw dose slope cannot separate those stories. So **every number in this family is an observational (adjusted) association and a sensitivity/triangulation view — never a treatment effect.** The correct reading is "children who attended more scored higher, and here is whether that relationship bends", not "more sessions cause more progress". For the genuinely causal contrasts see the ITT suite (`notes/202607200901-findings-itt-suite.md`) and the difference-in-differences note.

How this differs from its neighbours. The **causal ITT effect τ** compares the randomly assigned arms — for word reading (W) the ITT τ is about **+2.4 of 79 words** (89% CI +0.7 to +4.1; strong evidence it helps). The dose slope answers a _within-treated_ "who progressed" question instead and carries no such causal licence. And the **mechanism family's knee-test** `lrp-rli-mech-191` ("intervention sessions → word reading, GP dose knee-test") uses a flexible Gaussian-process curve to ask whether the sessions→W relationship _bends_ (accelerates or saturates); this dose family instead fits a **straight line in standardised sessions within each period** — it estimates the slope's _direction and size_ and lets it differ _between periods_, but it does not fit a nonlinear knee within a period.

## How to read these numbers (recap)

Full conventions are in the shared reading guide. In brief:

- The point estimate is the posterior **median**; lead with the **interval**, not the point, because with ~54 children an estimate that just clears a threshold is on average inflated.
- Uncertainty is the **89% equal-tailed credible interval** ("89% posterior probability the value lies in this range"), with the inner **50%** interval as the "most-of-the-mass" range. 89%, not 95%, is the deliberate house standard.
- Direction is the **tail probability** — P(association > 0), read directly, never as a p-value. Its **evidence label** is fixed by round odds: _inconclusive_ (< 0.75), _suggestive_ (≥ 0.75), _moderate_ (≥ 0.91), _strong_ (≥ 0.97), _very strong_ (≥ 0.99). The label grades _evidence for a directional claim_; it never describes the _size_ of the effect.
- Two scales are reported. The **logit slope** (`dose_slope_summary.csv`) is the change in log-odds per **1 standard deviation (SD) of session count**; here 1 SD ≈ **31 sessions** (mean ≈ 54, so "+1 SD" is roughly going from 54 to 85 sessions). The interpretable translation (`dose_marginal_summary.csv`) is the **average marginal association in test items** — the average change in expected score, in items of that test, for a 1-SD dose increase, averaged over every fitted child-period.
- **Causal versus association.** Nothing here is causal. Every slope is an _adjusted association_; reading it as a lever is the Table-2 fallacy. Residual confounding by latent general ability remains, and the child random intercept pools stable differences but does **not** stand in for general ability.

## Per-model results

**Convergence gate.** Each fit is checked against R-hat ≤ 1.01 (chains agree), effective sample size (ESS) ≥ 400, BFMI ≥ 0.3, and zero divergences. Four models — **dose-077, dose-083, dose-084, dose-177** — carry a **divergence-only** flag: they pass R-hat, ESS and BFMI comfortably (max R-hat ≤ 1.0015, min ESS ≈ 3,800-6,400, all BFMI ≥ 0.5) and fail the gate _only_ on a small number of divergent transitions (17, 4, 2 and 13 respectively — all well under the METHODS ≤ 1% guidance). They are usable with that caveat noted. **dose-277 passes cleanly** (0 divergences, max R-hat 1.002, min ESS ≈ 3,600).

All are **associations** — none is causal.

**A note on the two probabilities.** For the four period-varying models the _logit_ headline (`dose_overall`) is the population hyper-mean of the three period slopes and additionally absorbs the between-period spread, so its 89% interval crosses zero and its own P(> 0) is only 0.81-0.91. The _items_ average-marginal association is tighter and more strongly positive (P 0.94-0.99) because it averages the **realised, per-period** slopes — which are individually far better determined, especially in period 1 — over the observed child-periods. Both point the same way; the items figure is the better single summary of "across the observed periods, more dose went with higher scores", while the logit hyper-mean honestly reflects the extra uncertainty about whether the periods share one common slope.

### dose-077 — word reading (W), base model (association)

Adjusts for group (G), age (A) and own baseline word reading (`W_pre`), with a period-varying dose slope and a child random intercept. Children who attended +1 SD (~31) more sessions scored on average **+1.16 of 79 words higher** (89% CI +0.38 to +1.98; 50% CI +0.83 to +1.50; P(> 0) = 0.992, **very strong** as a directional association) — roughly **+0.4 words per extra 10 sessions** (an approximate linear read; the logit link makes it mildly nonlinear). The relationship is clearly **front-loaded across periods**: period 1 slope +0.177 logit [+0.076, +0.293], P = 0.998 (very strong); period 2 +0.135 [−0.007, +0.273], P = 0.937 (moderate); period 3 +0.097 [−0.045, +0.214], P = 0.867 (suggestive). The between-period slope SD `sigma_dose_between_period` is 0.107 [0.010, 0.444] — its lower bound sits essentially at zero, so the data are consistent with a genuinely period-varying slope _or_ a flat common one. This is an association only: it does not show that adding sessions to a given child raises their reading; abler/better-attending children may drive both.

### dose-083 — letter-sound knowledge (L), base model (association)

L is the YARC letter-sound task (name the sound for each of 32 letters). Adjusts for G, A and own baseline `L_pre`, period-varying. Per +1 SD (~31) sessions the association is **+0.80 of 32 letter sounds** (89% CI +0.14 to +1.41; 50% +0.53 to +1.06; P(> 0) = 0.973, **strong**) — about **+0.26 letter sounds per 10 sessions**. Front-loading again: period 1 +0.227 logit [+0.084, +0.379], P = 0.995 (very strong); period 2 +0.169 [−0.049, +0.384], P = 0.899 (suggestive); period 3 +0.054 [−0.173, +0.237], P = 0.648 (**inconclusive** — direction unclear late). Between-period SD 0.180 [0.022, 0.577]. Association only.

### dose-084 — phoneme blending (B), base model (association)

B is a 10-item phoneme-blending task (hearing separate sounds and blending them into a word). Adjusts for G, A, own baseline `B_pre`, period-varying. Per +1 SD sessions the association is **+0.29 of 10** (89% CI −0.00 to +0.57; 50% +0.17 to +0.41; P(> 0) = 0.942, **moderate**) — about **+0.09 items per 10 sessions**; the 89% interval just touches zero, so this is the weakest of the five and its magnitude is uncertain. Its period pattern differs from the reading measures — the strongest slope is **period 2**, not period 1: period 1 +0.104 logit [−0.073, +0.275], P = 0.828 (suggestive); period 2 +0.291 [+0.026, +0.643], P = 0.962 (moderate); period 3 +0.037 [−0.224, +0.252], P = 0.597 (inconclusive). Between-period SD 0.229 [0.026, 0.663] — the largest in the family, consistent with a genuinely uneven slope, though still floored at ~zero. Association only.

### dose-177 — word reading (W), ability-adjusted sensitivity variant (association)

A variant of dose-077 (parent `lrp177`, role _alternate_) that adds a **baseline-skill cluster** — baseline letter sounds, expressive vocabulary and phoneme blending (`L_pre`, `E_pre`, `B_pre`) — to the adjustment set as a proxy for general ability, to probe whether the dose-W association is just ability leaking through. It is **not**: the association barely moves, to **+1.28 of 79 words** (89% CI +0.47 to +2.12; 50% +0.93 to +1.63; P(> 0) = 0.994, **very strong**), essentially the same as the base model's +1.16. Front-loading persists (period 1 +0.199 logit [+0.093, +0.316], P = 0.999; period 2 +0.150 [+0.003, +0.294], P = 0.948; period 3 +0.100 [−0.043, +0.223], P = 0.872; between-period SD 0.117 [0.012, 0.460]). Adjusting for observed baseline skills does not explain the dose-W link away — but this rules out only _measured_ ability, not latent general ability, so the collider caveat still stands.

### dose-277 — word reading (W), pooled-slope comparator (association; the one clean-gate fit)

A variant of dose-077 (parent `lrp277`, role _comparator_) with the **same adjustment set (G, A, W_pre)** but a **single pooled dose slope** — no period variation. This is the family's identification anchor: it is the only model that **passes the convergence gate cleanly** (0 divergences), and because it lands squarely on the period-average it shows why the period-varying story is only suggestive. The pooled logit slope is **+0.143 [+0.065, +0.224]**, P(> 0) = 0.998 (**very strong**) — and here the logit interval is comfortably positive because it is one well-determined slope rather than a hyper-mean. In items, **+1.28 of 79 words** per +1 SD (89% CI +0.57 to +2.04; 50% +0.98 to +1.60; P 0.998) — about **+0.42 words per 10 sessions**. This matches the base and ability-adjusted fits almost exactly, so the headline dose-W association is robust to whether the slope is allowed to vary by period.

## What the family concludes

Across all five fits the message is consistent and one-directional: **children who attended more sessions tended to score higher, most markedly on word reading (W) and letter sounds (L), and most strongly in the earliest study period.** For word reading the association is stable at about **+1.2-1.3 of 79 words per +1 SD (~31) sessions** whether the slope is pooled (dose-277), period-varying (dose-077) or additionally ability-adjusted (dose-177) — its robustness to the baseline-skill cluster is the family's most reassuring internal check. The letter-sound and blending associations are weaker and noisier (L strong, B only moderate with an interval touching zero).

Two structural cautions temper this. First, the **period front-loading is suggestive, not established**: for every period-varying model the between-period slope SD has a lower 89% bound essentially at zero, so the data cannot distinguish a genuinely front-loaded slope from a flat one — which is exactly why the pooled comparator (dose-277) fits comparably (LOO elpd −407.3 vs −407.7 for the period-varying base; the ability-adjusted dose-177 is nominally best at −404.4, but these gaps are small and no formal LOO comparison table was written, so read the ranking as "indistinguishable"). Second, and decisively, **none of this is causal.** The pattern triangulates with — and is fully consistent with — the causal ITT word-reading benefit (~+2.4 words), but it cannot add to that causal claim: the ITT τ already answers "does assignment help?", and the dose slope only describes "who, among the treated, progressed more". The mechanism knee-test (`lrp-rli-mech-191`) is the place to look for whether the sessions→W curve _bends_; this family deliberately does not model that.

## Caveats and convergence

- **Nothing causal.** Session dose was not assigned and is a partial collider; every slope is an observational association and remains confounded by latent general ability and by attendance/availability, even in the ability-adjusted dose-177. Read the whole family as "children who did more also scored higher, most markedly early on", never "more sessions cause faster growth".
- **Gate flags.** dose-077 (17), dose-177 (13), dose-083 (4) and dose-084 (2) fail the gate on divergences only, with R-hat, ESS and BFMI all comfortably passing; they are usable with the caveat. dose-277 passes cleanly and is the identification anchor. The recurring divergences reflect the mildly funnel-shaped geometry of the period-varying random-intercept parameterisation and would be the target of any future reparameterisation.
- **Small N and floors.** ~53-54 children (156-160 child-period rows); intervals are wide and point estimates prone to winner's-curse inflation — lead with the interval. Posterior-predictive 50% intervals cover ~69-72% of observations (slightly conservative/over-covered), so the fits are not obviously mis-calibrated.
- **Linearity within period.** Each period's slope is linear in standardised sessions; no nonlinear dose knee/threshold and no dose-by-covariate interaction is estimated here — that question belongs to `lrp-rli-mech-191`.
