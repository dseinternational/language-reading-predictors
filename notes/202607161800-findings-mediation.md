## What these models ask

Given that the intervention raises word reading (from the [ITT](202607161800-findings-itt.md) analysis), _through which skill does that effect travel_? These 16 models (13 single-mediator + 3 two-mediator) use a **g-formula decomposition** by counterfactual simulation — they re-run the fitted model under "what if the mediator behaved as it would with vs without the programme" scenarios and difference the results — to split the **total** effect into:

- **NDE / direct** — the part **not** running through the named mediator (the intervention's effect on reading holding the mediator fixed);
- **NIE / indirect** — the part that **does** run through the mediator (e.g. "the programme taught letter sounds, and letter sounds carried the reading gain").

By construction `total ≈ NDE + NIE`, and **proportion mediated** = NIE / total. The outcome is word reading (W) in 12 of the 16 models; the exceptions are `med-086/186` (off-the-floor nonword reading N) and `med-087/187` (phoneme blending B). Most quantities are on the **items scale** (raw score points); the floored nonword outcome (`med-086/186`) is reported on a **percentage-point (pp) risk-difference scale** — see the note under the table.

`med-078`, `med-186` and `med-187` are **interventional-effects** companions (labelled IIE/IDE) to `med-059`, `med-086` and `med-087` respectively: a slightly different formal interpretation of the same split that is better behaved when mediator-outcome confounders are themselves affected by treatment. Numerically they land on the same estimates as their natural-effects siblings, so we report them alongside.

Reading Bayesian output, in frequentist-friendly terms: a **95% credible interval** is the range the parameter lies in with 95% probability _given the data and the priors_ — a direct probability statement about the parameter, unlike a confidence interval. **P(effect > 0)** ("direction probability" below) is the posterior probability the true effect is positive. We grade the _direction_ probability against the project evidence ladder (#179): `inconclusive` < 0.75 ≤ `suggestive` < 0.91 ≤ `moderate` < 0.97 ≤ `strong` < 0.99 ≤ `very strong`. This family does **not** emit a ROPE ("region of practical equivalence" — a band around zero deemed too small to matter) table, so the "big enough to matter" judgement here rests on the credible interval and the item-scale magnitude rather than a formal ROPE verdict.

**Crucial caveat, stated in every model:** the direct/indirect split is **model-based, not an identified natural effect**. It assumes there is no unmeasured confounder sitting between the mediator and the outcome — a strong, untestable assumption. So NIE/NDE are **best read as a plausible decomposition under assumptions**, not as proven pathways, and no mediator-route coefficient is ever "X drives Y". `med-079` is a deliberate **negative control** (a route through grammar that the causal diagram says should carry ≈0) used to calibrate how much spurious "leakage" residual confounding produces.

## Convergence gate

## The adjusted-association detail — path-specific and direct effects

The single most important row-level fact the headline table hides is that **the direct (NDE) effect is not always positive, and in the correctly time-ordered model it is negative**:

- **Direct effects carry almost the whole total for the routes that should _not_ mediate.** In `med-074` (nonword-decoding route) the direct effect is +2.84 items (0.991, very strong) against an indirect of only +0.02 (0.554, inconclusive) — the nonword route is essentially null, floor-limited, and the reading gain sits in the direct part. The negative control `med-079` behaves the same way: direct +2.07 (0.97, strong), indirect ≈0. The taught-vocabulary routes `med-068`/`med-080` also load onto the direct part (direct +1.60 / +1.60, suggestive) with small uncertain indirect shares (+0.30 / +0.21).
- **`med-076` (L at t2 → reading at t4, correct time-ordering) has a _negative_ direct effect: −1.66 items (P(<0)=0.82, leans negative).** With the mediator measured before the outcome, the indirect L route (+2.91, 0.997) actually exceeds the total (+1.40) — i.e. under this decomposition essentially all — and then some — of the reading gain is attributed to the letter-sound pathway. This is the strongest single-model statement of the "gain travels through the code" reading, though it remains an association, not proof. (After the 2026-07-17 confounder reconciliation the direct effect is more negative and the total weaker than before, so the "all via L" pattern is if anything sharper.)
- **In the two-mediator models the joint indirect is carried entirely by letter sounds; the blending path-specific effect is slightly _negative_.** `med-064` (L vs expressive vocab): NIE_L +1.89 (+0.39, +4.10), 0.996 → very strong, vs NIE_E +0.03 (−0.69, +1.02), 0.58 → inconclusive — L dominates E decisively. `med-066` (L vs blending, parallel): NIE_L +1.62 (+0.31, +3.59), 0.995 → very strong, but **NIE_B is −0.03 (−0.84, +0.59), P(>0)=0.42 → inconclusive**. `med-075` (sequential L → B → reading): NIE_L +1.62 (+0.33, +3.63), 0.996, and again **NIE_B −0.03 (−0.88, +0.62), 0.42**. So although the _joint_ code-route indirect is clearly positive in both, that positive share is entirely the letter-sound path — the blending-specific contribution is small and if anything slightly negative. Read the earlier draft's "code-route mediators jointly carry a positive share" / "consistent with a decoding chain" with that in mind: do not over-credit blending.

## Sensitivity to unmeasured mediator-outcome confounding

Because the split is only valid under no-unmeasured-confounding, each model reports a **tipping point**: the fraction of the mediator → outcome path that would have to be pure confounding (rather than a real mediator effect) to erase the indirect effect. Smaller fractions = more fragile. For the clearly-positive-NIE models: `med-059` **0.375**, `med-086` **0.45**, `med-076` **0.45**, `med-092` **0.498**. In every case `robust_over_full_sweep = False` — i.e. the NIEs are only **moderately** robust: roughly 38–50% of the mediator-outcome association would need to be confounding to null them, and the full sweep does breach that. So the letter-sound indirect route is a **repeated, direction-stable association**, not an effect that survives arbitrarily large hidden confounding.

**Instrumental-strength (IS) calibration caveat:** the g-formula cross-check against the companion cumulative-session dose model is **not available for `med-059`/`med-086` (and their IIE companions)** because that dose model, `lrp-rli-dose-083`, failed its own convergence gate in this refit. The decompositions still stand on their own priors and data, but this external calibration leg is missing for the two headline letter-sound models.

## The negative control (calibration check)

`med-079` routes the reading gain through **receptive grammar (T)** — a path the causal diagram says should carry ≈0. It does: NIE **+0.08 items (−0.30, +0.81), P(>0)=0.712 → inconclusive**, with the direct effect (+2.07, strong) carrying essentially the whole total. This is the reassuring result: the machinery is **not** manufacturing large spurious indirect effects out of residual confounding. It gives us some licence to take the clearly-positive letter-sound NIEs more seriously than we otherwise could — though, again, "more seriously" is not "proven".

## The one-paragraph story

The word-reading benefit is **most consistently associated with movement through letter-sound knowledge**: the indirect route through L is clearly positive in several independent framings (`med-059` +1.68 items, 0.997; the correctly time-ordered `med-076` L-at-t2 → reading-at-t4 +2.91, 0.997; the period-stacked `med-092` +0.75 all-period / +0.61 period-1, 0.998), and the same holds for the off-the-floor **nonword** gain (`med-086` +8.66 pp via L, 0.993). Routes through **vocabulary** (taught or standardised, receptive or expressive) carry only small, uncertain shares (`med-064` expressive NIE_E +0.03; `med-068` +0.30; `med-080` +0.21). In the two-mediator code-route models the positive joint indirect is carried **entirely by letter sounds** — the blending path-specific share is small and slightly negative. The **negative-control** route through grammar (`med-079`) correctly reads ≈0. Taken together this is consistent with — but does **not prove** — a picture in which the programme's reading benefit travels through the code (letter sounds → decoding) rather than by broadly lifting vocabulary.

## What is causal

The **total** intervention contrast inherits the randomised ITT logic for the single-outcome and two-mediator models — with one exception: **`med-092`'s exposure is per-period on-intervention under an ignorability assumption, not randomisation** (it stacks periods in the gain-factor scaffold), so its total is a weaker, adjusted quantity than a true ITT effect; the period-1 transition readout (+2.50) is the defensible causal-flavoured number there, not the all-period +3.03. The **direct/indirect split is never identified** — it is a decomposition under a no-unmeasured-mediator-outcome-confounding assumption. So read every NIE/NDE (and IIE/IDE) as "under this model", check it against the negative control (`med-079` ≈ 0) and the tipping-point sweeps (0.375–0.498, none robust over the full sweep), and never state a mediator route as an established mechanism. Positive total = intervention benefit; a positive NIE means "under the model, this much of that benefit is attributable to the mediator" — an adjusted decomposition, not a proven pathway.
