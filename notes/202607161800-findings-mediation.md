## What these models ask

Given that the intervention raises word reading (from the [ITT](202607161800-findings-itt.md) analysis), _through which skill does that effect travel_? These 18 models (15 single-mediator + 3 two-mediator; the 18th, `med-276`, is a t3-outcome ceiling-sensitivity companion to the reverse-direction `med-176`) use a **g-formula decomposition** by counterfactual simulation ŌĆö they re-run the fitted model under "what if the mediator behaved as it would with vs without the programme" scenarios and difference the results ŌĆö to split the **total** effect into:

- **NDE / direct** ŌĆö the part **not** running through the named mediator (the intervention's effect on reading holding the mediator fixed);
- **NIE / indirect** ŌĆö the part that **does** run through the mediator (e.g. "the programme taught letter sounds, and letter sounds carried the reading gain").

By construction `total Ōēł NDE + NIE`, and **proportion mediated** = NIE / total. The outcome is word reading (W) in 12 of the 16 models; the exceptions are `med-086/186` (off-the-floor nonword reading N) and `med-087/187` (phoneme blending B). Most quantities are on the **items scale** (raw score points); the floored nonword outcome (`med-086/186`) is reported on a **percentage-point (pp) risk-difference scale** ŌĆö see the note under the table.

`med-078`, `med-186` and `med-187` are **interventional-effects** companions (labelled IIE/IDE) to `med-059`, `med-086` and `med-087` respectively: a slightly different formal interpretation of the same split that is better behaved when mediator-outcome confounders are themselves affected by treatment. Numerically they land on the same estimates as their natural-effects siblings, so we report them alongside.

Reading Bayesian output, in frequentist-friendly terms: a **95% credible interval** is the range the parameter lies in with 95% probability _given the data and the priors_ ŌĆö a direct probability statement about the parameter, unlike a confidence interval. **P(effect > 0)** ("direction probability" below) is the posterior probability the true effect is positive. We grade the _direction_ probability against the project evidence ladder (#179): `inconclusive` < 0.75 Ōēż `suggestive` < 0.91 Ōēż `moderate` < 0.97 Ōēż `strong` < 0.99 Ōēż `very strong`. This family does **not** emit a ROPE ("region of practical equivalence" ŌĆö a band around zero deemed too small to matter) table, so the "big enough to matter" judgement here rests on the credible interval and the item-scale magnitude rather than a formal ROPE verdict.

**Crucial caveat, stated in every model:** the direct/indirect split is **model-based, not an identified natural effect**. It assumes there is no unmeasured confounder sitting between the mediator and the outcome ŌĆö a strong, untestable assumption. So NIE/NDE are **best read as a plausible decomposition under assumptions**, not as proven pathways, and no mediator-route coefficient is ever "X drives Y". `med-079` is a deliberate **negative control** (a route through grammar that the causal diagram says should carry Ōēł0) used to calibrate how much spurious "leakage" residual confounding produces.

## The adjusted-association detail ŌĆö path-specific and direct effects

The single most important row-level fact the headline table hides is that **the direct (NDE) effect is not always positive, and in the correctly time-ordered model it is negative**:

- **Direct effects carry almost the whole total for the routes that should _not_ mediate.** In `med-074` (nonword-decoding route) the direct effect is +2.84 items (0.991, very strong) against an indirect of only +0.02 (0.554, inconclusive) ŌĆö the nonword route is essentially null, floor-limited, and the reading gain sits in the direct part. The negative control `med-079` behaves the same way: direct +2.07 (0.97, strong), indirect Ōēł0. The taught-vocabulary routes `med-068`/`med-080` also load onto the direct part (direct +1.60 / +1.60, suggestive) with small uncertain indirect shares (+0.30 / +0.21).
- **`med-076` (L at t2 ŌåÆ reading at t4, correct time-ordering) has a _negative_ direct effect: ŌłÆ1.66 items (P(<0)=0.82, leans negative).** With the mediator measured before the outcome, the indirect L route (+2.91, 0.997) actually exceeds the total (+1.40) ŌĆö i.e. under this decomposition essentially all ŌĆö and then some ŌĆö of the reading gain is attributed to the letter-sound pathway. This is the strongest single-model statement of the "gain travels through the code" reading, though it remains an association, not proof. (After the 2026-07-17 confounder reconciliation the direct effect is more negative and the total weaker than before, so the "all via L" pattern is if anything sharper.)
- **In the two-mediator models the joint indirect is carried entirely by letter sounds; the blending path-specific effect is slightly _negative_.** `med-064` (L vs expressive vocab): NIE_L +1.89 (+0.39, +4.10), 0.996 ŌåÆ very strong, vs NIE_E +0.03 (ŌłÆ0.69, +1.02), 0.58 ŌåÆ inconclusive ŌĆö L dominates E decisively. `med-066` (L vs blending, parallel): NIE_L +1.62 (+0.31, +3.59), 0.995 ŌåÆ very strong, but **NIE_B is ŌłÆ0.03 (ŌłÆ0.84, +0.59), P(>0)=0.42 ŌåÆ inconclusive**. `med-075` (sequential L ŌåÆ B ŌåÆ reading): NIE_L +1.62 (+0.33, +3.63), 0.996, and again **NIE_B ŌłÆ0.03 (ŌłÆ0.88, +0.62), 0.42**. So although the _joint_ code-route indirect is clearly positive in both, that positive share is entirely the letter-sound path ŌĆö the blending-specific contribution is small and if anything slightly negative. Read the earlier draft's "code-route mediators jointly carry a positive share" / "consistent with a decoding chain" with that in mind: do not over-credit blending.

## Direction contrast: LS ŌåÆ WR vs WR ŌåÆ LS (`med-076` vs `med-176`)

Every model above (and the whole suite) otherwise _assumes_ the direction letter sounds ŌåÆ word reading. `med-176` is the deliberate mirror of the forward longitudinal model `med-076`, built to put the reverse under a fair test: it reuses the same two-wave machinery but swaps the roles ŌĆö **mediator = word reading at t2, outcome = letter sounds at t4** ŌĆö and it exercises a `WR_t ŌåÆ LS_t1` edge added to the lagged DAG on 2026-07-17 specifically to permit the reverse (the graph previously forbade it). Full rationale and caveats: `notes/202607172100-reverse-mediation-wr-ls-direction-spec.md`.

Read the two together (mind that the NIEs are on **different scales** ŌĆö forward on the 79-item word-reading count, reverse on the 32-item letter-sound count ŌĆö so compare the _proportion mediated_ and the direction evidence, not the raw items):

- **Forward, `med-076` (LS ŌåÆ WR):** the indirect route carries **essentially the whole** reading gain ŌĆö NIE via letter sounds **+2.91 (0.997, very strong)**, with the direct effect actually _negative_ (ŌłÆ1.66) so the proportion mediated exceeds 1. Letter sounds is the dominant carrier.
- **Reverse, `med-176` (WR ŌåÆ LS):** there _is_ a credibly-positive reverse indirect ŌĆö NIE **+0.45 (0.963, moderate)** ŌĆö but it is **minor**: most of the letter-sound gain is direct (NDE +1.21), only **Ōēł 23%** runs back through word reading, and the NIE's 95 % interval still grazes zero (ŌłÆ0.04, +1.30), so no tipping-point could even be computed (not credibly non-zero at the 95 % level).

So the contrast is a **mild reciprocal with strong forward dominance**: both cross-lagged indirect routes point positive, but `LS ŌåÆ WR` carries the entire reading gain and is very strong, while `WR ŌåÆ LS` carries only about a quarter of the letter-sound gain and is moderate-and-fragile. This supports letter sounds ŌåÆ word reading as the **primary** pathway without ruling out a small feedback the other way.

Three caveats keep this from being a directional _proof_ (all in the spec note): (1) **both** decompositions are ID-2 / `GA`-confounded, and a positive reverse cross-lag is exactly what shared general ability manufactures (a higher-ability child gains in both), so the +0.45 is very plausibly ability-leakage rather than a genuine `WR ŌåÆ LS` effect ŌĆö its fragility (CrI includes 0) is consistent with that; (2) the design randomises an early letter-sound shock but **no** early word-reading shock (reading moves later), so the reverse direction is intrinsically **less well-powered** ŌĆö a small reverse NIE is not strong evidence _against_ reverse causation; (3) the reverse outcome (letter sounds at t4) is mildly ceilinged (~12 % of children at 32), which if anything _attenuates_ the reverse NIE. That ceiling concern is **checked and cleared** by the t3-outcome sensitivity `med-276` (letter sounds at t3, only ~6 % ceilinged): its reverse NIE is **+0.50 (95 % ŌłÆ0.03 to +1.43, 0.966, moderate)** ŌĆö essentially identical to the t4 value, so the small reverse signal is _stable_ across waves rather than a ceiling artefact, and it stays equally fragile (95 % interval grazes zero at both t3 and t4). Net: the reverse test **qualifies** the assumed direction ("primarily LS ŌåÆ WR, with at most a small and unproven WR ŌåÆ LS feedback"), it does not overturn it.

**Why not a proper cross-lagged model?** The principled successor here is a random-intercept cross-lagged panel model (RI-CLPM), which would net out the stable between-child ability the mediation reads cannot. It was planned and its feasibility pre-tested by simulation ([`202607172230-riclpm-direction-plan.md`](202607172230-riclpm-direction-plan.md)): the model is well-behaved (clean sampling, variances recovered, honest coverage) but has **~zero power** to resolve the within-person direction contrast at n Ōēł 54 ├Ś 4 waves ŌĆö across 60 simulated datasets with a true forward-dominant effect, the 90 % interval for the direction difference **never** cleared zero. So the within-person estimator is not run on real data (there is no better number to be had at this n), and the direction question rests on the mediation reads above.

## Sensitivity to unmeasured mediator-outcome confounding

Because the split is only valid under no-unmeasured-confounding, each model reports a **tipping point**: the fraction of the mediator ŌåÆ outcome path that would have to be pure confounding (rather than a real mediator effect) to erase the indirect effect. Smaller fractions = more fragile. For the clearly-positive-NIE models: `med-059` **0.375**, `med-086` **0.45**, `med-076` **0.45**, `med-092` **0.498**. In every case `robust_over_full_sweep = False` ŌĆö i.e. the NIEs are only **moderately** robust: roughly 38ŌĆō50% of the mediator-outcome association would need to be confounding to null them, and the full sweep does breach that. So the letter-sound indirect route is a **repeated, direction-stable association**, not an effect that survives arbitrarily large hidden confounding.

**Instrumental-strength (IS) calibration caveat:** the g-formula cross-check against the companion cumulative-session dose model is **not available for `med-059`/`med-086` (and their IIE companions)** because that dose model, `lrp-rli-dose-083`, failed its own convergence gate in this refit. The decompositions still stand on their own priors and data, but this external calibration leg is missing for the two headline letter-sound models.

## The one-paragraph story

The word-reading benefit is **most consistently associated with movement through letter-sound knowledge**: the indirect route through L is clearly positive in several independent framings (`med-059` +1.68 items, 0.997; the correctly time-ordered `med-076` L-at-t2 ŌåÆ reading-at-t4 +2.91, 0.997; the period-stacked `med-092` +0.75 all-period / +0.61 period-1, 0.998), and the same holds for the off-the-floor **nonword** gain (`med-086` +8.66 pp via L, 0.993). Routes through **vocabulary** (taught or standardised, receptive or expressive) carry only small, uncertain shares (`med-064` expressive NIE_E +0.03; `med-068` +0.30; `med-080` +0.21). In the two-mediator code-route models the positive joint indirect is carried **entirely by letter sounds** ŌĆö the blending path-specific share is small and slightly negative. The **negative-control** route through grammar (`med-079`) correctly reads Ōēł0. Taken together this is consistent with ŌĆö but does **not prove** ŌĆö a picture in which the programme's reading benefit travels through the code (letter sounds ŌåÆ decoding) rather than by broadly lifting vocabulary.

## What is causal

The **total** intervention contrast inherits the randomised ITT logic for the single-outcome and two-mediator models ŌĆö with one exception: **`med-092`'s exposure is per-period on-intervention under an ignorability assumption, not randomisation** (it stacks periods in the gain-factor scaffold), so its total is a weaker, adjusted quantity than a true ITT effect; the period-1 transition readout (+2.50) is the defensible causal-flavoured number there, not the all-period +3.03. The **direct/indirect split is never identified** ŌĆö it is a decomposition under a no-unmeasured-mediator-outcome-confounding assumption. So read every NIE/NDE (and IIE/IDE) as "under this model", check it against the negative control (`med-079` Ōēł 0) and the tipping-point sweeps (0.375ŌĆō0.498, none robust over the full sweep), and never state a mediator route as an established mechanism. Positive total = intervention benefit; a positive NIE means "under the model, this much of that benefit is attributable to the mediator" ŌĆö an adjusted decomposition, not a proven pathway.
