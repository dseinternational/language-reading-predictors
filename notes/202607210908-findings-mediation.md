This is one of a set of per-family notes from the full 2026-07-21 re-fit of every Bayesian statistical model in the study (production `reporting` configuration, 6 chains × 6000 draws, 89% credible intervals; all data preliminary). It covers the **mediation family** — 18 models that ask _through which intermediate skill_ the reading/phonics intervention's effect travels. Read the shared index and reading guide `notes/202607210900-findings-00-index-and-reading-guide.md` first for the study background, the outcome measures and their maxima, and how to read Bayesian numbers. **All 18 models in this family pass the convergence gate.**

## What this family probes

The study is a **waitlist-crossover randomised trial**: about 54 children with Down syndrome were randomly assigned to receive a reading/phonics intervention immediately or after a wait, and were tested at four timepoints. The intention-to-treat (ITT) family answers _whether_ assignment moved a skill. This family answers a different question: **of the intervention's total effect on an outcome, how much runs _through_ a specific intermediate skill (the "mediator"), and how much does not?** The motivating hypothesis is that the intervention's word-reading gain is carried by the letter-sound (decoding) route rather than by a vocabulary route.

Each model splits a total effect into two parts using the **g-formula** — a way of estimating what would have happened under hypothetical ("counterfactual") scenarios by simulating from the fitted model rather than multiplying coefficients together. The two parts are a **natural direct effect (NDE)** — the share that does _not_ pass through the mediator — and a **natural indirect effect (NIE)** — the share that _does_. By construction Total = NDE + NIE. Three models (078, 186, 187) are **interventional** companions that relabel the split IDE/IIE (interventional direct/indirect effect); they target a slightly weaker-assumption version of the same partition. Three others (064, 066, 075) carry **two** mediators at once and report a **joint** indirect effect (NIE_joint) across both routes.

Every model conditions on a DAG-pre-specified adjustment set — the covariates fixed in advance by the causal diagram, not chosen from the data. Across the family these include general ability (GA) or being-on-programme (the on-programme indicator, for the period-stacked scaffold), age (A), the relevant baseline skill levels (the child's own pre-scores and other upstream skills such as receptive/expressive vocabulary RV and EV), hearing (HS), and speech/phonology measures (the `deapp` and `erbto` terms), each with its missingness indicator. The precise set differs per model and is listed in that model's `config.json`.

## How to read these numbers

Full conventions are in the reading guide; in brief, the point estimate is the posterior **median**, uncertainty is the **89% equal-tailed credible interval** ("89% posterior probability the value lies in this range"), and direction is read from the **tail probability** P(effect > 0), never from whether an interval excludes zero and never as a p-value. The evidence ladder attached to a tail probability is fixed by round odds: _inconclusive_ (< 0.75), _suggestive_ (≥ 0.75), _moderate_ (≥ 0.91), _strong_ (≥ 0.97), _very strong_ (≥ 0.99), and it grades the _evidence for a direction_, never the _size_. Effects are modelled on a probability (proportion-correct) scale and translated into **items** by multiplying by the test maximum (word reading WR out of 79, letter sounds LS out of 32, blending PA out of 10); the heavily-floored nonword outcome NW is reported as an off-floor **risk difference in percentage points (pp)** — the change in the chance of coming off the floor — rather than an item count. With ~54 children an estimate that just clears a threshold is on average inflated, so lead with the interval, not the point.

**The framing rule that matters most here: none of the total, direct or indirect quantities is a causal lever.** The **total** contrast is the model's estimate of the overall treatment→outcome effect, and it lines up with the randomised ITT τ, so it is the one quantity comparable to a causal effect — this note reports its tail probability and evidence label. But the _split_ of that total into direct and indirect parts is an **association-based decomposition**. It rests on a _sequential-ignorability / no-unmeasured mediator–outcome confounding_ assumption that is not testable in this study: latent general ability plausibly drives both a mediator and the reading outcome, and intervention dose is a treatment-induced confounder of the mediator–outcome path. So read every NIE as _"how much of the outcome's movement is statistically **accounted for** by the route through mediator X"_, not _"X drives Y"_ — the Table-2 fallacy in its sharpest form, applying to the direct part too. Because this is a g-formula family, there is **no PSIS-LOO** cross-validation for these models. The fit-time key findings report the tail probability and evidence label for the **total**; for the NIE they give the median and 89% interval, so below the NIE is read directly from that interval (whether it lies entirely above zero or spans zero) rather than from a separate label.

### The letter-sound route into word reading (the positive result)

The clearest finding of the family is that the word-reading gain is **statistically accounted for through letter-sound knowledge (LS)** — the count of letter–sound correspondences a child knows, out of 32. **med-059** (concurrent LS → WR) puts the indirect part at NIE = +1.7 of 79 words with an 89% credible range of +0.6 to +3.2 — an interval lying **entirely above zero** — against a total of +1.9 words that is itself only moderately positive (P = 0.93). Because the NIE is comparable in size to the total, the letter-sound route accounts for most of the movement, with the remainder (the not-through-LS direct part) making up the small balance. **med-078** is the interventional-effects companion and returns identical numbers (IIE = +1.7 words); the match is an implementation check on the two estimands, not extra evidence.

**med-076** enforces time order — letter sounds measured at t2, word reading at t4 — and gives a _larger_ indirect part, NIE = +2.9 words (89% +1.1 to +5.3, entirely above zero), while the total is only +1.4 words and _inconclusive_ (P = 0.74). Read plainly, the later word-reading advantage that co-travels with assignment is more than accounted for by the earlier letter-sound advantage, with the residual direct channel evidently pulling the other way so that the two nearly cancel in the total — a signature to treat cautiously at this sample size but wholly consistent with a decoding route. **med-092** is the period-stacked "gain-factor scaffold" (157 rows from 53 children, keyed to being-on-programme rather than assignment): a strongly positive total of +3.0 words (P = 0.99) with a smaller but clearly positive letter-sound indirect part, NIE = +0.8 words (89% +0.3 to +1.4). Different scaffold, smaller indirect share, same direction.

### The vocabulary and grammar routes into word reading (the near-zero contrast)

Set against the letter-sound route, the vocabulary and grammar routes carry **little or nothing**, even though the totals remain positive. **med-068** (through taught-expressive vocabulary TE) has a total of +2.0 words (P = 0.94, moderate) but an indirect part of only NIE = +0.3 words whose 89% range (−0.2 to +1.3) **spans zero**. **med-080** (through taught-receptive vocabulary TR) is similar: total +1.9 words (P = 0.92) with NIE = +0.2 words (89% −0.3 to +1.2, spanning zero). **med-079** is a deliberate **negative control**: receptive grammar (RG) sits on a DAG-severed path, so its indirect part should be ≈ 0 — and it is, NIE = +0.1 words (89% −0.2 to +0.6), even though the total is strongly positive (+2.2 words, P = 0.97). This calibrates how much apparent mediation residual confounding can manufacture here: very little. **med-074** routes through nonword decoding (NW), itself floor-limited: the total is strong (+2.9 words, P = 0.99) but the indirect part is essentially nil, NIE = +0.0 words (89% −0.4 to +0.5). In each of these, the direct (not-through-mediator) part carries the whole positive total.

### Downstream: letter sounds into other decoding skills, and the code composite

Letter-sound knowledge also statistically accounts for movement in the other code-related outcomes. **med-086** (LS → nonword reading NW, an off-floor risk difference because most children score zero) gives NIE = **+8.7 percentage points** in the chance of coming off the floor (89% +2.8 to +16.0, entirely above zero), against a total of +7.9 pp that is itself only suggestive (P = 0.88). **med-186** is its interventional companion and matches it exactly (IIE = +8.7 pp). **med-087** (LS → phoneme blending PA, out of 10) gives NIE = +0.2 of 10 (89% −0.0 to +0.6, the lower limit just touching zero) with a total of +0.7 (P = 0.93); **med-187** is its interventional twin (IIE = +0.2). **med-062** bundles the two code skills into a single **code-route composite** mediator (letter sounds + blending) for word reading and tells the same story with the skills combined: NIE = +0.9 words (89% +0.1 to +2.2, above zero) against a suggestive total of +1.6 words (P = 0.89).

### Reverse-direction checks (does word reading carry the letter-sound gain?)

Mediation cannot by itself fix the _direction_ of a mediator→outcome link, so two models run the arrow the other way. **med-176** (word reading at t2 → letter sounds at t4) finds a non-trivial reverse route: NIE = +0.5 of 32 letter sounds (89% +0.0 to +1.1) against a total of +1.7 (P = 0.92, moderate). **med-276** repeats it with the outcome at t3 (less ceilinged) and points the same way: NIE = +0.5 of 32 (89% +0.0 to +1.2) against a smaller, suggestive total of +1.0 (P = 0.77). So word reading _also_ statistically accounts for part of the letter-sound gain. This bidirectionality is what one expects when two skills co-develop and share latent ability, and it is exactly why the family stops short of a directional causal claim: the forward route (LS → WR) is the larger and better-supported story in item terms, but the reverse route is not negligible.

### Two mediators head-to-head

The single-mediator contrast is echoed _within_ one model, which is the cleaner comparison because both mediators compete for the same variance. **med-064** carries letter sounds (LS) and expressive vocabulary (EV) together and gives a joint indirect effect NIE_joint = +2.0 words (89% +0.6 to +3.9, entirely above zero) — essentially the whole of the +2.0-word total (P = 0.91). **med-066** pits LS against phoneme blending (PA): NIE_joint = +1.6 words (89% +0.4 to +3.1) against a total of +1.8 (P = 0.90). **med-075** is the sequential-code variant (letter sounds → blending → reading) and reproduces med-066 almost exactly: NIE_joint = +1.6 words (89% +0.4 to +3.1), total +1.7 (P = 0.89). Across all three, the joint indirect effect is close to the full total, consistent with the single-mediator finding that the letter-sound leg does most of the work; the per-route split that would confirm which leg carries it is in the model CSVs but not quoted in this note.

## What to take away

Read as association, the family speaks with one voice: **whatever moves word reading in this cohort moves with letter-sound knowledge, not with vocabulary.** The letter-sound route into word reading is sized at roughly +1.7 to +2.9 of 79 words across the concurrent, longitudinal and period-stacked specifications, with 89% intervals clear of zero; the vocabulary routes (taught-expressive, taught-receptive) and the grammar negative control all have indirect parts indistinguishable from zero, and the two-mediator models show the joint indirect effect sitting close to the full total. Letter sounds likewise account for the off-floor nonword-reading movement (+8.7 pp) and some of the blending gain. Two reverse-direction checks show word reading also accounts for part of the letter-sound gain, so the split is best read as _shared movement_ between co-developing skills.

The g-formula **totals** (roughly +1.4 to +3.0 words) sit in the same range as the randomised ITT word-reading effect, so the decomposition is anchored to a real, randomised benefit — but the _partition_ of that total into direct and indirect parts is associational, resting on an untestable no-unmeasured-confounding assumption, with residual latent general-ability confounding that cannot be removed and no cross-validation available for this family. All 18 models cleared the convergence gate. Preliminary study, small sample: lead with the intervals, and treat the mediated routes as descriptions of _which skills move together_, not as proof of mechanism.
