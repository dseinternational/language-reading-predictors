## Unexpected: LKJ drop did not eliminate LRP55 divergences

After dropping the LKJ residual I re-ran LRP55 in reporting config
expecting divergences to fall from 7.9 % (with residual) toward the
sub-percent range. Instead:

| LRP55 variant | Divergences | τ (W) | τ (L) | ELPD | p_loo |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| LKJ residual *on* | 2836 / 36000 (7.9 %) | −0.432 | −0.668 | −1165.8 | 101.6 |
| **LKJ residual off (new default)** | **3028 / 36000 (8.4 %)** | **−0.426** | **−0.667** | **−1167.3** | **76.4** |

- τ posteriors and γ_own are unchanged within MC error (as expected —
  the LKJ block was prior-dominated and not carrying signal).
- p_loo drops from 102 to 76 — consistent with removing the
  per-(obs × outcome) random effects `u` — confirming the removal
  took.
- **Divergences did not drop.** The source of the funnel is *not*
  the LKJ block as I had hypothesised, but the **nine HSGP amplitudes**
  (shared `mu_A` plus eight outcome-specific `delta_A_k`). Each
  amplitude's posterior can touch zero, creating the same η × basis-
  weights funnel we identified in LRP52.

### What next for LRP55 divergences

Three options, in order of preference:

1. **Drop the age GP from LRP55** (add a `use_age_gp` flag to
   `build_joint_model`). The LRP52 sensitivity showed the HSGP
   contributes essentially nothing to out-of-sample prediction on
   bounded-count outcomes at this sample size; there is no reason to
   expect it to carry signal in LRP55 either.
2. Tighten the age-GP amplitude prior further (e.g. `HalfNormal(0.1)`
   for the joint-model `mu_A` and `delta_A_k`). Would halve the
   funnel neck but not eliminate it.
3. Accept the 8 % divergence rate and caveat the report. The τ
   posteriors are faithful (matched by the clean univariate LRP52/53/54
   fits); divergent transitions in the GP tail do not bias the
   substantive estimand.

Recommendation: option 1, as a follow-up change. τ inference is
unaffected either way; the argument is purely honesty and diagnostic
cleanliness. Not done in this change-set because the user's
instruction was specifically to drop the LKJ residual; I would not
make a second default flip without confirmation.

## Mechanism-forest fix — what changed in the numbers

Previous version used `np.linspace(-2, 2)` as a fake x-grid, which
made the per-draw `np.gradient` slopes numerically degenerate (the
fake grid didn't match the posterior `f_mech` support at all). Fixed
version pulls `logit_safe(mech_post, N)` per model from the same
prepared data the factory used.

Numerical results from the fixed forest (reporting config):

| Model | slope mean | 95 % CI |
|---|---|---|
| LRP56 (R → W) | +0.112 | (−0.044, +0.377) |
| LRP57 (E → W) | +0.196 | (−0.013, +0.501) |
| LRP58 (L → W) | +0.137 | (−0.002, +0.304) |

All three slopes are positive. **LRP58's lower bound touches zero at
−0.002**, consistent with the earlier finding that its `f_mech__eta`
amplitude 95 % CI excludes zero at the lower bound (0.001). LRP57 has
the largest point estimate but the widest CI. LRP56 is essentially
centred on zero with a long positive tail.

These slopes are *average* slopes over the observed posterior grid,
not slopes at a single anchor point; they confirm that the mechanism
curves are predominantly positive across their support but do not
rule out local negative stretches.
