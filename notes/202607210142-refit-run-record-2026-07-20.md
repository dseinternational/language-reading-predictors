Run record for a complete re-fit of every registered Bayesian (PyMC) statistical model at the production `reporting` sampling configuration, with per-model Quarto report renders. For per-family interpretation and how to read the numbers, the reference remains the per-family findings notes and the index/reading guide, `notes/202607210900-findings-00-index-and-reading-guide.md`; this note records _the run_ (what was fit, what converged, where the artefacts are), not a re-derivation of the findings.

## What was run

- **Command**: `scripts/fit_statistical_model.py <id> --config reporting --render`, driven per-model (not `all`) by a resumable driver that writes a `status/<id>.done` marker per model and skips completed ones — deliberately, because a monolithic backgrounded `all --config reporting` sweep has been reaped mid-run before. Per-model `--render` also renders each report immediately after its own fit, so an interruption never leaves a fitted-but-un-rendered backlog.
- **Sampling preset** (`reporting`): 6 chains × 6000 draws × 6000 tune, `target_accept = 0.95` (NUTS via `nutpie`). 36 000 posterior draws per model. Credible intervals reported at the house-standard **89%** equal-tailed (`ci_prob = 0.89`).
- **Scope**: **179 registered models** across 16 families (the authoritative count from the live `MODELS` registry; earlier docs saying 89/159 are stale). Every registered model was fit — no `--include-variants`-style filtering; the driver iterated the full sorted registry.
- **Cross-model comparison**: `scripts/compare_statistical_models.py --config reporting` run after the sweep.
- **Wall time**: ≈ 7 h 18 m (2026-07-20 18:20 → 2026-07-21 01:38), sequential on 16 cores. Slowest fits are the mediation g-formula family (e.g. `lrp-rli-med-059` ≈ 10.6 min) and the historical/factor families; typical ITT fits ≈ 20–40 s.

## Outcome

- **179 / 179 fit successfully** (every `fit_exit = 0`) and **179 / 179 rendered** (`index.html` present in every `-reporting` output dir). Zero fit crashes, zero render failures.
- **Convergence gate: 162 pass / 17 review.** The 17 that miss the gate split cleanly into two groups:

### Group A — divergence-only, within tolerance (13 models, usable-with-a-note)

`did-007`, `dose-077`, `dose-083`, `dose-084`, `dose-177`, `hs-001`, `mech-095`, `mech-156`, `mech-157`, `mech-188`, `mech-189`, `mech-190`, `mech-191`.

Each passes r̂ (all ≤ 1.003), ESS (thousands), and BFMI, and fails **only** the zero-divergence check. Divergence counts run 1–31 out of 36 000 draws — a maximum of **0.086%** (`mech-190`), comfortably inside the METHODS ≤ 1% guidance. These are the expected divergence-prone geometries: dose-slope partial-collider funnels, HSGP mechanism surfaces, and the horseshoe funnel. Treat as usable; note the divergences. The comparison script correctly excludes `dose-077` and `did-007` from the nested-LOO tables on gate-review grounds.

### Group PA — genuine concern, the measurement / correlated-factor family (4 models)

`mm-001`, `mm-002`, `mm-101`, `rlm-mm-001`.

These fail **r̂ and ESS** (r̂ up to 1.048 for `mm-002`, 1.028 for `rlm-mm-001`), the latent-factor funnel the corr_factor family is known for. Per the established guidance, their **domain correlations are the robust, reportable quantity**; the **structural coefficients should be held** pending a non-centred reparameterisation or higher `target_accept`. Unchanged in character from prior runs — not a regression introduced here.

**Triangulation** (`comparison/triangulation_consistency.csv`): WR, LS, PA, TR, TE, EV, LF are direction-consistent and interval-overlapping across all three designs (ITT / DiD / gain-factor ANCOVA), all three converged. RV is the only outcome flagged direction-inconsistent — but every design puts it at ≈ 0 (ITT +0.008, DiD −0.002, gf −0.078), i.e. a consistent near-null rather than a real disagreement.
