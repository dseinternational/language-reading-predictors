# Future directions for exploring causal and associative predictors of progress

Date: 2026-07-09. A discussion note collecting candidate directions for extending the study's exploration of predictors of progress in language and reading skills, developed after the 2026-07-08 full reporting fit (89/89 models; `notes/202607082140-statistical-models-full-reporting-fit.md`). It is deliberately broader than the suite gap-analysis umbrella (issue #228, which covers coverage asymmetries and unused variables) and the skill-to-skill mediation question (issue #229): the focus here is on _estimands and identification strategies the suite does not yet use at all_, plus two questions the education team raised directly — characterising non-responders, and children who remain at or around floor on phonics/nonword measures at the final timepoint. Everything here is a proposal for consideration, not a committed analysis; numbers quoted are from the reporting-fit output CSVs and `data/rli_data_long.csv` (54 children × 4 timepoints).

All the usual caveats apply and are load-bearing at this sample size: only the randomised terms (ITT τ, DiD δ, gain-factor on-intervention marginal) are causal; everything else is an adjusted association; credible intervals are wide; and a "no reliable pattern" answer is a legitimate, publishable result, not a failure. See `METHODS.md`.

## 1. Causal machinery the suite does not yet use

- **CACE / principal stratification on dose.** The LRP-RLI-ITT suite design note (`notes/202606251321-lrpitt-suite-design.md`) explicitly _deferred_ a complier-average / instrumental-variable dose analysis, and no issue currently tracks it. Every existing dose-response model is flagged "sessions-received is a DAG collider, sensitivity view only." Using randomisation as an instrument for sessions-received yields the one _causal_ dose estimand available — the complier average causal effect — under stated monotonicity and exclusion-restriction assumptions. Wide at n≈54, but it upgrades "more sessions track more gain" from an association to a bounded causal claim.
- **A negative-control outcome.** Cheap and high value: run the ITT machinery on an outcome the intervention should _not_ move (block design / non-verbal ability, `blocks`). A null validates the pipeline against testing effects and residual bias; a non-null is an alarm worth hearing. The suite currently has no falsification target — untaught vocabulary only partially plays this role because generalisation there is substantively plausible.
- **Quantified sensitivity for the mediation no-unmeasured-confounding assumption.** LRP-RLI-MED-059/062/064 _state_ "no unmeasured mediator–outcome confounding" as the binding assumption but nothing quantifies it. A sensitivity parameter (residual mediator–outcome correlation swept over a grid, or a Bayesian E-value analogue) answers "how strong would a hidden confounder need to be to null the L→W indirect path?" — converting the suite's most heavily qualified claim into a defended one, reusing the existing g-formula code in `mediation.py`.
- **Historical-cohort borrowing for the nuisance trajectory.** LRP-RLM-HG-001 currently only reproduces the historical cohort. `METHODS.md` permits _external independent-sample_ information in priors (never the analysis data's own posterior). A meta-analytic-predictive prior from that cohort on the maturation / time trend — never on τ — could sharpen the DiD and level-factor models, where the untreated trajectory is the nuisance everything hinges on. Note the standing constraint: the analysis dataset _is_ the Burgoyne et al. (2012) trial (DOI: 10.1111/j.1469-7610.2012.02557.x), so that trial's own published effects remain off-limits as a τ prior.

## 2. Estimands not yet asked

- **Treatment-effect heterogeneity as a variance component.** Issue #228 item 9 proposes pooling the treatment × baseline interaction across outcomes, but nothing estimates _how much the treatment effect varies across children_ — a random treatment slope with its SD as the estimand. "Is τ homogeneous?" is a different, and for practice arguably more central, question than "is τ moderated by X?". At this n, report the variance posterior, not per-child predictions. This is also the prerequisite model for the non-responder question (section 4).
- **Time-to-off-floor for phonetic spelling (P) and nonword reading (N).** The floor rule collapses these to a binary at fixed timepoints, discarding _when_ a child first comes off floor across the four waves. A discrete-time survival model (treatment as a hazard shift, baseline covariates on the hazard) extracts the ordering information the floored outcomes actually contain — likely the most information-efficient reading of the spelling/nonword data available. This is also the right vehicle for section 5.
- **Responder profiles — a general-responder factor.** Do the _same_ children gain across skills, or are gains skill-specific? A joint gain model with correlated child-level effects across the eight outcomes (recombining the LKJ machinery from ITT-012 / MM-001) tests for a single "general responder" dimension — directly relevant to whether the intervention has one mechanism or several.
- **A readiness-threshold summary.** The HSGP mechanism surfaces already estimate the L→W relationship non-parametrically; adding a posterior for the _knee location_ ("does reading move only above ~k letter sounds?") is post-processing, not a new model, and speaks directly to instructional sequencing. The floor-sitter descriptives in section 5 suggest there is a real threshold to estimate.

## 3. Measurement and missingness debts

- **MNAR / informative-attrition sensitivity.** The complete-case comparators (ITT-113/114) address covariate missingness, but nothing bounds informative _attrition_. A delta-adjustment / pattern-mixture sweep on the flagship ITT models ("if dropped-out children gained δ less…") is cheap and closes the standard reviewer question.
- **Unused measured domains.** Beyond the home-literacy / TA-fidelity items already scoped-and-parked in #228 (items 7–8): the speech battery (`deapp*`, `lsam*`), behaviour (`sdq`), and hearing / vision history (`hearing`, `vision`, `earinf`) never enter any statistical model. They warrant a scoping decision — most naturally as adj-065-style between-child predictors of progress, after a missingness audit. The speech battery is specifically implicated by the floor-sitter production puzzle (section 5).

## 4. Characterising non-responders — two distinct questions

The education team's interest in "non-responders" resolves into two genuinely different analyses with very different identifiability.

**(a) Latent non-response (counterfactual).** An individual treatment effect is never observed; a child with zero observed gain is a mixture of true non-response, measurement noise on two bounded tests, maturation, and regression to the mean. The classic two-stage recipe — classify children as responders/non-responders on observed change, then compare the groups' characteristics — must be avoided: it manufactures a subgroup out of noise, mechanically correlates "non-response" with baseline, and finds predictors of an artefact (Senn 2016, _Statistics in Medicine_; DOI: 10.1002/sim.7059). The waitlist-crossover design helps, because every child contributes an on-vs-off contrast. The honest ladder:

1. **Does child-level response variance exist at all?** Add a random on-intervention slope to the gain-factor scaffold; make its SD the estimand. If the posterior concentrates near zero, apparent non-response is noise plus modelled covariates — a clean result that supports _not_ gatekeeping the intervention on perceived early response. Only if the variance is clearly non-zero does characterising non-responders become a real question. (Two repo facts set expectations: HS-001 selected _no_ predictors of word-reading gain, and the trt × baseline interaction is negative in 7/8 gain-factor models — weak evidence, but pointing against the common "low starters can't benefit" hypothesis.)
2. **If variance exists, characterise via one-stage moderation, never classification** — regress the latent child-level response on candidate characteristics inside the model (equivalently, trt × X interactions with hierarchical shrinkage across the candidate set), so no child is ever labelled.
3. **Two design-specific traps:** dose is post-randomisation (conditioning on attendance opens the collider the dose models flag — the CACE machinery of section 1 is the companion), and a child's on-vs-off contrast is confounded with age/order, so the immediate arm's anchoring (as in the DiD family) must stay in any variance model.

**(b) Observable persistent low attainment.** The more tractable reading of "non-responder" — children still at or around floor on phonics/nonword measures at t4 — is an _observed_ subgroup, not a latent quantity, so most of the above caution does not apply. This is section 5.

## 5. Persistent floor-sitters on phonics/nonword at t4

Motivated directly by the team's question about children still struggling at or around floor on nonword reading (N) and phonetic spelling (P) at t4 despite two or three intervention periods. A quick data cut (all figures from `data/rli_data_long.csv`) reframes it usefully:

- **Not a small tail — the harder half of a distribution still in motion.** At t4, 21/52 (40%) remain at floor on nonword reading and 26/54 (48%) on phonetic spelling (16 children at floor on both). Floor rates decline steadily across waves (N: 72% → 64% → 52% → 40%), so the cohort is coming off floor throughout; the t4 test is simply still developmentally hard for nearly half.
- **Floor status at t4 is arm-balanced** (nonword: 10 immediate vs 11 waitlist), with no crude signal that an earlier start or the extra period shifts it.
- **The event needs a "sustained" definition.** Of 48 children with all four waves observed, 12 _never_ came off floor on nonword (the near-noise-proof persistent group), but 7 came off and fell _back_ to zero by t4 — almost certainly 1–2-point flickering at the floor boundary. Any model should use a sustained off-floor event or a graded near-floor band, matching the team's "at or around floor" phrasing.
- **The dominant story is incomplete prerequisites, not inability.** Nonword floor rate at t4 by concurrent letter-sound band (of 32): **6/7 at floor when L<16, 11/23 at 16–25, 4/22 at 26–32** — a steep monotone gradient. Floored children have median L=20/32 and blending 5/10 at t4 (only 7/21 reach blending ≥6). Most t4 floor-sitters are earlier on the same decoding path, not off it. Several nonetheless read real words (median 5, max 34 of 79) — sight-reading without decoding, exactly as the MED-064 docstring cautions.
- **A small, clinically interesting puzzle group (n=4):** near-complete letter sounds (≥26/32), blending 5–10 (median 9.5) and some word reading, yet zero on nonword reading — one of them spelling 53 phonetically. A child who _spells_ phonetically but scores zero _reading_ nonwords aloud points at the task's speech-production demand — highly salient in this population, and connecting to the unused DEAP/LSAM speech battery (section 3). At n=4 this is case-review territory, not a model, but arguably the most clinically interesting observation in the cut.

**Proposed work:** a dated descriptive note done properly (both measures, overlap, trajectories, missingness); the discrete-time survival model of section 2 as the primary vehicle (hazard covariates _are_ the characterisation of persistent floor-sitters, in one identified model); a readiness-threshold estimand (P(off-floor on N | letter-sound level) with a posterior for the knee — the 16/26 banding suggests a real answer); and the four-child production case review against the speech measures. Slots alongside #225 (gf-N / lf-N off-floor models) and #228 item 12 (mediation into N via L/B).

**One causal caveat kept visible:** by t4 both arms have been treated, so there is no untreated comparison — "still at floor _despite_ intervention" is a prognostic description, not evidence the intervention failed these children (their untreated counterfactual is unknowable and could be worse). The hopeful practical reading runs the other way: floor status at t4 mostly reflects incomplete letter-sound foundations, and letter sounds is precisely the outcome the intervention moves most strongly (τ=+0.110, P=0.999) — pointing toward continue-and-extend, not screen-out.

## 6. Synthesis across the suite

- **Formal triangulation.** ITT, DiD and gain-factors converge qualitatively; a small cross-design layer (three τ's per outcome, design random effect) would give one pooled headline with cross-design uncertainty. Caveat: the designs share data, so the pooling is partly decorative — better delivered as a consistency statistic in `compare_statistical_models.py` than promoted to a headline.
- **Systematise the gradient-boosting → Bayesian handoff.** Step 1's SHAP-interaction findings currently feed step 2 by human judgement. A hierarchical-shrinkage model over _all_ candidate interactions (rather than promoting the eye-catching ones) would close the multiplicity gap in the two-step methodology itself.
- **Design analysis for the next study.** Turn the posteriors into assurance calculations: what sample size / measure choice would settle TR (P=0.968, just short of "strong") or the taught→untaught generalisation contrast? Nothing currently owns this finding-to-planning step.

## Suggested priority

If forced to rank: the **negative-control outcome** and the **mediation sensitivity analysis** first — cheap, and they _defend_ claims already made, which at n≈54 is worth more than new estimands. Then the **persistent floor-sitter descriptive + survival model** (real information currently discarded, and a direct team question), the **treatment-effect heterogeneity variance component** (prerequisite for the non-responder question), and **CACE**. The synthesis items (section 6) are worthwhile but can trail.
