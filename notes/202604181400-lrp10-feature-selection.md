## Motivation

LRP10 predicts CELF receptive-grammar **level** (`celf`) —
paralleling LRP02 (word-reading level), LRP04 (expressive-
vocabulary level), LRP06 (letter-sound knowledge level), and
LRP08 (receptive-vocabulary level). Baseline started from the
full `Predictors.DEFAULT_LEVEL` set minus the target (32
predictors), n=214, with no outlier exclusion.

extreme L1 alpha (4.72 vs typical 0.01–0.5) essentially forces
the model to zero-out weak predictors at training time. Plausibly
compensating for the coarse 0–18 target scale where a
finely-distributed ensemble would overfit; the tuner settled on
using heavy regularisation to force simplicity.

Large regime shift: the extreme-L1-few-fast-shallow full-set

Reading: the full-set tune's extreme L1 was compensating for
32 predictors with dense correlation — it zeroed out most
features to prevent over-fit. With Select01 handling redundancy
at the feature-selection step, the tune can move to a more
conventional "moderate deep" regime.

**Retune wins on every CV metric** — and tuner-inner CV MAE

Plausible mechanism: LRP09's full-set tune (many-slow-deep +

over-fitted inner CV. LRP10's full-set tune (extreme-L1-few-

not generalise cleanly — retuning on the cleaner 12-predictor

dropped from 0.437 to 0.289 — much tighter fold variance.

In-sample R² 0.912 is higher than the 32-pred tune (0.769) —
the deeper trees can over-fit more. But CV R² improved at the
same time, so the generalisation gap widened in both directions
coherently. Not a concern.

## Select02: construct-driven drop of eowpvt + b1reto (12 → 10)

Drops the two highest-importance retained features — `eowpvt`
(standardised expressive vocabulary, rank 1 at 0.146) and
`b1reto` (Block 1 intervention-taught receptive vocabulary,
rank 2 at 0.133) — to answer the research question "what
predicts receptive grammar **beyond** vocabulary?". Mirrors
LRP04's construct-driven Select02 drop of `b1exto`. Note the
cut retains `rowpvt` (standardised receptive vocabulary) and
`aptinfo` (language composite), so this is not a full vocabulary-
removal — rather a "drop the strongest two vocabulary handles"
cut that exercises whether the retained language measures can
compensate.

Expected to hurt CV R² since these are the top two predictors;
the trade is worse accuracy for a more interpretable model that
isolates the grammar-specific signal from the
vocabulary/language signal.
