# LRP72 — the phonics route: letter-sound × blending → decoding

> [!WARNING]
> These notes were generated by an AI tool. Model numbers are from the
> **reporting** config (6 chains × 6000 draws).

Date: 2026-06-16

Second model in the interaction family (after LRP71). Stacked on
`feature/lrp71-moderation` (which is stacked on the arviz-1.x migration, #68);
to be rebased once those land. Tests the dual-route / triangle-model hypothesis
that the two phonological prerequisites combine to produce decoding.

## Hypothesis

You need **both** phoneme blending (`B`) and letter-sound knowledge (`L`) to
decode a novel word, and they are **super-additive** (each worth more when the
other is present). Outcome: decoding = `nonword` (`N`, six monster nonwords).
This is the phonological-route sub-mechanism feeding letter-sound → word reading
(LRP58). The strongest, most theory-clean interaction in the exploration
(`notes/202606161215-…`): pooled `blending × letter-sound` on decoding, t ≈ 4.1
(positive).

## Model

LRP71's moderated-mechanism template with three swaps + two machinery additions:

- Mechanism = `L`, moderator = `B`, outcome = `N`. Estimand
  `gamma_int · z(logit L) · z(B)` (+ main effects `beta_mech · z(logit L)`,
  `gamma_mod · z(B)`).
- **Linear mechanism** (new factory flag `linear_mechanism`): decoding is a
  57%-floored 0–6 count, so the `f_mech` HSGP is replaced by a single linear
  slope `beta_mech` (`beta_mech_prior` = Normal(0, 1)). No GP → no funnel.
- **Minimal prepared outcomes** (`extra["outcomes"]=["L","B","N"]`): so
  `drop_missing_pre` doesn't discard rows for unrelated measures. Pipeline
  `_pre`-filter generalised from `W_pre` to any `*_pre` so `N_pre` works as the
  autoregressive baseline.
- **Adjustment {G, A, N_pre}; W excluded** — W is a sibling/descendant of
  decoding, so conditioning on it is over-control / collider risk (celf/LRP70
  error class). Documented in the report.
- `lrp72base` = no-interaction companion (L + B main effects, B as a plain
  confounder) for a clean nested PSIS-LOO test of the interaction. Same 153 rows.

## Result (reporting; n = 153, 53 children; 0 divergences, R-hat 1.00, ESS ~20k)

| coefficient                           |      mean | 95% CI              |     P(>0) |
| ------------------------------------- | --------: | ------------------- | --------: |
| `beta_mech` (letter-sound → decoding) |     +1.14 | [0.70, 1.62]        |     ~1.00 |
| `gamma_mod` (blending main effect)    |     +0.44 | [0.14, 0.75]        |     0.998 |
| `gamma_int` (L × B interaction)       | **−0.29** | **[−0.59, −0.001]** | **0.025** |

**Both prerequisites have strong, credible positive main effects** on decoding.
But the **interaction is credibly _negative_** (sub-additive) — the **opposite**
of the hypothesised super-additivity, with the 95% CI just excluding zero.

**This does not confirm the dual-route super-additive prediction.** Read it
honestly with two qualifications:

1. **It reverses the exploratory pooled signal.** The screen's positive
   `t ≈ 4.1` came from a pooled regression (~4 rows/child, no subject random
   effects, no baseline) the note itself flagged as inflated. Under the proper
   model (subject random intercepts, `N_pre`, phase intercepts) the sign flips —
   exactly the confirmation step the exploration demanded, and it fails. A good
   illustration of why the two-step (explore → confirm) workflow matters.
2. **Link-saturation ambiguity.** On a 57%-floored bounded count, a negative
   _logit-scale_ interaction between two strong positive main effects is partly
   what a saturating sigmoid produces when both predictors are high. So the
   negative `gamma_int` should not be over-read as substantive antagonism. The
   defensible conclusion: the two routes contribute **largely independently**
   (additively), with at most modest, link-ambiguous evidence of diminishing
   returns — not super-additive synergy.

**PSIS-LOO vs `lrp72base`**
(`output/statistical_models/comparison/phonics_route_loo_compare.csv`): the
interaction model ranks first but elpd_diff ≈ 2 with dse ≈ 2.5 — **within
noise**. Adding the interaction does not decisively improve predictive fit,
consistent with the modest, link-ambiguous `gamma_int`.

## Engineering

- Factory: `linear_mechanism` flag (linear `beta_mech·z(L)` in place of the HSGP;
  default False keeps the GP for LRP56–58/71); `z_L` now computed whenever
  moderator-or-linear. Pipeline: `extra["outcomes"]` override, generalised
  `*_pre` baseline filter, `linear_mechanism` pass-through, `beta_mech` in
  diagnostics. `beta_mech_prior` added to `priors.py`.
- New `lrp72.py`, `lrp72base.py`, registered in `fit_statistical_model.py`.
  `docs/models/lrp72/index.qmd`. `compare_statistical_models.py`:
  `phonics_route_loo_compare` (refactored a shared `_loo_compare` helper).
- No `measures.py` change: `B` (blending, N=10) and `N` (nonword, N=6) already
  exist and are `n_trials_confirmed=True`.
- Sampling clean (0 divergences) — the linear-mechanism choice avoided the
  floor-induced funnel a GP would have risked.

## Record for the team: reading comprehension can't be modelled in this sample

The exploration's other interaction candidate, reading comprehension (`yarcsi`),
is **67% floored** (max observed 11, nonzero for only ~33% of observations; ~40%
by the last timepoint). Reading _comprehension_ has not emerged for most children
with Down syndrome at these reading levels — they are at the word-**decoding**
stage. The "where does vocabulary / grammar matter for reading?" question (which
the LRP71 vocabulary null naturally raises) therefore **needs an older /
higher-reading sample** and is not answerable in these data. A data-coverage
limitation and a motivation for future data collection — not a modelling gap.
