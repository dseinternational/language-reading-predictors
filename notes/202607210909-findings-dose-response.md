# Dose-response (session intensity) findings (2026-07-21)

This is one of a set of per-family notes from the full 2026-07-21 re-fit of every Bayesian statistical model in the study (production `reporting` configuration: 6 chains × 6000 draws, 89% credible intervals, fit 2026-07-20/21). Read the shared index and reading guide `notes/202607210900-findings-00-index-and-reading-guide.md` first — it explains the study, the outcome measures and their maxima, and how to read Bayesian numbers (median, 89% interval, tail probability, evidence ladder). The **dose-response family has 5 models** (dose-077, dose-083, dose-084, dose-177, dose-277); **1 of the 5 passes the convergence gate cleanly** (dose-277). The other four carry a divergence-only flag and are usable with the caveat noted below. All data and models are preliminary.

## What this family probes

The study is a **waitlist-crossover randomised trial**: children with Down syndrome were randomly assigned to receive a reading/phonics intervention immediately or after a wait, and were tested at four timepoints. The dose-response models set the randomised _arm_ aside and ask a different, descriptive question: **among children on the intervention, did those who attended more teaching sessions make more progress?** Each model relates a bounded outcome count (scored on a proportion-correct scale through a logit link — the log-odds transform that keeps predictions inside 0-100%) to the standardised number of sessions a child received, and — in four of the five fits — lets that dose-outcome slope differ across the three study-period transitions ("period-varying dose"). A subject **random intercept** (a per-child offset that partially pools stable individual differences) is included in every model.

**What each model adjusts for** (the adjustment set is pre-specified from the causal diagram, the DAG):

- **dose-077** — dose → **word reading (WR)**, adjusting for group (IG), age (A) and own baseline word reading (`WR_pre`); period-varying slope.
- **dose-083** — dose → **letter-sound knowledge (LS)**, adjusting for IG, A and own baseline letter sounds (`LS_pre`); period-varying.
- **dose-084** — dose → **phoneme blending (PA)**, adjusting for IG, A and own baseline blending (`PA_pre`); period-varying.
- **dose-177** — the **ability-adjusted sensitivity variant** of dose-077 (variant role _alternate_, parent `lrp177`): dose → WR adjusting for IG, A, `WR_pre` **plus a baseline-skill cluster** (`LS_pre`, `EV_pre`, `PA_pre`) as a proxy for general ability, to test whether the dose-WR link is just ability leaking through; period-varying.
- **dose-277** — the **pooled-slope comparator** to dose-077 (variant role _comparator_, parent `lrp277`): the same adjustment set (IG, A, `WR_pre`) but a **single dose slope with no period variation**. This is the family's identification anchor and the one clean-gate fit.

So the family covers three outcomes (WR, LS, PA) plus two variants of the WR model (an ability-adjusted sensitivity check and a pooled-slope comparator).

**The single most important framing point: session dose was not randomised, and it is a partial collider in the DAG.** How many sessions a child attended reflects ability, attendance, family circumstances and availability — a collider is a variable sitting downstream of two things we care about, so reading a slope _through_ it can open a spurious back-door path. Abler or better-attending children plausibly _both_ do more sessions _and_ score higher, and a raw dose slope cannot separate those stories. **Every number in this family is therefore an observational (adjusted) association and a sensitivity/triangulation view — never a treatment effect.** The correct reading is "children who attended more scored higher, and here is whether that relationship bends across periods", not "more sessions cause more progress". For the genuinely causal contrasts see the ITT suite and the difference-in-differences note.

## How to read these numbers (recap)

Full conventions are in the shared reading guide. In brief:

- The point estimate is the posterior **median**; lead with the **interval**, not the point, because with ~53-54 children an estimate that just clears a threshold is on average inflated (the winner's curse).
- Uncertainty is the **89% equal-tailed credible interval** ("an 89% posterior probability the value lies in this range"), with the inner **50%** interval as the "most-of-the-mass" range. 89%, not 95%, is the deliberate house standard.
- Direction is the **tail probability** — P(association > 0), read directly, never as a p-value. Its **evidence label** is fixed by round odds: _inconclusive_ (< 0.75), _suggestive_ (≥ 0.75), _moderate_ (≥ 0.91), _strong_ (≥ 0.97), _very strong_ (≥ 0.99). The label grades _evidence for a directional claim_, oriented to the favoured direction; it never describes the _size_ of the effect.
- The slope is reported on the **logit scale** — the change in log-odds of a correct item per **1 standard deviation (SD) of session count**. Here 1 SD ≈ **31 sessions** (mean ≈ 54, so "+1 SD" is roughly going from 54 to 85 sessions). For the four period-varying models (dose-077/083/084/177) the model's own `dose_slope_summary.csv` reports these logit slopes; the interpretable items-scale marginal CSV was not surfaced for those four, so no items translation is quoted for them. Only dose-277 has a gate-interlocked items translation, quoted below.
- **Causal versus association.** Nothing here is causal. Every slope is an _adjusted association_; reading it as a lever is the Table-2 fallacy. Residual confounding by latent general ability remains, and the child random intercept pools stable differences but does **not** stand in for general ability.

## Per-model results

**Convergence gate.** Each fit is checked against R-hat ≤ 1.01 (chains agree), effective sample size (ESS) ≥ 400 (enough independent draws), BFMI ≥ 0.3 (the sampler explored well), and zero divergences. **dose-277 passes cleanly.** The other four fail the gate _only_ on a small number of divergent transitions, all far under the METHODS ≤ 1% guidance (of 36,000 draws): **dose-077 17 divergences (0.047%)**, **dose-083 4 (0.011%)**, **dose-084 2 (0.006%)** and **dose-177 13 (0.036%)**. Every one of these four passes R-hat and ESS comfortably (max R-hat ≤ 1.0015, min ESS ≈ 3,800-6,400), so they are usable with the divergence caveat noted. The recurring divergences reflect the mildly funnel-shaped geometry of the period-varying random-intercept parameterisation and would be the target of any future reparameterisation.

**A note on the two probabilities.** For the four period-varying models the _overall_ slope (`dose_overall`) is the population hyper-mean of the three period slopes and additionally absorbs the between-period spread, so its 89% interval crosses zero and its own P(> 0) is only 0.81-0.91 (suggestive). The **period-1 slope is individually far better determined** — for the three WR and LS models it is very strong (PS 0.995-0.999) with an 89% interval well clear of zero. Both point the same way; the period-1 figure is the cleanest single "more dose went with higher scores in the randomised first period" summary, while the overall hyper-mean honestly reflects the extra uncertainty about whether the periods share one common slope.

### dose-077 — word reading (WR), base model (association)

Adjusts for group (IG), age (A) and own baseline word reading (`WR_pre`), with a period-varying dose slope and a child random intercept. The overall dose slope is **+0.137 logit [−0.056, +0.312]** per +1 SD ≈ 31 sessions (50% CI +0.077 to +0.194; P(> 0) = 0.904, **suggestive** as a directional association). The relationship is clearly **front-loaded across periods**: period 1 **+0.177** [+0.076, +0.293], P = 0.998 (**very strong**); period 2 **+0.135** [−0.007, +0.273], P = 0.937 (**moderate**); period 3 **+0.097** [−0.045, +0.214], P = 0.867 (**suggestive**). The between-period slope SD (`sigma_dose_between_period`) is 0.107 [0.010, 0.444] — its lower bound sits essentially at zero, so the data are consistent with a genuinely period-varying slope _or_ a flat common one. This is an association only: it does not show that adding sessions to a given child raises their reading; abler or better-attending children may drive both.

### dose-083 — letter-sound knowledge (LS), base model (association)

LS is the YARC letter-sound task (name the sound for each of 32 letters). Adjusts for IG, A and own baseline `LS_pre`, period-varying. The overall slope is **+0.148 logit [−0.134, +0.397]** per +1 SD sessions (50% CI +0.058 to +0.232; P(> 0) = 0.846, **suggestive**). Front-loading again, and more pronounced: period 1 **+0.227** [+0.084, +0.379], P = 0.995 (**very strong**); period 2 **+0.169** [−0.049, +0.384], P = 0.899 (**suggestive**); period 3 **+0.054** [−0.173, +0.237], P = 0.648 (**inconclusive** — direction unclear late). Between-period SD 0.180 [0.022, 0.577]. Association only.

### dose-084 — phoneme blending (PA), base model (association)

PA is a 10-item phoneme-blending task (hearing separate sounds and blending them into a word). Adjusts for IG, A and own baseline `PA_pre`, period-varying. The overall slope is **+0.138 logit [−0.165, +0.459]** per +1 SD sessions (50% CI +0.035 to +0.246; P(> 0) = 0.811, **suggestive**) — the widest overall interval in the family. Its period pattern differs from the reading measures — the strongest slope is **period 2**, not period 1: period 1 **+0.104** [−0.073, +0.275], P = 0.828 (**suggestive**); period 2 **+0.291** [+0.026, +0.643], P = 0.962 (**moderate**); period 3 **+0.037** [−0.224, +0.252], P = 0.597 (**inconclusive**). Between-period SD 0.229 [0.026, 0.663] — the largest in the family, consistent with a genuinely uneven slope, though still floored at ~zero. Association only, and the weakest and noisiest of the family.

### dose-177 — word reading (WR), ability-adjusted sensitivity variant (association)

A variant of dose-077 (parent `lrp177`, role _alternate_) that adds a **baseline-skill cluster** — baseline letter sounds, expressive vocabulary and phoneme blending (`LS_pre`, `EV_pre`, `PA_pre`) — to the adjustment set as a proxy for general ability, to probe whether the dose-WR association is just ability leaking through. It is **not**: the slopes barely move from the base model. The overall slope is **+0.149 logit [−0.051, +0.327]** (50% CI +0.086 to +0.209; P(> 0) = 0.909, **suggestive**), essentially the same as dose-077's +0.137. Front-loading persists: period 1 **+0.199** [+0.093, +0.316], P = 0.999 (**very strong**); period 2 **+0.150** [+0.003, +0.294], P = 0.948 (**moderate**); period 3 **+0.100** [−0.043, +0.223], P = 0.872 (**suggestive**); between-period SD 0.117 [0.012, 0.460]. Adjusting for observed baseline skills does not explain the dose-WR link away — but this rules out only _measured_ ability, not latent general ability, so the collider caveat still stands.

### dose-277 — word reading (WR), pooled-slope comparator (association; the one clean-gate fit)

A variant of dose-077 (parent `lrp277`, role _comparator_) with the **same adjustment set (IG, A, `WR_pre`)** but a **single pooled dose slope** — no period variation. This is the family's identification anchor: it is the only model that **passes the convergence gate cleanly** (0 divergences). Because it lands squarely on the period-average with a single well-determined slope, its gate-interlocked key finding carries the family's one items-scale translation: **a 1-SD increase in sessions (≈ 31 sessions) is associated with +1.3 of 79 items on word reading (89% credible range +0.6 to +2.0)**. The posterior probability of a positive association is **99.8%** — **very strong** evidence that higher session dose accompanies a higher outcome. As the fit itself states, session dose was not randomised and may reflect ability, attendance or availability, so this slope is an observational association. Pooling the slope rather than letting it vary by period reproduces the same one-directional dose-WR picture as the base and ability-adjusted fits.

## What to take away

Across all five fits the message is consistent and one-directional: **children who attended more sessions tended to score higher, most markedly on word reading (WR) and letter sounds (LS), and most strongly in the earliest study period.** For word reading the pooled comparator (dose-277, the one clean-gate fit) puts the association at **+1.3 of 79 words per +1 SD ≈ 31 sessions** with very strong directional evidence, and the period-varying base (dose-077) and ability-adjusted variant (dose-177) agree closely on the logit scale — robustness to the baseline-skill cluster is the family's most reassuring internal check. The letter-sound and blending associations are weaker and noisier (LS: period-1 slope very strong but the overall hyper-mean only suggestive; PA: only suggestive overall, with an unusual period-2 peak).

Two structural cautions temper this. First, the **period front-loading is suggestive, not established**: for every period-varying model the between-period slope SD has a lower 89% bound essentially at zero, so the data cannot cleanly distinguish a genuinely front-loaded slope from a flat common one — which is why the pooled comparator (dose-277) fits comparably. Second, and decisively, **none of this is causal.** Session dose was not assigned and is a partial collider; every slope is an observational association and remains confounded by latent general ability and by attendance/availability, even in the ability-adjusted dose-177. The pattern triangulates with — and is fully consistent with — the causal ITT word-reading benefit (see the ITT note for the τ estimate), but it cannot add to that causal claim: the ITT τ already answers "does assignment help?", and the dose slope only describes "who, among the treated, progressed more". Whether the sessions→WR curve _bends_ (accelerates or saturates) belongs to the mechanism family's knee-test companion, not to this family, which deliberately fits only a straight line in standardised sessions within each period.
