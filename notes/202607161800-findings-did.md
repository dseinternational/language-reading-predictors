## What these models ask

This is a **waiting-list crossover** design: the immediate arm starts the programme at t1, the waiting-list arm crosses over and starts at t2. The models fit the bounded score at each wave (t1, t2, t3) with separate arm-by-wave contrasts. The important distinction (the heart of issue #340):

- **`tau_t2`** — the **t2 arm gap** — is the **clean randomised comparison** (immediate arm treated, waiting-list arm not yet). This is the one **causal** number.
- **`arm_gap_t1`** is a baseline-balance check; **`arm_gap_t3`** and the **crossover catch-up** quantity are _post-crossover associations_, not second randomised effects. They lean on strong assumptions (no cumulative-dose or carryover differences) and are flagged as descriptive.

Two dose companions (`did-006` pooled, `did-007` period-resolved, with `did-107` a comparator) relate outcomes to the **amount** of intervention — an **observational association**, never a treatment effect.

## The one-paragraph story

A **third independent design** — using only the randomised t2 window — again lands on the same pattern: **very strong** evidence of benefit on **letter sounds**, and positive-leaning effects on word reading, blending and taught vocabulary, with broad standardised vocabulary flat. The crossover "catch-up" quantities (the waiting-list arm closing the gap once it starts) behave as expected but are descriptive, not a second experiment.

## What is causal, and what #340 fixed

Only the **t2 arm gap** is causal. The re-specification (#340) stopped the model from silently treating the _post-crossover_ t3 gap as if it were a second randomised effect, separated **treatment presence** from **attendance dose** in the dose companions, corrected the random-slope marginal in `did-013`, and made the cross-model LOO comparison **exclude gate-failing fits** — which is exactly what happened this run (`did-007` and `dose-077` were correctly dropped from their LOO comparisons).
