## What these models ask

This is a **waiting-list crossover** design: the immediate arm starts the programme at t1, the waiting-list arm crosses over and starts at t2. Each model fits the bounded score at each wave (t1, t2, t3) with separate arm-by-wave contrasts, using a Beta-Binomial likelihood on the post-score counts. The important distinction (the heart of issue #340) is which contrast randomisation actually licenses:

- **`tau_t2`** — the **t2 arm gap** — is the **clean randomised comparison** (immediate arm treated, waiting-list arm not yet started). This is the one **causal** number per model.
- **`arm_gap_t1`** is a **baseline-balance check** — the arm difference _before_ anyone is treated, so it should be near zero if randomisation worked.
- **`arm_gap_t3`** (the arm difference after the waiting-list arm has crossed over) and **`delta_crossover = tau_t2 − arm_gap_t3`** (the "catch-up" of the waiting-list arm once it starts) are **post-crossover associations**, not second randomised effects. They lean on strong assumptions (no cumulative-dose or carryover differences) and are flagged descriptive.

Three dose companions (`did-006` pooled for word reading, `did-007` period-resolved for letter sounds, with `did-107` a pooled-slope comparator for letter sounds) relate outcomes to the **amount** of intervention attended. Their `beta_dose`/`dose_*` slopes are **observational associations** — children who attend more are not a randomised subgroup — never a treatment effect.

A reading aid for the numbers below, since this note is written for readers more used to frequentist statistics. A **95% credible range** is the interval the parameter falls in with 95% probability _given the data and priors_ — a direct probability statement about the parameter, unlike a confidence interval. **P(effect>0)** is the posterior probability the true effect is positive (or, when the estimate leans negative, we quote the probability of the favoured direction). The **evidence label** grades that direction probability against the project ladder (#179) — inconclusive `<0.75≤` suggestive `<0.91≤` moderate `<0.97≤` strong `<0.99≤` very strong — so it measures **how sure we are of the sign, not how big the effect is**. A 92% here means "92% sure the effect is positive", not "an effect of 92%".

Reading the associations. Every **`arm_gap_t1`** is small and mostly slightly negative, and none reaches even the suggestive bar — exactly what you want to see: the arms were **balanced at baseline**, consistent with successful randomisation. The **`arm_gap_t3`** figures are the arm difference _after_ the waiting-list arm has crossed over; they mostly lean positive (immediate arm still nominally ahead) but only reach "suggestive" for L, W and B and are otherwise inconclusive — and, crucially, they are **not a second randomised comparison** because both arms are treated by t3. The two largest **`delta_crossover`** ("catch-up") signals are **did-004 TE (+1.84 items, strong)** and **did-002 L (+2.22 items, moderate)**: they describe the waiting-list arm's gain once it finally starts the programme, which is reassuring but remains an observational contrast confounded by timing and age-at-onset.

### did-013 — exploratory catch-up heterogeneity (word reading)

`did-013` reproduces the did-001 word-reading model (`arm_gap_t1` −0.67 items; `arm_gap_t3` +2.0 items, P(>0)=0.844, suggestive; `tau_t2` +2.2 items, moderate — all matching did-001) and adds a **random-slope catch-up** term to ask whether children vary in how much they close the gap after crossover. The corrected sample-average marginal (the #340 fix to the random-slope calculation) is **`delta_crossover_sample_average` = +0.103 logit, 95% −0.29 to +0.49, P(>0)=0.698, inconclusive**, averaged over 25 children. There is no credible catch-up heterogeneity, and the items-scale version of this marginal is not available (it would need an integrated waiting-list counterfactual). This is a descriptive/exploratory quantity, not causal.

### Dose companions (observational — NOT treatment effects)

These relate the outcome to **attendance dose** (sessions), adjusting for arm, the shared pre-randomisation t1 outcome and t1 age. `beta_dose`/`dose_*` are **intensive-margin observational associations** — the "more sessions ↔ higher score" gradient among children who differ in attendance — and must **not** be read as "more dose causes more learning". `theta_treated` separates mere treatment _presence_ from dose intensity; `beta_group`/`beta_period` are further adjusted associations.

The one genuinely strong dose signal is **did-006's word-reading `beta_dose` (+0.228 logit, P(>0)=0.997)** — but it is an **observational** gradient, not a treatment effect, and word reading is the outcome with the clearest randomised benefit anyway. `did-007` allows the dose slope to vary by period (period-1 randomised transition vs later); the two period slopes are close (+0.149 vs +0.112) and the between-period heterogeneity σ (0.179) has a wide range straddling near-zero, so there is **no evidence of a period-specific dose effect or any dose threshold/nonlinearity** — and this is the model that failed the gate, so both slopes are caveated. `did-107` is the pooled-slope (no-period-variation) LOO comparator to `did-007`; its dose slope is weaker and only suggestive.

## Per-arm trajectories (why the design works)

The fitted per-wave levels show the mechanism the design relies on. For word reading (`did-001`): both arms start balanced (**t1 ≈ 6.1 items immediate vs ≈ 6.8 waiting-list**), the **causal gap opens at t2** once only the immediate arm is treated (**10.8 vs 8.6**), and by t3 the waiting-list arm has crossed over and is closing the gap (**14.9 vs 12.9**). Both arms grow throughout; the causal signal is the _t2 divergence_, and the t3 convergence is the expected (descriptive) catch-up, not evidence the effect fades.

## The one-paragraph story

A **third independent design** — using only the randomised t2 window — again lands on the same pattern: **very strong** evidence of benefit on **letter sounds** (+3.5 items), and positive-leaning **moderate** effects on **word reading** (+2.2), **phoneme blending** (+0.9) and **taught expressive vocabulary** (+1.5), with broad standardised vocabulary (R, E) flat and the heavily-floored reading measures (N, P) too imprecise to call. This corroborates the [ITT](202607161800-findings-itt.md), [gain-factors](202607161800-findings-gain_factors.md) and [level-factors](202607161800-findings-level_factors.md) families. The crossover "catch-up" quantities behave as expected (largest for TE and L) but are **descriptive associations**, not a second experiment, and the dose gradients — including did-006's strong word-reading slope — are **observational**, not causal.

## What is causal, and what #340 fixed

Only the **t2 arm gap (`tau_t2`)** is causal — it is the one contrast randomisation licenses. Everything else in this note (`arm_gap_t1` baseline balance, `arm_gap_t3` and `delta_crossover` post-crossover gaps, `did-013`'s catch-up heterogeneity, and every dose slope in did-006/007/107) is a **latent-ability-confounded adjusted or observational association**, never "X drives Y". The re-specification (#340) stopped the model from silently treating the _post-crossover_ t3 gap as if it were a second randomised effect, separated **treatment presence** (`theta_treated`) from **attendance dose** (`beta_dose`) in the dose companions, corrected the random-slope marginal in `did-013`, and made the cross-model LOO comparison **exclude gate-failing fits** — which is exactly what happened this run (`did-007` and `dose-077` were correctly dropped from their LOO comparisons).
