## Question and design

How much of the intervention's effect on word reading (W) flows **through** its
effect on letter-sound knowledge (L)? Decompose the total effect into the natural
indirect effect (NIE, via L) and natural direct effect (NDE, other paths).

- **Phase 0 only** (`phase_mode="itt"`, t1→t2): the only randomised window (group
  1 intervention, group 2 wait-list control; both treated after t2). One row per
  child, **n = 53**. `G = group−1` (G=0 intervention, G=1 control); reported
  intervention-helps (positive = raises reading).
- Joint Beta-Binomial model: mediator `logit(L_t2) ~ a0 + a_G·G + a_L·logit(L_t1)
  + a_C·C_t1`; outcome `logit(W_t2) ~ b0 + b_G·G + b_M·z(logit L_t2) +
  b_GM·G·z(L_t2) + b_W·logit(W_t1) + b_C·C_t1`. G×M interaction included.
- **NDE/NIE by counterfactual simulation from the posterior (g-formula)**, not a
  coefficient product (invalid on the logit scale): per draw, simulate L_t2 under
  each arm from the mediator model, push through the outcome model, average over
  the observed covariate distribution.
- **Adjustment {G, A, E, R, L_t1, W_pre}** at **baseline (t1)** — randomisation
  handles G→Y, G→M; we adjust the measured L→W confounders (age, expressive vocab
  E — LRP58 found it independently predicts W — receptive vocab R, baselines).
  Baseline (not post) values respect the cross-world assumption.

## Result (reporting; n = 53; 0 divergences, R-hat 1.00, ESS ~21k)

Intervention-helps direction, word-count scale (out of 90):

| quantity | mean | 95% CI | P(>0) |
| --- | ---: | --- | ---: |
| **Total** (intervention → reading) | **+2.92** | [0.53, 5.35] | 0.99 |
| **NIE** (via letter-sound) | **+1.83** | [0.46, 3.64] | **0.998** |
| NDE (other paths) | +1.09 | [−0.95, 3.16] | 0.85 |
| **proportion mediated** (NIE/Total) | **0.62** | [0.17, 1.89] | — (P(Total>0)=0.99) |

**Headline: the intervention raises word reading largely by raising letter-sound
knowledge.** The indirect path (NIE) is credibly positive (P>0 = 0.998); the
direct path (NDE) is not credible (P>0 = 0.85); the point estimate is **~62%
mediated**. Coefficients are sensible and reconcile with the programme: `a_G` =
−0.59 (intervention raises letter-sound, cf LRP55 τ_L), `b_M` = +0.52
(letter-sound raises reading, cf LRP58).

**Reconciliation with LRP52.** LRP59's Total (+2.92 words/90 ≈ +3.2pp) agrees in
**direction and order of magnitude** with LRP52's τ_W (+1.83pp ≈ +1.65 words;
both credibly positive, overlapping wide CIs). It is modestly larger here,
attributable to the different, mediation-specific **linear** adjustment set
(LRP52 used age + own-baseline GPs and all seven cross-baselines).

## Honest reading

- **Wide intervals (n = 53).** The proportion-mediated CI [0.17, 1.89] is broad;
  the >100% upper tail reflects draws where the direct effect is slightly negative
  (indirect carries "more than all" of a smaller total). The robust reading is the
  **NDE/NIE split** (indirect credible, direct not), not a precise percentage.
- **Under assumptions.** Causal only under no-unmeasured-L→W-confounding (the
  binding, unverifiable assumption — the main limitation, stated front-and-centre
  in the report), plus the cross-world/consistency/positivity assumptions.
  Randomisation secures the G→Y and G→M legs. Not assumption-free proof of
  mechanism.

This converts the programme's headline from "the intervention raises reading, and
letter-sound predicts reading" into the active-ingredient claim DSE needs.
