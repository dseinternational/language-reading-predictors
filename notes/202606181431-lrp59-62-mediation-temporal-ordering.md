## The problem

The mediation decomposition is the only analysis that can use the randomised
contrast, which lives in the **t1 → t2** window. As a consequence the mediator and
the outcome are measured at the **same wave**:

- **LRP59**: mediator $M = L_{t2}$ (letter-sound), outcome $Y = W_{t2}$ (word reading).
- **LRP62**: mediator = phonics-route composite at t2, outcome $W_{t2}$.

The g-formula NDE/NIE decomposition assumes the mediator lies on the causal path
*before* the outcome. Contemporaneous measurement means the ordering $M \to Y$ is
**assumed, not observed** — reverse or simultaneous influence ($W_{t2} \to L_{t2}$)
cannot be ruled out within the wave. This is a **forced consequence of the design**:
both arms are treated from t2 on, so there is no randomised window with a strictly
later outcome. It cannot be fixed by re-specification — only handled by
interpretation, explicit caveats, and triangulation.

## Decision

1. **State the temporal-ordering assumption in the headline, not just the body.**
   Both reports (`docs/models/lrp59/index.qmd`, `docs/models/lrp62/index.qmd`) now
   carry a `callout-warning` near the top — *"Mediator and outcome are measured at
   the same wave"* — and the assumption is restated alongside the proportion-mediated
   headline (the "Interpret under these assumptions" / "Triangulation" callouts), not
   only in the assumptions section.

2. **Add a non-randomised W_t3 sensitivity check as a triangulation point.** Refit
   the **identical** model with the **outcome word reading at t3** while the mediator
   stays at t2, so the mediator precedes the outcome in time. See spec below.

3. **Keep the proportion-mediated / phonics-route-share headline — with the caveat
   stated alongside it.** It is not dropped or demoted; it is reported with the
   temporal-ordering assumption (and the no-unmeasured-confounding assumption and
   wide interval) made explicit at the point of reading.

## W_t3 sensitivity specification

Mediator at t2, **outcome word reading at t3**; everything else identical to the
headline model (mediator/route baseline at t1, confounders $E_{t1}, R_{t1}$, age at
t1, $G$ the randomised group). $n = 53$ — the same children as the ITT headline (no
attrition penalty; only t4 drops to 51).

**Outcome autoregressive baseline stays $W_{t1}$, not $W_{t2}$.** This was the one
genuine specification choice. $W_{t2}$ is **post-randomisation** — affected by $G$
and correlated with the mediator (both at t2) — so conditioning on it would (a)
**block part of the $G \to W_{t3}$ total effect** that runs through $W_{t2}$,
distorting NDE/NIE, and (b) condition on a treatment-affected, mediator-correlated
variable (collider risk). This is the same rule the models already enforce for
confounders: the factory docstring states post-treatment values are *"inadmissible"*
and the report says *"Conditioning on post-treatment vocabulary would violate this"*
(the cross-world assumption). So $W_{t1}$ is correct; only the outcome **wave** moves.

**Caveat carried into the reports.** The **t2 → t3 increment is not randomised**
(both arms are treated after t2), so the *total* effect in this variant is an
attenuated, non-randomised contrast. Read the **sign/direction of the indirect
effect** — its agreement with the contemporaneous headline — as the triangulation,
**not** the magnitudes; intervals are wider.

**Observed direction** (development-config check on the real data; final numbers
come from the reporting run): the **indirect effect through letter-sound / the
phonics route remained robustly positive** ($P(\text{NIE}>0) \approx 0.99$) with the
temporally-ordered t3 outcome, while the direct/residual path shrank toward zero and
intervals widened — consistent with, and triangulating, the contemporaneous headline.

## Implementation

- `preprocessing.load_and_prepare_lagged_outcome(outcome_symbol, outcome_time=3)` —
  ITT prep (t1 baselines, t2 mediator) with the outcome symbol's post-counts swapped
  to a later wave, aligned by subject. Reuses `build_mediation_model` / `decompose`
  unchanged.
- `pipeline._fit_t3_sensitivity` + a sensitivity pass in `fit_mediation`: builds,
  samples (reusing the run's sampling config), and decomposes the t3 variant, writing
  `mediation_summary_t3.csv` alongside the headline `mediation_summary.csv`.
- Reports embed a *"Temporal-ordering sensitivity — outcome at t3"* section.

## Not resolved here

- The binding limitation remains **no unmeasured mediator→outcome confounding**;
  this note is about temporal ordering only.
- A later outcome does not restore randomisation — the t3 check is triangulation,
  not a cleaner estimate. If future waves or a design with a randomised later outcome
  become available, revisit.
