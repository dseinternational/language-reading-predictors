## What this family does, and the question it answers

The study is a **waitlist-crossover randomised trial**: children with Down syndrome were randomly assigned to receive a reading/phonics intervention immediately or after a wait, and were tested at four timepoints (~54 children). The intention-to-treat (ITT) family answers _whether_ assignment moved a skill. This family answers a different question: **of the intervention's total effect on an outcome, how much runs _through_ a specific intermediate skill (the "mediator"), and how much does not?**

Each model splits a total effect into two parts by **g-formula** (a way of estimating what would have happened under hypothetical, "counterfactual" scenarios by simulating from the fitted model rather than multiplying coefficients): a **natural direct effect (NDE)** — the part that does _not_ pass through the mediator — and a **natural indirect effect (NIE)** — the part that _does_. Total = NDE + NIE. Three models (078, 186, 187) are **interventional** companions that relabel the two parts IDE/IIE; they target a slightly weaker-assumption version of the same split (see caveats). Three others (064, 066, 075) carry **two** mediators at once and split the indirect part per route.

The central question the family was built to test: **is the intervention's word-reading gain carried by the letter-sound (decoding) route, rather than by a vocabulary route?** The concrete contrast is NIE-through-letter-sounds versus NIE-through-vocabulary.

## How to read these numbers (recap, and one crucial framing rule)

Full conventions are in the shared reading guide. In brief: the point estimate is the posterior **median** (the "posterior" is the full probability distribution for a quantity given data and priors); uncertainty is the **89% equal-tailed credible interval** ("89% posterior probability the value lies in this range"), with the inner **50%** interval as the "most-of-the-mass" range; direction is the **tail probability** P(effect > 0), read directly and never as a p-value. The evidence label is fixed by round odds: _inconclusive_ (< 0.75), _suggestive_ (≥ 0.75), _moderate_ (≥ 0.91), _strong_ (≥ 0.97), _very strong_ (≥ 0.99), and it grades the _evidence for a direction_, never the _size_. Effects are modelled on a probability (proportion-correct) scale and translated to **items** by multiplying by the test maximum (word reading W /79, letter sounds L /32, blending B /10; the off-floor nonword outcome N is a risk difference in **percentage points**). With ~54 children an estimate that just clears a threshold is on average inflated, so lead with the interval.

**The framing rule that matters most here: none of these NDE/NIE/total quantities is a causal lever.** The decomposition rests on a _no-unmeasured-mediator–outcome-confounding_ assumption that is not testable in this study — latent general ability plausibly drives both any mediator and the reading outcome. Worse, intervention **dose** (sessions attended) is a _treatment-induced_ confounder of the mediator–outcome path, which means the natural direct and indirect effects are **not identified even by randomisation**, and simply adjusting for dose does not repair them (VanderWeele, Vansteelandt & Robins 2014). So read every NIE as _"how much of the reading gain is statistically **accounted for** by the route through mediator X"_, not _"X drives Y"_. This is the Table-2 fallacy in its sharpest form, and it applies to the direct part too. The one quantity comparable to a causal effect is the **total** — the model's estimate of the overall treatment→outcome effect, which lines up with the randomised ITT τ — but the _split_ of that total into direct and indirect parts is associational.

### The letter-sound route into word reading (the positive result)

The clearest finding of the family is that the word-reading gain is **statistically accounted for almost entirely by the route through letter-sound knowledge (LS)** — the count of letter–sound correspondences a child knows, out of 32.

**med-059** (concurrent L→W) puts the indirect part at NIE = +0.021 on the probability scale, about **+1.68 of 79 words** (89% CI +0.58 to +3.21; inner 50% +1.16 to +2.27), P(NIE > 0) = 0.997, _very strong_. The direct (not-through-L) part is essentially nothing: NDE +0.17 words (89% −1.70 to +2.07), P = 0.558 — _inconclusive_. So of a total of +1.95 words, the median share carried by letter sounds is ~0.82 (the `proportion_mediated` row, which is unstable and should not be read too literally because the total can approach zero). **med-078** is the interventional-effects companion to med-059 and returns identical numbers (IIE +1.68 words, P = 0.997) — by design, because it re-uses the same fitted mediator law; the agreement is an implementation check, not extra evidence.

**med-076** enforces time order — letter sounds measured at t2, word reading at t4 — and sharpens the picture into a classic _inconsistent-mediation_ pattern: NIE = +2.91 words (89% +1.12 to +5.28), P = 0.997 _very strong_, while the direct part is **negatively** signed, NDE = −1.66 words (89% −4.56 to +1.30), P(NDE < 0) = 0.817 (_suggestive_). The two nearly cancel, so the total is only +1.40 words and _inconclusive_ (P = 0.744). Read plainly: essentially all of the (modest) later word-reading advantage that co-travels with assignment is captured by the earlier letter-sound advantage, with the residual direct channel pointing the other way — a signature to treat cautiously at this sample size, but wholly consistent with a decoding route.

**med-092** is the period-stacked "gain-factor scaffold" (157 rows from 53 children, being-on-programme rather than assigned): NIE via L = +0.0095, about **+0.75 of 79 words** (89% +0.29 to +1.38), P = 0.998 _very strong_, alongside a positive direct part (+2.24 words). Different scaffold, smaller indirect share (~0.25), same direction.

### The vocabulary and grammar routes into word reading (the near-zero contrast)

Set against the letter-sound route, the vocabulary routes carry **little or nothing**. **med-068** (through taught-expressive vocabulary TE) gives NIE = +0.30 words (89% −0.23 to +1.32), P = 0.811 — _suggestive_ in direction but the interval spans zero, and the sensitivity analysis flags it as not credibly non-zero. **med-080** (through taught-receptive vocabulary TR) is similar: +0.21 words (89% −0.28 to +1.22), P = 0.754. **med-079** is a deliberate **negative control**: receptive grammar (RG) sits on a DAG-severed path, so its NIE should be ≈ 0 — and it is, +0.08 words (89% −0.19 to +0.63), P = 0.712, _inconclusive_; this calibrates how much apparent mediation residual general-ability confounding can manufacture (answer: very little here). **med-074** routes through nonword decoding (NW), itself floor-limited: NIE ≈ +0.02 words (89% −0.39 to +0.51), P = 0.561, _inconclusive_ — no discernible route. In each of these, the direct part carries the whole (positive) total.

### Head-to-head, two mediators in one model

The single-mediator contrast is confirmed _within_ one model, which is the cleaner comparison. **med-064** carries letter sounds (LS) and expressive vocabulary (EV) together: the route via **L** is +1.89 words (89% +0.62 to +3.63), P = 0.996 _very strong_; the route via **E** is +0.03 words (89% −0.47 to +0.75), P = 0.583 _inconclusive_ — indistinguishable from zero. **med-066** pits L against phoneme blending (PA): via **L** +1.62 words (P = 0.994), via **B** −0.03 words (P = 0.423) — again the vocabulary/blending co-mediator adds nothing once letter sounds are in the model. **med-075** is the sequential-code variant (letter sounds → blending → reading) and reproduces med-066 almost exactly: via L +1.62 words (P = 0.995), via B −0.03 (P = 0.419). Across all three, the **joint** NIE (+1.57 to +1.95 words, P = 0.984–0.992) is essentially the letter-sound leg on its own.

### Downstream: letter sounds into other decoding skills

Letter-sound knowledge also statistically accounts for movement in the other code-related outcomes. **med-086** (L → nonword reading N, an off-floor risk difference because most children score zero): NIE = **+8.7 percentage points** in the chance of coming off the floor (89% +2.8 to +16.0), P = 0.993 _very strong_, while the direct part is null (−1.1 pp, P = 0.419). **med-186** is its interventional companion — identical (IIE +8.7 pp). **med-087** (L → phoneme blending B, /10): NIE = +0.23 of 10 (89% −0.03 to +0.59), P = 0.921 _moderate_ — directionally supported but the 89% interval just includes zero; **med-187** matches it (IIE +0.23). **med-062** uses a composite code-based-route mediator (letter sounds + blending) for word reading and gives NIE = +0.92 words (89% +0.07 to +2.19), P = 0.961 _moderate_ — the same story with the two code skills bundled, though (see caveats) this is the most fragile fit to unmeasured confounding.

### Reverse-direction checks (does word reading carry the letter-sound gain?)

Mediation cannot by itself fix the _direction_ of a mediator→outcome link, so two models run the arrow the other way. **med-176** (word reading at t2 → letter sounds at t4) finds a non-trivial reverse route: NIE = +0.45 of 32 letter sounds (89% +0.04 to +1.11), P = 0.963 _moderate_. **med-276** repeats it with the outcome at t3 (less ceilinged): NIE = +0.50 of 32 (89% +0.05 to +1.23), P = 0.966 _moderate_. So word reading _also_ statistically accounts for part of the letter-sound gain. This bidirectionality is exactly what one expects when two skills co-develop and share latent ability, and it is the reason the family stops short of a directional causal claim: the forward route (L→W) is the larger and better-supported story in item terms, but the reverse route is not negligible, so the split is a description of _shared movement_, not proof that letter sounds _cause_ word reading.

## What the family concludes

Read as association, the family speaks with one voice: **whatever moves word reading in this cohort moves with letter-sound knowledge, not with vocabulary.** The letter-sound route into word reading is _very strong_ and reasonably sized (~+1.7 to +2.9 of 79 words across the concurrent, longitudinal and period-stacked specifications); the vocabulary routes (taught-expressive, taught-receptive, broad expressive) and the grammar negative control are all indistinguishable from zero, and the two-mediator models show the letter-sound leg swallowing the joint indirect effect whole. Letter sounds likewise account for the off-floor nonword-reading movement (+8.7 pp) and some of the blending gain.

This triangulates tightly with the other families. The **ITT** suite finds the intervention's largest randomised effect on letter sounds (τ ≈ +3.5 of 32, _very strong_), a solid word-reading effect (τ ≈ +2.4 of 79, _strong_), and flat broad vocabulary (R and E); the **mechanism** and **decoding-specificity** notes tell the same code-over-lexicon story. Mediation adds the "how much travels through what" layer: the randomised word-reading benefit is decoding-mediated, and the vocabulary channel — even the _taught_ vocabulary the programme explicitly drills — is not a detectable conduit to reading over this window. The g-formula totals (~+1.4 to +3.0 words) sit in the same range as the ITT word-reading τ, so the decomposition is anchored to a real effect; it is only the _partition_ that is associational.

## Caveats and convergence

- **Identification, not just precision.** As stated up front, natural NDE/NIE are not identified here even under randomisation, because intervention dose is a treatment-induced mediator–outcome confounder, and the no-unmeasured-confounding assumption is untestable with latent general ability in play. The interventional companions (med-078/186/187) target a weaker-assumption estimand that side-steps the dose obstacle, but they do **not** remove residual ability confounding and, in this fully-parametric implementation, return numbers identical to their natural-effect parents. Treat all splits as model-based decompositions under stated assumptions.
- **Robustness to unmeasured mediator–outcome confounding** (`mediation_sensitivity_summary.csv`, a Bayesian E-value-style sweep — how large a spurious mediator→outcome association would have to be to pull the indirect interval to zero, expressed as a fraction of the fitted slope). The letter-sound routes are the most robust: med-059/078 tip at ~52% of the fitted L→W slope, med-076 at ~60%, med-086/186 at ~52%, med-092 at ~62%. The two-mediator L legs tip at ~45–52% (joint at ~37–45%). The **reverse** routes are more fragile — med-176/276 tip at only ~30% — and **med-062** (composite mediator) is the most fragile of the credible set, tipping at just ~16% of its fitted slope. The routes already flagged _inconclusive_ or interval-crossing-zero (med-068, 074, 079, 080, 087/187, and the E/B legs of the two-mediator models) have "no non-zero indirect effect to explain away".
- **Small N and the winner's curse.** With 50–54 children (157 stacked rows for med-092), lead with the intervals; single-threshold point estimates are on average inflated. The `proportion_mediated` row is unstable whenever the total effect can cross zero (conspicuously med-076, where it exceeds 1, and the off-floor med-086/186) — do not headline it.
- **Floor effects.** Nonword reading (NW) is scored on very few items and mostly at the floor, so med-086/186 report an off-floor **risk difference** rather than an item count, and med-074 (N as a _mediator_) is detection-limited.
- **Temporal ordering.** Where available, a t3 sensitivity table re-runs the split with the mediator measured before the outcome as a direction check; the explicitly longitudinal models (med-076, and the reverse med-176/276) already enforce that ordering by design, and point the same way as their concurrent counterparts.
- All 18 models cleared the convergence gate cleanly (0 divergences throughout); med-092 has the weakest but still passing diagnostics (max R-hat 1.0032, minimum effective sample size ≈ 2,880).
