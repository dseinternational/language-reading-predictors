## Construct

``blending`` is a phoneme-blending score on a 0–10 scale from a
phonological awareness task: the examiner pronounces a word's
constituent phonemes with a short gap between each, and the child
selects which of three pictures depicts the blended word.
Phonological awareness measure — a precursor to phonological
decoding (``nonword``, LRP13/14) rather than a decoding measure
itself.

## Cross-model observation

**LRP16 is the first model in the suite where the tuned tree-
regime's construct cluster dominates without vocabulary
interference.** LRP10 (CELF concepts), LRP12 (TROG grammar) both
routed level prediction through vocabulary measures even though
construct-matched siblings were in the predictor set. LRP16
(blending) routes through reading + phonology — construct-
aligned — with vocabulary only appearing at positions 2
(`b1reto`) and 8 (`eowpvt`).

Possible explanation: phonological awareness is measurably
distinct from vocabulary in this sample (the construct-separation
is strong), whereas grammar and concept-knowledge may be
harder to disentangle from vocabulary at n=215.
