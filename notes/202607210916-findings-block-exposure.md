## What this family probes

The vocabulary component of the intervention teaches its target words in two consecutive **blocks**: a first set of words (block 1) is taught for a period, then a second, different set (block 2) is taught in a later period. Because the block-2 words are only actively taught during that second window, the family asks a **parallel-trends** question: does a child score higher on the block-2 words in the window when block-2 teaching is _active_ than in the earlier window when only block-1 teaching was running? The quantity reported is the difference in the block-2 outcome between block-2-active periods and block-1-active periods, translated into items on the relevant block-2 word list.

The four models cover the four block-2 vocabulary measures: the directly-taught expressive words (`bx-001`, TE2), the directly-taught receptive words (`bx-002`, TR2), and the two matched _not-taught_ generalisation measures — expressive (`bx-003`, UE2) and receptive (`bx-004`, UR2). The "not-taught" measures use words the intervention does not teach, so they gauge transfer beyond the specific taught set. Each model stacks all four phases for the roughly 54 children (159 observations for `bx-001`/`bx-002`/`bx-003`; 158 for `bx-004`) and fits the outcome as a count of correct items on a proportion-correct (logit) scale, adjusting for a DAG-pre-specified set of covariates — general cognitive ability, a block-2 vocabulary-exposure/teaching term, hearing status and linear age (with a phonology composite in the expressive models) — before reading off the exposure difference. Those covariate coefficients are not surfaced in the ground truth used for this note, so they are described qualitatively only and no covariate numbers are quoted here; in the previous fit the general-ability and block-2 vocabulary-exposure terms were the dominant, consistently positive signals, and the exposure difference itself was comparatively weak.

**This family is exploratory and nothing in it is causal.** Which words fell in block 1 versus block 2, and the staggered schedule on which the blocks were taught, were **not randomised** the way arm assignment was. The exposure difference is therefore identified only under a **parallel-trends assumption** — that the block-2-active and block-1-active groups would have tracked together on the block-2 words absent the difference in exposure. That assumption is untestable here, so every number below is an **adjusted association** describing who scores higher, not a treatment effect, and residual confounding by latent general ability remains.

## How to read these numbers

Each model's headline is a **difference in items** on the block-2 word list, comparing periods when block-2 teaching was active with periods when only block-1 teaching was active. A positive difference means higher block-2 scores when block-2 teaching is active. The **point estimate is the posterior median** and the uncertainty is the **89% equal-tailed credible interval** (the range containing the value with 89% posterior probability — the Bayesian counterpart of a confidence interval, but a direct probability statement about the quantity). **Direction is read from the tail probability** (e.g. "83% probability of a positive association"), never from whether the interval excludes zero and never as a p-value. That probability is graded on the fixed evidence ladder — inconclusive below 0.75, suggestive from 0.75, moderate from 0.91, strong from 0.97, very strong from 0.99 — and the label grades confidence in the _direction_, not the _size_, of the difference. Because this is a non-randomised parallel-trends contrast, the difference is an **association, not a causal effect**.

## What to take away

The block-2 exposure family is exploratory triangulation, and its headline exposure differences are **modest and, for three of the four measures, not confidently signed**. Only the directly-taught expressive measure (`bx-001`) leans positive as one might hope (suggestive), the directly-taught receptive measure (`bx-002`) leans the other way (suggestive negative), and both not-taught generalisation measures (`bx-003`, `bx-004`) are inconclusive. Every credible range straddles zero, so none of these differences is "big enough to matter" on the evidence here. Crucially, **none is a causal effect**: block-2 exposure was not randomised, the differences rest on an untestable parallel-trends assumption, and they remain open to residual confounding by latent general ability. These models add a consistency check around the taught-vocabulary story rather than a new claim, and they do not overturn the main intention-to-treat analyses.
