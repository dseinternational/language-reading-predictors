# LRP02 retrospective refits under the n_estimators cap-fix

> [!WARNING]
> These notes were generated by an AI tool.

Date: 2026-04-17

## Context

PR #16 fixed a pre-existing bug in `RunConfig.n_estimators` semantics:
the `test` / `dev` presets _replaced_ the model's tuned
`n_estimators` with their preset value (500 / 100) rather than capping
it. As a consequence, every LRP02 CV number in merged PRs
#12 / #13 / #14 / #15 was computed at 500 trees regardless of the
value the Optuna tuner had selected. Some variants were tuned well
below 500 (e.g. `lrp02_log_prediction` at 28 trees, `lrp02` at 53)
and were being massively inflated at fit time — i.e. allowed to
over-fit.

Comparisons remained self-consistent (all models ran at 500) but the
tuned `n_estimators` values pinned in `models/lrp02.py` were never
honoured in the CV pipeline. This PR retroactively refits all seven
LRP02 variants at their tuned `n_estimators` and reports the impact.

## Method

1. Snapshot the previous 500-tree CV metrics from each
   `output/models/{mid}/metrics.json` into `/tmp/lrp02_before_refit.csv`.
2. Run `python scripts/fit_model.py <mid> --config test` for each of
   the seven LRP02 variants. The cap-fix (PR #16) now honours the
   tuned `n_estimators`.
3. Recompute paired-fold tests via `scripts/compare_variants.py` on
   the fresh per-fold CV scores.

No hyperparameter re-tuning — only the refit under the cap-fix. Any
change in CV metrics is entirely attributable to using the tuned
`n_estimators` instead of the 500-tree cap.

### Pattern

- **MAE-tuned exploratory variants** (`lrp02`, `lrp02_select02`,
  `lrp02_log`, `lrp02_select02_log`, `lrp02_log_select`) mostly show
  small _increases_ in CV MAE (+0.05 to +0.26). Fewer trees give the
  model less capacity to fit the mid-range; the net effect is a
  slight mean-error penalty. R² and MedAE move in noisy small
  directions. **In-sample R² drops uniformly by 2–8 percentage
  points** — the models are no longer memorising.
- **RMSE-tuned prediction variants** (`lrp02_prediction`,
  `lrp02_log_prediction`) show **large CV improvements**. The 500-tree
  fit with squared-error loss had been severely over-fitting; the
  tuned 28-72 trees are vastly more honest.
  - `lrp02_log_prediction` CV R² jumps from 0.262 to **0.388** (+0.126)
    and in-sample R² drops from 0.992 to 0.854 — classic
    overfit-correction signature.
  - `lrp02_prediction` CV R² rises 0.299 → 0.357 (+0.059); in-sample
    1.000 → 0.962.

## What the cap-fix teaches us

1. **The log-transform win is robust.** `lrp02` → `lrp02_log` MedAE
   improvement survives: `−0.877`, p=0.005 / 0.006, 9/10 folds wins.
   It is the single most reliable finding across all LRP02 work.
2. **The "RMSE objective over-fits at n=210" claim was partly an
   artefact of the override bug.** At tuned `n_estimators` the RMSE
   variants are competitive — not uniformly worse. The prior
   significant-worse results were largely driven by being forced to
   train 500 trees with squared-error loss on a long-tailed target.
   At their tuned 28-72 trees, RMSE variants are honest and much
   closer to parity with MAE variants.
3. **Overall ranking is preserved.** `lrp02_log` remains the best
   variant on CV MAE (5.63) and CV MedAE (3.21). `lrp02_select02_log`
   is basically tied on CV R² (0.492 vs 0.489). `lrp02_log_select`
   sits slightly behind. None of the earlier decisions (log-space
   first selection, RMSE variants as reference only, etc.) are
   overturned.
4. **`lrp02` is now the weakest exploratory model** (R² 0.314). At
   53 trees it is both less expressive than it was at 500 and the
   weakest CV performer among MAE-tuned variants. The log transform
   is what separates the good from the middling.

## Implications for the recorded findings

The LRP02 notes file (`202604161949-lrp02-feature-selection.md`)
contains many specific numbers — CV MAE 6.019, 5.581, 5.735, etc. —
that correspond to the 500-tree fits. Those numbers were
_self-consistent_ (all variants compared at 500 trees) but not the
tuned-n_estimators numbers. Two options:

- **Leave the notes as historical record.** The conclusions drawn
  (log transform helps; primary-space selection is robust to the
  transform; RMSE variants are reference-only) remain valid under
  the cap-fix.
- **Edit the notes to reflect cap-fix numbers.** More accurate but
  rewrites merged history.

This PR takes the first path: the earlier LRP02 notes stand as a
record of what was believed at the time, and this new notes file is
the corrected post-fix record. The "Current state" section at the
