# LRP01 signed-log target transform — marginal improvement

> [!WARNING]
> These notes were generated by an AI tool.

Date: 2026-04-17

## Motivation

The LRP02 log-transform investigation
(`notes/202604161949-lrp02-feature-selection.md`) showed that fitting
`log1p(ewrswr)` instead of raw `ewrswr` materially improves CV metrics
(CV R² +0.07, CV MedAE −20%). That benefit was driven by three
properties of the target: hard floor at 0, heavy right skew, and
training gradients dominated by the tail.

LRP01 predicts `ewrswr_gain` — the *change* in single-word reading
score between consecutive timepoints. A natural question: does the
same log-transform benefit apply?

## Why signed-log, not log1p

Inspection of `ewrswr_gain` (n=157, lrp01's filtered data):

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| min | −4 |
| max | 21 |
| median | 2 |
| mean | 3.46 |
| std | 4.31 |
| skewness | 1.33 |

| Sign | Count | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Negative | 19 | 12.1% |
| Zero | 24 | 15.3% |
| Positive | 114 | 72.6% |

Gains can be negative (children can regress between timepoints), so
plain `log1p(y)` is NaN on 12% of observations. The natural alternative
is **signed log**:

$$
\operatorname{signed\_log1p}(y) = \operatorname{sign}(y) \cdot \log_1\!p(|y|)
$$

with inverse $\operatorname{sign}(x) \cdot (\exp(|x|) - 1)$. This:

- Preserves the sign (negative gains remain negative in transformed
  space).
- Keeps the natural zero ($0 \mapsto 0$).
- Compresses both tails symmetrically around zero.

Spot-check round-trip on representative values:

| y | signed_log1p(y) | signed_expm1(x) |
|---|---|---|
| −4 | −1.6094 | −4 |
| −1 | −0.6931 | −1 |
| 0 | 0 | 0 |
| 2 | 1.0986 | 2 |
| 21 | 3.0910 | 21 |

The range [−4, 21] compresses to roughly [−1.6, 3.1] — meaningful
compression of the positive tail, modest on the negative side.

## Target pathology is milder than LRP02's

The in-sample quartile MAE pattern on the primary `lrp01`:

| Target range | In-sample MAE |
|---|---|
| (−4, 0] | 2.32 |
| (0, 2] | 0.70 |
| (2, 6] | 1.17 |
| (6, 21] | **6.82** |

The top-quartile error is ~10× the low-positive quartile — a real
"error-scales-with-magnitude" pattern, but with a much smaller target
range (span 25) than LRP02's `ewrswr` (span 64, top-quartile error
2.72 vs low-quartile 0.43). The compression opportunity is real but
more modest.

## Implementation

### `LGBMSignedLogPipeline`

New pipeline subclass of `LGBMPipeline` in
`src/language_reading_predictors/models/lgbm_signed_log_pipeline.py`.
Mirrors the existing `LGBMLogPipeline` structure:

- Wraps the estimator in
  `TransformedTargetRegressor(func=signed_log1p, inverse_func=signed_expm1)`
  so predictions are inverse-transformed before scoring.
- Overrides `shap_analysis` to unwrap to the fitted inner regressor
  before calling `shap.TreeExplainer`.

The forward/inverse functions are exported as module-level
`signed_log1p` / `signed_expm1` helpers.

### Tuner integration

`scripts/tune_model.py` was already set up for pluggable target
transforms from the LRP02 work. Extended:

- Added `"signed_log"` entry to `_TARGET_TRANSFORMS` (forward /
  inverse pair).
- Added `"LGBMSignedLogPipeline"` → `"signed_log"` to
  `_PIPELINE_DEFAULT_TRANSFORM` so the transform is auto-inferred
  from the model's `pipeline_cls`.

Invocation:

```bash
python scripts/tune_model.py lrp01_log --n-trials 150 --scoring mae --lgbm-objective mae
```

## Pre-existing bug: `n_estimators` override semantics

While debugging this variant (tuned at 19 trees initially, refitting
to CV MAE 3.147 under test config) I discovered that the `test`
`RunConfig` *replaces* `n_estimators` with 500 rather than capping.
Every model's tuned `n_estimators` was being ignored during
`fit_model.py --config test`.

Fixed in this PR: all three pipeline classes now treat
`run.n_estimators` as an upper cap:

```python
if run.n_estimators is not None:
    tuned = lgbm_params.get("n_estimators", run.n_estimators)
    lgbm_params["n_estimators"] = min(tuned, run.n_estimators)
```

`save_config()` in `base_pipeline.py` mirrors the cap semantics so
`config.json` reports the effective value actually fitted.

**Retrospective implication for LRP02:** all prior LRP02 CV numbers
in merged PRs (#12 #13 #14 #15) were fitted at 500 trees regardless of
tuned `n_estimators`. The comparisons remain self-consistent (all
models ran at 500) but none of them actually used their tuned
`n_estimators`. Refitting LRP02 variants under the cap-fix is a
follow-up and is out of scope for this PR.

## Tuning

Optuna 150 trials, 10-split GroupKFold, seed 47, scoring=mae,
lgbm_objective=mae, target_transform=signed_log.

### Tuner trajectory

All 150 trials cluster in 2.7404 to 2.9477 (std 0.037) on tuner-inner
CV MAE. Best trial #112 picked `n_estimators=19` — suspiciously low.
The top 15 trials span `best_iter` from 18 to 71, showing the tuner
is indifferent between "few shallow trees" and "moderate deeper
trees" regimes.

### Refit with top candidates

Direct CV (no inner early stopping) at tuned `n_estimators`:

|---|---|---|---|---|
| trial #112 (tuner's best) | 19 | 4 | 2.793 ± 0.649 | 0.033 |
| trial #13 | **54** | **8** | **2.770 ± 0.614** | 0.024 |
| trial #3 | 50 | 8 | 2.778 | 0.043 |

Extending #112's depth-4 regime to more trees degrades CV

## Quartile in-sample MAE

| Target range | `lrp01` | `lrp01_log` | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| (−4, 0] | 2.32 | **2.00** | −14% |
| (0, 2] | 0.70 | 0.68 | −3% |
| (2, 6] | 1.17 | 1.22 | +4% |
| (6, 21] | 6.82 | **6.73** | −1% |

Signed-log mostly helps the **negative-gains quartile** (−14%).
Top-quartile improvement is negligible. This is consistent with the
symmetric compression the transform applies: the biggest magnitude
residuals on the negative side (gains down to −4) get pulled toward
zero more than positive extremes in the 6–21 range.

## Conclusion

Signed-log transform on `ewrswr_gain`:

1. **Marginal CV MAE improvement** (−0.032, 9/10 folds, Wilcoxon
   p=0.064) — directional but not significant at α=0.05.
2. Other CV metrics **unchanged**.
3. Importance rankings **preserved**.
4. Biggest quartile gain is on **negative gains** (−14% in-sample MAE).
5. **Nothing close to LRP02's log-transform impact** (CV R² +0.07,
   MedAE −20%). The milder skew of `ewrswr_gain` (1.33 vs heavier on
   `ewrswr`) limits how much tail compression can help.

`lrp01_log` is retained as a variant for completeness and for
practitioners who care specifically about MAE on negative-gain cases.
`lrp01` remains the primary model.
