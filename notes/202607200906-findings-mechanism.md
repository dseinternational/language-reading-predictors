This note reports the full re-fit (production `reporting` configuration: 6000 draws × 6000 tune × 6 chains) of the **mechanism** family — 27 models that describe the _shape_ of one measured skill's association with another. It is a companion to the shared reading guide `notes/202607200900-findings-00-index-and-reading-guide.md`; read that first for the study background, the outcome measures and their maxima, and the house reporting standard. Every figure here is traceable to a model's own output CSVs in `output/statistical_models/models/<id>-reporting/`. Work in progress; all data are preliminary.

## What this family does, and the question it answers

The study is a **waitlist-crossover randomised trial**: about 54 children with Down syndrome were randomly assigned to receive a reading/phonics intervention immediately or after a wait, and were tested at four timepoints. The mechanism family does **not** use randomisation. Instead each model asks a descriptive question about how two _measured_ skills go together: **"among children who are otherwise alike, how much higher is skill Y when skill X is higher, and what is the shape of that relationship?"** X is called the exposure (the earlier or upstream skill), Y the outcome.

Each model stacks every measurement phase and fits a curve of the outcome on the exposure, with a **subject random intercept** (a per-child offset that partially pools stable between-child differences so repeated measures on the same child are not double-counted — it is _not_ a control for latent general ability), adjusting for the covariates the study's causal diagram (**DAG** — a graph that encodes which variables to adjust for) pre-specifies. It then reports the outcome difference on the **items scale** (test units) from the bottom to the top of the exposure's observed range, plus, for the flexible curves, the exposure count at which the outcome rises fastest (the "knee", or **readiness threshold**).

This is what the other families do not give you: the _scaffolding map_ of the reading system — which skill tracks which, how steeply, and whether the relationship is roughly straight-line or has a threshold. It is the observational complement to the causal families. Crucially, **nothing in this family is causal.** Every slope, curve, knee and interaction here is an **adjusted association**, describing _who progresses_, not a lever you can pull. The randomised causal claims live in the ITT suite (`notes/202607200901-findings-itt-suite.md`) and its difference-in-differences and gain-factor re-analyses; the causal split of the reading gain is attempted (under stronger assumptions) by the mediation family.

## How to read these numbers (recap)

Full conventions are in the shared reading guide. In brief:

- The point estimate is the posterior **median** (the "posterior" is the full probability distribution for a quantity given data and priors; the median is preferred because it does not change when the effect is re-expressed from the model's logit/log-odds scale to a probability). With ~54 children, lead with the **interval**, not the point.
- Uncertainty is the **89% equal-tailed credible interval** ("89% posterior probability the value lies in this range"), with the inner **50%** interval as the "most-of-the-mass" range. 89%, not 95%, is the deliberate house standard.
- Direction is the **tail probability** P(association > 0), read directly, never as a p-value. Its **evidence label** is fixed by round odds: _inconclusive_ (< 0.75), _suggestive_ (≥ 0.75), _moderate_ (≥ 0.91), _strong_ (≥ 0.97), _very strong_ (≥ 0.99). The label grades the _evidence for a directional claim_; it never describes the _size_ of the effect.
- Effects are modelled on a probability (proportion-correct) scale and translated to **items** by multiplying by the outcome test's maximum. Each headline below is the items difference across the exposure's _observed range_; because the ranges and the outcome maxima differ enormously, the raw items numbers are **not comparable across rows** (a swing across a wider range is not "more important"). The percentage-point figure (pp = items ÷ outcome maximum) is added so the size can be read honestly. Note the nonword-reading outcome (N) has only 6 items, so its pp figures look large.
- **Causal versus association.** Nothing here is causal. Reading a mechanism slope, a moderator's own coefficient, or an adjustment-set covariate as if it were a lever is the **Table-2 fallacy**, and it is wrong for every quantity in this family. Residual confounding by latent general ability remains throughout; the child random intercept pools stable individual differences but does not stand in for general ability. Adjustment sets are fixed in advance from the DAG, so a skill absent from a model was excluded by the diagram, not found unimportant.

### Plain dose-response curves (15 models)

**The flagship: letter-sound knowledge → word reading (mech-058, GP curve).** This is the single most important curve in the family. "GP" means the curve is fitted with a **Gaussian process** — a flexible, nonparametric form that lets the data choose the shape rather than forcing a straight line. Across the observed range of letter-sound knowledge (about 2 to 32 of 32 sounds) predicted word reading rises from roughly **5.3 items to 10.8 items (of 79)**, a difference of **+6.7 items [89% CI +2.6, +10.9], ≈ +8.5 percentage points**, with **P(> 0) = 0.996 — very strong** evidence the association is positive. A more conservative within-data contrast (a child at the 75th versus 25th percentile of letter-sound knowledge, 28 vs 17 sounds) is **+2.5 word-reading items [89% CI +0.8, +4.0]**. The shape is a gentle rise that **accelerates near the top**: predicted word reading is ≈ 5.9 items at 10 sounds, ≈ 7.1 at 18, ≈ 8.4 at 25, then ≈ 10.8 at 32. The **readiness threshold (knee)** — the count where word reading rises fastest per extra letter sound — sits at about **29–30 of 32 (89% CrI 20 to ~30, well-defined: the curve rises in 99.9% of posterior draws)**, close to the top of the scale (the upper limit pins at the observed maximum, so read it as "near letter-sound mastery"). Importantly, the **below-knee slope is clearly positive** (≈ 0.030 logit per sound versus ≈ 0.058 above), so word reading is _already climbing_ well before the knee — this is an accelerating rise, **not** a flat plateau that switches on at a threshold. Read plainly: children with more letter-sound knowledge tend to read more words, the relationship strengthens as they approach mastering the full set of sounds, and the whole thing is an **adjusted association**, not evidence that teaching more sounds _causes_ the extra reading (that causal question is the ITT suite's, and the mediation family's decomposition, to answer).

**Letter-sound knowledge → word reading, comparators (mech-101, mech-158, mech-173).** Three models re-estimate the same L→W relationship under different specifications and all agree. **mech-101** is the deliberately simple **linear** anchor: a straight-line +9.9 word-reading items across the range [+6.0, +13.5], P(> 0) = 1.000, very strong. **mech-158** is the **complete-case comparator** (43 children with no imputed confounders): +6.9 items [+2.4, +11.4], P(> 0) = 0.996, knee ≈ 29–30/32 — essentially identical to mech-058, so the imputation of missing confounders is not driving the result. **mech-173** adds an age main effect (the no-interaction baseline for the age-moderation model mech-073): +6.6 items [+2.5, +10.9], P(> 0) = 0.996, knee ≈ 29–30/32; age's own adjusted coefficient is mildly negative (median −0.100 [−0.177, −0.024], P(> 0) = 0.019 — older children slightly lower on word reading at a given letter-sound level, an association, not a lever). The L→W association is robust across all four specifications.

**Vocabulary → word reading (mech-056 R, mech-057 E).** Broad standardised vocabulary shows a weaker, less certain association with word reading than letter sounds do. Receptive vocabulary (mech-056, linear) gives +2.9 word-reading items across its range [−2.6, +8.2], P(> 0) = 0.803 — only _suggestive_, with the interval straddling zero. Expressive vocabulary (mech-057, linear) gives +5.3 items [−0.5, +10.9], P(> 0) = 0.927 — _moderate_. Both are far less resolved than the letter-sound curve, and (see the knee-tests below) neither survives a flexible re-fit.

**Taught vocabulary → word reading (mech-088 TR, mech-089 TE).** The specific words the intervention teaches track word reading strongly: taught-receptive vocabulary (mech-088, linear) +9.0 items [+4.9, +13.1], P(> 0) = 1.000; taught-expressive (mech-089, linear) +8.6 items [+3.2, +13.4], P(> 0) = 0.993 — both _very strong_. These are among the steepest associations in the family, but note taught vocabulary is itself an intervention target, so the association partly reflects shared exposure to teaching rather than a distinct skill channel.

**Phonological memory → word reading (mech-090, linear).** Phonological memory (word/nonword repetition, RW; entered as a raw-score covariate, not a bounded count) shows +3.1 word-reading items across its raw range [+0.1, +6.0], P(> 0) = 0.952 — _moderate_, with the interval only just clearing zero.

**Letter sounds → nonword reading (mech-096, linear; mech-172 baseline).** The decoding channel: letter-sound knowledge tracks nonword reading (N, decoding of unfamiliar letter strings) very strongly. mech-096 gives +3.7 of 6 nonword items across the letter-sound range [+2.8, +4.5], P(> 0) = 1.000. mech-172 is the two-predictor baseline (letter sounds plus a phoneme-blending main effect, the no-interaction companion to mech-072): +3.3 nonword items for letter sounds [+2.3, +4.2], P(> 0) = 1.000, and the blending main effect is positive (median +0.281 [+0.024, +0.531], P(> 0) = 0.958, _moderate_ — an adjusted association). Because N has only 6 items these look enormous in percentage points (≈ 62pp, ≈ 55pp), so read them as "letter-sound knowledge tracks almost the full nonword range", not as a huge absolute-item effect.

**Letter sounds → broad language "negative-control" outcomes (mech-097 R, mech-098 E, mech-099 T, mech-100 F).** These four models were included as **negative controls**: if letter-sound knowledge specifically drove _word reading_, it should show little association with skills it has no direct channel to. Instead all four "light up" with _very strong_ positive associations — L→R +16.2 vocabulary items [+9.2, +23.3]; L→E +13.8 [+7.5, +20.1]; L→T (receptive grammar) +4.6 [+1.6, +7.5]; L→F (basic concepts) +5.2 [+2.9, +7.4], every P(> 0) ≥ 0.994. This is an important interpretive flag rather than a discovery: a skill that predicts _everything_ after adjustment is behaving like a marker of **shared general ability**, so the letter-sound associations (including the flagship L→W) are partly carried by a common factor the model cannot remove. It is the clearest in-family reminder that these are associations, not specific causal channels.

### Interaction / moderation models (6 models)

These fit the L→W (or L→N) curve and let a second skill (the _moderator_) shift its steepness. Two coefficients matter: **`gamma_mod`**, the moderator's own adjusted association with the outcome, and **`gamma_int`**, the interaction — _positive_ means the mechanism association is **stronger** at higher moderator values (synergy/complementary), _negative_ means **weaker** (substitutive/compensatory), and a coefficient whose direction is undecided means **additive** (the two skills contribute independently). The recurring pattern across the five L→W models is that the moderators' _own_ associations are clearly positive, while the _interaction_ is consistently slightly **negative but weak** — i.e. the skills are close to additive, with a mild substitutive lean.

- **mech-071 (L→W moderated by expressive vocabulary E).** gamma_mod +0.191 [+0.078, +0.303], P(> 0) = 0.997 (_very strong_: higher E, higher word reading). gamma_int −0.065 [−0.152, +0.019], P(> 0) = 0.110, so P(< 0) = 0.89 — _suggestive_ substitutive lean: the letter-sound slope is a little shallower for children with stronger expressive vocabulary, but the evidence is weak and the interval includes zero. Essentially additive.
- **mech-093 (× receptive vocabulary R).** gamma_mod +0.113 [−0.001, +0.227], P(> 0) = 0.943 (_moderate_). gamma_int −0.053 [−0.136, +0.027], P(< 0) = 0.85 — _suggestive_, additive-to-mildly-substitutive.
- **mech-094 (× taught-receptive vocabulary TR).** gamma_mod +0.186 [+0.084, +0.288], P(> 0) = 0.998 (_very strong_). gamma_int −0.062 [−0.138, +0.015], P(< 0) = 0.90 — _suggestive_ substitutive lean; additive.
- **mech-095 (× taught-expressive vocabulary TE).** gamma_mod +0.211 [+0.101, +0.322], P(> 0) = 0.999 (_very strong_). gamma_int −0.085 [−0.173, +0.002], P(< 0) = 0.94 — _moderate_ substitutive lean, the strongest of the L→W set, but still with the interval touching zero. Gate: divergence-only (2 divergences), usable.
- **mech-073 (× age).** gamma_mod (age) −0.069 [−0.152, +0.013], P(< 0) = 0.91 — _suggestive_/_moderate_ negative (older children slightly lower, an association). gamma_int −0.057 [−0.119, +0.006], P(< 0) = 0.93 — _moderate_: the letter-sound→word-reading association is somewhat weaker for older children in this sample. Read as adjusted description, not a claim that ageing weakens the mechanism.
- **mech-072 (L→N moderated by phoneme blending B).** This is the one clear interaction in the family. gamma_mod (blending) +0.361 [+0.095, +0.625], P(> 0) = 0.985 (_strong_). gamma_int **−0.327 [−0.570, −0.093], P(< 0) = 0.987 — strong evidence of a substitutive interaction**: letter-sound knowledge and blending partly _stand in for_ each other in predicting nonword decoding — a child strong on one gains less decoding advantage from also being strong on the other. This is consistent with two partly redundant routes into the same skill, and is the family's most decisive moderation result (though still an association).

### GP "knee-test" re-attempts (6 models)

These re-fit six exposures with the flexible Gaussian-process form specifically to test for a knee — a count above which the outcome takes off. For a knee to be trustworthy the curve must rise in nearly all posterior draws (`increasing_frac` > 0.9); below that the knee is ill-defined. Per the brief, the small divergence counts these models carry are themselves part of the finding: **the knee stays only weakly identified** in every one, so read them as "a knee is/ is not clearly supported", not as a precise location.

- **mech-156 (R → W).** 24 divergences. Headline +0.2 items [−1.6, +3.5], P(> 0) = 0.614 — _inconclusive_. The curve rises in only **59%** of draws, so **no knee is defined** and the relationship is effectively flat. This flatly fails to corroborate the linear mech-056 suggestive slope — the receptive-vocabulary→word-reading association does not survive a flexible re-fit.
- **mech-157 (E → W).** 16 divergences. +0.5 items [−1.1, +5.1], P(> 0) = 0.701 — _inconclusive_. Rises in only **58%** of draws; below-knee and above-knee slopes are both near zero (≈ 0.0018, ≈ 0.0012). **No knee**, effectively flat — and again this does not corroborate the linear mech-057 _moderate_ slope. Taken together, mech-156/157 are a caution that the apparent broad-vocabulary→word-reading associations are fragile once curvature is allowed.
- **mech-188 (TR → W).** 3 divergences. +3.6 items [+0.0, +8.3], P(> 0) = 0.950 — _moderate_. Rises in **98%** of draws (well-defined); knee ≈ **18 of 24 taught-receptive words** [89% CrI 14.5 to 19.75], with the curve climbing throughout (below-knee slope ≈ 0.033, above ≈ 0.042). A genuine, if modestly steepening, knee — the clearest of the knee-tests, caveated by 3 divergences.
- **mech-189 (TE → W).** 1 divergence. +1.8 items [−0.8, +7.9], P(> 0) = 0.826 — _suggestive_. Rises in **89%** of draws (borderline, treat cautiously); a possible early knee ≈ **7 of 24 taught-expressive words** [3.75, 14.5], after which the slope flattens (below ≈ 0.023, above ≈ 0.016). Weakly identified.
- **mech-190 (B → W, phoneme blending).** 31 divergences (the most in the family). +0.5 items [−0.8, +4.3], P(> 0) = 0.736 — _inconclusive_. Rises in only **76%** of draws; slopes near zero both sides (≈ 0.013). **No clear knee**, effectively flat — blending on its own does not trace a resolved word-reading curve (its role is clearer as a moderator in mech-072).
- **mech-191 (IS → W, intervention sessions attended).** 29 divergences. +2.1 items [+0.1, +4.2], P(> 0) = 0.965 — _moderate_. Rises in **97%** of draws, but the knee pins at the **lower boundary (≈ 40 sessions)** with the below-knee slope undefined, so the "steepest rise" is at the very start of attendance — a diminishing-returns shape, weakly identified. Attendance/dose is a special case: it is an adjusted association at best (and a collider elsewhere in the study), so this is emphatically not "more sessions cause more reading".

## What the family concludes

The mechanism family draws a consistent **developmental scaffolding map**, all in the language of association. The dominant, robust relationship is **letter-sound knowledge → word reading**: very strong across every specification (mech-058/101/158/173), steepening as children approach mastering the full set of sounds (knee ≈ 29–30 of 32), and echoed in the decoding channel **letter sounds → nonword reading** (mech-096/072/172). Letter sounds also track nonword decoding so tightly that the two partly substitute for phoneme blending (mech-072, the one strong interaction). By contrast, **broad standardised vocabulary → word reading is weak and fragile**: only suggestive/moderate as straight lines (mech-056/057) and effectively vanishing once the curve is allowed to bend (mech-156/157). Taught vocabulary tracks reading steeply (mech-088/089/188) but partly because it is itself a teaching target. Across the moderation set the moderators' own associations are positive while the interactions are near-additive with a weak substitutive lean (mech-071/093/094/095/073).

This triangulates cleanly with the causal families. The ITT suite finds the randomised benefit concentrated on the code-related and directly-taught skills (letter sounds, blending, word reading, taught expressive vocabulary) and inconclusive on broad vocabulary; the mechanism map explains _why the pieces fit_ — letter-sound knowledge is the skill most tightly wired to word reading and decoding — while the mediation family formally attributes the word-reading gain to the letter-sound route rather than the vocabulary route. The mechanism family cannot make that causal split itself: the "negative-control" models (mech-097/098/099/100) show letter-sound knowledge predicting vocabulary, grammar and basic concepts just as strongly, which flags that a shared general-ability factor inflates all of these associations. That is the family's own honesty check, and the reason its every number stays an adjusted association.

## Caveats & convergence

- **Nothing here is causal.** Every slope, curve, knee, moderator coefficient and adjustment-set covariate is an adjusted association. Do not read any of them as a lever (the Table-2 fallacy); the randomised causal estimates are in the ITT suite and its DiD/gain-factor re-analyses.
- **Residual confounding by general ability.** The child random intercept pools stable between-child differences but does not remove latent general ability; the negative-control models (mech-097–100) show directly that letter-sound knowledge is entangled with a general factor, so the letter-sound associations are upper bounds on any specific channel.
- **Small sample.** ~54 children (43 in the complete-case mech-158). Point estimates that just clear a threshold are on average inflated (winner's curse) — the intervals, not the points, carry the message, and several (mech-056, mech-090, several knee-tests) straddle or barely clear zero.
- **Gate.** Twenty models pass cleanly. Seven carry **divergence-only** flags (all pass R-hat, ESS and BFMI; divergences well under the 1% guidance): mech-095 (2), and the six knee-tests mech-156 (24), mech-157 (16), mech-188 (3), mech-189 (1), mech-190 (31), mech-191 (29). For mech-095 the interaction is usable with a note. For the knee-tests the divergence count is part of the finding: the knee is only weakly identified in each, so knee _locations_ should be read as approximate, and where the curve rises in fewer than ~90% of draws (mech-156, mech-157, mech-190) no knee is defined at all.
- **Scale comparability.** The headline items differences span different exposure ranges and different outcome maxima (N has only 6 items), so raw items are not comparable across rows; use the percentage-point figures and the per-model prose, not a ranking of the items column.
