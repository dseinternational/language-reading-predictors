## Construct

``deappfi`` is a percentage-scale articulation score from the
Diagnostic Evaluation of Articulation and Phonology (Dodd et
al., 2006). The child is asked to name pictures; ``deappfi``
scores the proportion of the *final* consonants correctly
produced (distinct from ``deappin`` initial consonants and
``deappvo`` voicing of target sounds). First articulation-
domain target in the suite — DEAP measures have been used as
predictors across every other model in LRP01–LRP20 but never
as targets until now.

## Cross-model observation: same-test-sibling dominance

Across the six pairs added in LRP11–LRP22, three level models
routed their primary signal through a same-test sibling:

| Model | Target | Top predictor | Importance | Spearman |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|
| LRP18 | `aptgram` | `aptinfo` | 0.392 | 0.990 |
| LRP20 | `aptinfo` | `aptgram` | 0.233 | 0.975 |
| LRP22 | `deappfi` | `deappin` | 0.688 | 0.929 |

The three pairs (APTGRAM/APTINFO from the Action Picture
Test utterance sample; DEAPPFI/DEAPPIN from the DEAP
picture-naming task) all show strong same-test-sibling
dominance. When derived from the same stimulus material, the
boosted model naturally routes most of the variance through
the partner score before falling back on general-construct
predictors.

For the Bayesian follow-up, this suggests joint models of
same-test pairs (e.g. bivariate emissions from a shared
latent articulation factor) will be more interpretable than
univariate models that treat same-test siblings as
exogenous predictors.

## Next-step candidates

- **Log/signed-log variant for LRP22.** The −0.87 skew from
  the ceiling motivates a signed-log transform (or a beta
  regression in the Bayesian phase) to recover the
  near-ceiling precision that the linear tree model cannot.
- **Select01 + construct-driven Select02 for LRP22**: drop
  `deappin` to ask "what predicts fine-articulation beyond
  the same-test partner?" — parallels the LRP18/LRP20
  Select02 pattern suggested in the APT pair notes.
- **Select01 on LRP21** — with only 19 trees the model is
  already heavily regularised, but a dcorr-informed cut of
  the long noise tail (ranks 20+ all at ≈0) should tighten
  further.
- **Joint DEAP model** — a multivariate Bayesian model for
  ``(deappfi, deappin, deappvo)`` mirroring the APT pair
  suggestion. Same rationale: same-test-sibling dominance is
  structurally informative, not nuisance.
- **Cross-construct Bayesian model**: the consistent
  articulation→phonological-memory→reading cascade visible
  across LRP13/14, LRP15/16, and LRP21/22 is a natural DAG
  to formalise.
