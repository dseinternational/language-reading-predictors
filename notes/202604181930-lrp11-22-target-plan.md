## Construct gaps vs. the study brief

Relative to "predictors of progress in language and reading skills
for children with Down syndrome":

1. **Grammar** — completely un-modelled as outcome. `trog`
   (receptive, TROG-2) and `aptgram` (expressive, APT) are used
   only as predictors.
2. **Phonological decoding** (`nonword`) — distinct reading
   subskill from `ewrswr`, clinically important for Down syndrome
   where decoding tends to lag sight-word recognition.
3. **Phonological awareness precursors** (`blending`,
   `spphon`) — upstream of reading.
4. **Articulation** (DEAP: `deappfi` / `deappin` / `deappvo`) —
   used as predictor across every model but never as target.
5. **Expressive information** (`aptinfo`) — included in several
   predictor sets, never target.

## Ranked next targets

### Top 3 (do these first)

**1. TROG (receptive grammar) — LRP11 (`trog_gain`) / LRP12 (`trog`)**
- Fills the grammar gap. `trog` is already well-characterised as a
  predictor across LRP01–LRP10, so Select01 should reuse established
  redundancy patterns quickly.
- Distribution: near-Gaussian (skew 0.29), full sample (n=215
  level / n=161 gain) — best-behaved candidate for a first
  gradient-boosting fit.
- Expected CV R² in the LRP08 / LRP04 range (0.50–0.55 level,
  0.10–0.20 gain).

**2. Non-word reading — LRP13 (`nonword_gain`) / LRP14 (`nonword`)**
- Phonological decoding — distinct reading subskill from
  `ewrswr` (word recognition). Clinically important for Down
  syndrome where decoding characteristically lags.
- Distribution: floor-heavy (median 0, skew 1.38), coarse 0–6
  scale. The shape is itself a research-relevant finding about
  the sample.
- Likely wants a log-transform variant alongside the primary
  (mirror LRP01 / LRP02 conventions) or a quantile objective.

**3. Phoneme blending — LRP15 (`blending_gain`) / LRP16 (`blending`)**
- Phonological-awareness precursor to reading. Symmetric
  distribution (skew 0.01, median 6, 0–10 scale).
- Coarse 0–10 scale likely caps achievable R² (see CELF's
  0–18 parallel). Expected modest R² at level (~0.40) and low
  at gain.

### Second tier (do after top 3)

**4. APTGRAM (expressive grammar) — LRP17 / LRP18**
- Expressive-side parallel to TROG. Strong right-skew (1.23,
  median 6, 0–28 scale) means a log variant is effectively
  mandatory — mirror LRP01's `lrp01_log` approach.
- Important for research framing: grammar expressive vs
  receptive asymmetry is a live question in DS language
  research.

**5. APTINFO (expressive information) — LRP19 / LRP20**
- From the same Action Picture Test as APTGRAM but scores
  information content rather than syntax. Clean distribution
  (skew 0.24, 0–37.5 scale). Less-commonly-referenced
  construct than grammar or vocabulary — output interpretation
  may be more speculative.

**6. DEAP articulation — LRP21 (`deappfi_gain`) / LRP22 (`deappfi`)**
- DEAP has three measures (`deappfi` fine, `deappin` intention,
  `deappvo` voicing); `deappfi` has the cleanest scale and
  importance rank on prior models.
- Distribution: left-skew (−0.87) with 5–95 percentage range.
  Ceiling effects possible at the top.
- `deappvo` (skew −2.14) and `deappin` (−0.65) can follow as
  LRP23/24 and LRP25/26 if there's value in covering the full
  articulation trio.

## Deferred (not in this plan)

- **Language sample measures (LSAM family)** — n=106 level,
  **n=53 gain** is too small for reliable tree-based modelling
  under 10-fold GroupKFold. Come back with a dedicated n ≥ 150
  cohort.
- **YARCSI** — floor-heavy (median 0, skew 1.57) and plausibly
  redundant with `ewrswr`. Low marginal information.
- **ERB measures (`erbword`, `erbnw`)** — valid targets but
  narrow specialised constructs. Lower value than grammar,
  decoding, blending.
- **SPPHON** — phonics measure, floor-heavy like nonword but
  with a much wider range. Could fold into a later phonological
  pair.

commit adds the two model files + docs templates + feature-
selection note + `__init__.py` import, runs Optuna 150 trials for

commits. Feature-selection cuts come in follow-up PRs.
